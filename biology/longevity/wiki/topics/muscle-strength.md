# Muscular strength

Independent of cardiorespiratory fitness, muscular strength predicts
all-cause mortality across age groups. Sarcopenia (age-related
muscle loss) is one of the most reversible aging phenotypes.

## Flagship evidence
- [[papers/leong-2015-pure-grip-strength]] — PURE study, n=139,691,
  17 countries. Grip strength predicted all-cause mortality more
  strongly than systolic BP.
- [[papers/garcia-hermoso-2018-strength-mortality]] — meta-analysis
  pooling ~2 million adults; both upper- and lower-body strength
  inversely associated with mortality.

## Interventional evidence
- [[papers/saeidifard-2019-resistance-mortality]] — meta-analysis:
  resistance training ~21% lower all-cause mortality;
  ~40% reduction when combined with aerobic exercise.

## Practical
- 2 full-body resistance sessions per week, progressive load on
  compound movements (squat, hinge, press, pull, carry).
- Grip strength is a cheap, reproducible biomarker (~$30
  dynamometer); test annually as a global aging signal.
