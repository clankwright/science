# Alcohol

The "moderate drinking is protective" narrative from older
observational studies has been substantially walked back; modern
analyses correcting for the "sick quitter" confound show a clear
dose-mortality relationship above ~7 standard drinks per week.

## Flagship evidence
- [[papers/wood-2018-alcohol-thresholds]] — IPD pooled analysis,
  n=599,912 current drinkers, 83 prospective studies. Lowest
  all-cause mortality at ~100 g/week ethanol (~7 standard drinks).
  No safe lower threshold for stroke or fatal hypertensive disease.

## Practical
- If you don't drink, don't start.
- If you do: ≤7 standard drinks per week (men or women) is the
  evidence-supported upper bound.
- The 2018 GBD "no safe level" framing is consistent with this for
  CVD subtypes other than non-fatal MI.
