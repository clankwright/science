# Smoking

The single largest accelerator of measurable biological aging in
modern cohorts. Effect size dwarfs any drug intervention available
to the public.

## Flagship evidence
- [[papers/jha-2013-smoking-mortality]] — modern US cohort
  (NHIS, n>200k). Current smokers have ~3x mortality vs
  never-smokers and lose >10 years of life expectancy. Quitting
  recovers most of that loss, the earlier the better.

## Practical
- Don't start. If you smoke: quit, the earlier the better.
- Combination pharmacotherapy (varenicline + NRT) plus behavioural
  support has the highest cessation rates.
- The mortality benefit of quitting starts within months and is
  largely complete within 10 years.

## Aging-clock effects
- Smoking accelerates DNA-methylation, inflammatory, and proteomic
  clocks measurably and reversibly. The
  [[papers/lifestyle-atlas-tirolgesund-2025]] cohort includes a
  smoking-cessation arm with multi-omic timecourse.
