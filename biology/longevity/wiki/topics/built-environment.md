# Built environment

The built-environment angle on longevity: how physical surroundings
(daylight, nature views, ambient noise, indoor air quality, room
temperature, circadian lighting) shape stress, sleep, and recovery
biology. Frequently asked about; mostly small-N or observational
evidence rather than mortality-grade RCT data.

## Foundational primary citations

- [[papers/ulrich-1984-window-view-recovery]] — *Science* 1984.
  Retrospective matched-pair, n=23. Tree-view rooms had ~0.74-day
  shorter cholecystectomy hospital stays vs brick-wall views.
  The most-cited single paper in the biophilic-design
  literature; small-N retrospective design.
- [[papers/marucha-1998-stress-wound-healing]] — within-subjects
  RCT-style, n=11 dental students. Wounds during exam week
  healed 40% slower with 68% less IL-1β mRNA than wounds during
  vacation. Cleanest mechanism link from psychological stress to
  measurable healing impairment.

## Where built environment intersects established longevity
mechanisms

- **Sleep and circadian** ([[topics/sleep]],
  [[papers/cappuccio-2010-sleep-mortality]]) — light exposure and
  ambient noise are upstream of the sleep mechanisms with
  T7-grade mortality data.
- **Stress / inflammation** ([[topics/inflammation]]) — stress is
  a real upstream lever for chronic inflammation. The Marucha
  result demonstrates a specific cytokine pathway, not a generic
  "stress is bad" claim.
- **Recovery from acute illness / surgery** — the Ulrich result
  is in the surgical-recovery domain, not chronic disease
  prevention. Useful for hospital-design decisions, less so for
  prospective longevity planning.

## Honest limits

- Most of the biophilic-design / "nature exposure" / circadian-
  lighting literature is observational, small-sample, or lab-
  surrogate. RCT-grade mortality outcomes do not exist.
- Effect sizes are modest. The high-leverage longevity
  interventions ([[topics/cardiorespiratory-fitness]],
  [[topics/muscle-strength]],
  [[topics/cardiovascular-prevention]]) move 10-yr mortality more
  than any built-environment intervention has been shown to.
- Reddit commenter `tiohlongm` flagged this as a missing dimension
  in the longevity wiki. The wiki position: cite the strongest
  primary sources (Ulrich, Marucha) for context, but do not
  promote built-environment interventions to the same tier as
  fitness, sleep, or BP control.
