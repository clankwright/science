# Cardiovascular prevention

Standard medical optimization of blood pressure and lipids is among
the highest-leverage longevity interventions available, and
systematically underprescribed in primary prevention.

## Blood pressure
- [[papers/sprint-2015-intensive-bp]] — SPRINT trial, n=9,361.
  Intensive SBP target <120 vs <140 cut all-cause mortality 27%
  in non-diabetic CV-risk adults. Trial halted early for benefit.

## Lipids: which marker?
- [[papers/sniderman-2011-apob-meta]] — apoB beats LDL-C by ~12%
  on relative-risk-reduction prediction; non-HDL-C is intermediate.
  apoB measurement is now the recommended marker per 2019 ESC/EAS
  guidelines.

## Lipids: the intervention works
- [[papers/ctt-2012-statins-low-risk]] — Cholesterol Treatment
  Trialists IPD meta-analysis of 27 RCTs. Each 1 mmol/L
  (~39 mg/dL) LDL-C reduction yields ~21% lower vascular events
  per year, including in low-risk primary-prevention adults.

## Practical
- Home BP cuff > clinic measurement; sustained <120/80 if at CV
  risk, <130/80 otherwise.
- Add **apoB** to standard lipid panels at routine bloodwork
  (~$10-20 added cost).
- apoB targets: <80 mg/dL (primary prevention), <60 mg/dL
  (high-risk / secondary).
- Generic statins (atorvastatin, rosuvastatin) are first-line
  for elevated apoB / LDL-C.
