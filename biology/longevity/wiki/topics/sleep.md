# Sleep

Sleep duration follows a U-shaped relationship with mortality. Short
sleep (<6 hr) and long sleep (>9 hr) both elevate risk vs the
~7-8 hr reference, with short-sleep effects more clearly causal.
The brain-aging-dominates-healthspan finding
([[papers/plasma-proteomics-brain-immune-2025]]) makes sleep
particularly load-bearing.

## Flagship evidence
- [[papers/cappuccio-2010-sleep-mortality]] — meta-analysis of 16
  prospective studies, ~1.4M adults. Short sleep (<6 hr) RR 1.12;
  long sleep (>9 hr) RR 1.30 vs 7-8 hr.

## Practical
- Target: 7-9 hours per night.
- Fixed wake time is the highest-leverage lever (regularity may
  matter more than duration).
- Treat treatable causes of poor sleep: sleep apnea, restless legs,
  alcohol-induced fragmentation, evening blue light.

## Sleep apnea: treat for symptoms, not for CV prevention
- [[papers/save-2016-cpap-cv-prevention]] — SAVE RCT, n=2,687
  with moderate-severe OSA + prior CVD: CPAP did NOT reduce
  hard CV endpoints (HR 1.10, p=0.34). CPAP is highly effective
  for daytime sleepiness, snoring, and quality of life; the
  cardiovascular-prevention indication is not RCT-supported
  despite widespread clinical assumption. Weight loss is the
  highest-leverage upstream intervention for OSA itself.
