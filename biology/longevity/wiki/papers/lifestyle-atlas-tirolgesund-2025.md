# TirolGESUND: open multi-omic atlas of lifestyle-intervention effects

## Summary
Longitudinal lifestyle-intervention study from the TirolGESUND group.
**156 women aged 30–60**, **6 months** of either **intermittent fasting
(n=114)** or **smoking cessation (n=42)**, **4 timepoints**, **7
tissue types**. **3,450+ biospecimens** with **DNA methylation,
metabolomics, microbiome, immune profiling, and skin histology**.
**High-compliance fasters** show **attenuated or age-opposing molecular
trajectories within 6 months**.

## Why it matters
Largest open multi-omic longitudinal lifestyle-intervention atlas.
Lets a CS contributor evaluate **how lifestyle interventions move
molecular aging readouts on a real human cohort**, with both control
and intervention arms across multiple tissues and timepoints.
Counterpart on the cohort side to [[papers/x-atlas-orion-perturbseq-2025]]
on the perturbation side.

## Key findings
- 156 women, 30–60 yo; biweekly TPE-equivalent intensity for fasting
  arm.
- 4 timepoints, 7 tissues, 3,450+ specimens.
- DNA methylation, metabolomics, microbiome, immune phenotyping,
  histology.
- High-compliance intermittent-fasting subset shows **attenuated or
  age-opposing molecular trajectories**.
- Smoking-cessation arm: more limited but real molecular reversal.
- Public interactive portal.
