# Trametinib + rapamycin: additive ~30% mouse lifespan extension

## Summary
Partridge / Grönke lab (Cologne, Cell Metabolism, Cologne Excellence
Cluster). **Trametinib** (FDA-approved MEK inhibitor) extends mouse
lifespan **+7–10%** on its own. Combined with **rapamycin** (mTOR
inhibitor), the effects are **additive**: **~29% (female) / 27% (male)
median lifespan extension**, with reduced liver and spleen tumours,
reduced brain glucose hypermetabolism, and multi-tissue inflammation
drops. Both drugs are FDA-approved for cancer, making the combo
repurposing-ready.

## Why it matters
First demonstration of **additive geroprotection from simultaneous
inhibition of Ras-MEK-ERK and mTORC1**. Rapamycin monotherapy is the
best-replicated longevity intervention ([[papers/itp-nia]],
[[papers/pearl-rapamycin-2025]]); this study raises the ceiling by
combining two signalling axes that both converge on age-related
pathologies. Strong candidate for ITP replication and, given the FDA
status of both, for human trials under repurposing frameworks.

## Key findings
- Trametinib monotherapy: +7–10% median lifespan.
- Trametinib + rapamycin: ~29% (F) / 27% (M) — additive, not redundant.
- Reduced liver and spleen tumour burden; reduced brain glucose
  hypermetabolism.
- Multi-tissue anti-inflammatory signature.
- Tolerability comparable to rapamycin alone.
