# In-vivo reprogramming, organ by organ

## Summary
Review (Jo, Lee, Eom, Kim, Cha) of **transient OCT4/SOX2/KLF4/c-MYC
expression for in vivo reprogramming**, organised by target organ:
**retina, muscle, heart, liver, brain, intestine**. Synthesises evidence
that partial reprogramming can **restore regenerative capacity and
promote tissue rejuvenation** in each organ. Connects injury-induced
dedifferentiation to reprogramming-mediated regeneration. Surveys safety
strategies (cyclic induction, organ-specific promoters) developed to
mitigate teratoma risk.

## Why it matters
Best up-to-date organ-by-organ map of where in vivo reprogramming
works, where it fails, and which delivery strategies have been tried.
Useful as a reading anchor before [[analysis/promising-reverse-aging]]
or before designing a reprogramming experiment.

## Key topics covered
- Retina: AAV-OSK restoration of vision (Lu lab and follow-up work).
- Muscle / heart: regenerative-capacity rescue without full
  dedifferentiation.
- Liver and intestine: where off-target induction concentrates and how
  tissue-specific Cre drivers help.
- Brain: cautious progress; partial reprogramming with stricter
  windows.
- Cyclic induction protocols (e.g. 2 ON / 5 OFF) shown safe for up to
  35 cycles in some strains.
