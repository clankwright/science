# IL-11 inhibition extends mouse healthspan and lifespan

## Summary
Genetic deletion of the pro-inflammatory cytokine **IL-11** extended
median lifespan of mice **by 24.9%**. Treating 75-week-old mice with
**anti-IL-11 antibody** until natural death extended median lifespan
**+22.5% (males), +25% (females)**, on top of broad healthspan gains.
The anti-IL-11 antibody is already in human trials for fibrotic lung
disease, putting this on the shortest path from mouse-lifespan hit to
human readout.

## Mechanism
IL-11 (IL-6 family) is upregulated in aged tissues and signals through
an **ERK to AMPK to mTORC1** axis to drive cellular, tissue, and
organism-level aging pathologies, including fibrosis, chronic
inflammation, and metabolic decline.

## Key findings
- 24.9% median lifespan extension from genetic IL-11 deletion.
- 22.5% (M) / 25% (F) lifespan extension from anti-IL-11 antibody
  starting at 75 weeks (late-life intervention).
- Reduced cancer-related mortality plus improved metabolism, muscle
  function, and frailty score across sexes.
- Effects attributed to dampening an ERK to AMPK to mTORC1 axis that
  drives age-related fibrosis and inflammation.

## Why it matters
One of the largest late-life lifespan extensions ever reported in mice
**with a clinically advanced antibody already in trials**. Sets a high
bar for any new geroprotective intervention.
