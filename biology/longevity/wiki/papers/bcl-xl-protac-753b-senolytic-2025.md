# 753b: a tissue-tropic PROTAC senolytic that avoids navitoclax thrombocytopenia

## Summary
**753b** is a dual **BCL-xL / BCL-2 PROTAC** that recruits the **VHL E3
ligase** for targeted degradation. Liver-tropic by design: selectively
eliminates **senescent hepatocytes** in aged and **MASH-driven HCC**
mice. **Avoids the severe thrombocytopenia** that limited
**navitoclax / ABT-263** as a senolytic. Effective even **after
fibrosis is established**.

## Why it matters
Solves the central limitation of small-molecule BH3-mimetic senolytics
(platelet toxicity). Adds a third major senolytic modality to the
field's toolkit alongside **small-molecule** (Smer-Barreto-style ML
discovery, [[papers/smerbarreto2023-senolytics]]) and **CAR-T**
([[papers/senolytic-cart-upar-2024]]). Hepatocyte tropism is the right
bias for MASH/MASLD-driven aging, where senescent hepatocytes are a
major driver of fibrotic and oncogenic progression.

## Key findings
- 753b: dual BCL-xL/BCL-2 PROTAC, VHL-recruiting.
- Liver-tropic: selectively clears senescent hepatocytes.
- Reduces NAFLD progression and HCC in STAM mice.
- Avoids navitoclax-class thrombocytopenia.
- Effective post-fibrosis, not just preventive.

## Caveats
- Liver tropism is by physico-chemical bias, not active targeting;
  generalising to other tissues will need new analogues.
- Long-term tumour and immune-system safety still TBD.
