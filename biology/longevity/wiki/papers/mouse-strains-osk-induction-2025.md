# Off-target OSKM in reprogrammable mouse strains

## Summary
Comparative analysis (Picó and colleagues, Lausanne / ETH Zurich) of
**multiple reprogrammable mouse strains** under doxycycline-inducible
**Oct4/Sox2/Klf4/c-Myc (OSKM)** systems. Across strains, OSKM
expression is **highly heterogeneous** between tissues, with the
**liver, intestine, and kidneys** receiving the most reprogramming
signal — the same organs that show toxicity in long cyclic-OSKM and
chemical-cocktail experiments. Specifically, the **4Fj and 4Fk**
strains show the strongest off-target induction.

## Why it matters
Provides a mechanistic basis for why "whole-body OSKM" is unsafe and why
modern programs (Retro Bio's [[papers/retro-precision-reprog-2025]],
[[papers/lipid-droplets-reprog-2025]]) restrict expression to specific
cell populations or reduce cyclic-induction frequency. Anyone choosing a
reprogrammable strain for an aging study should consult this paper
first.

## Key findings
- OSKM expression varies sharply between tissues and between
  reprogrammable strains.
- Doxycycline-rich tissues (liver, intestine, kidney) take the largest
  off-target dose, matching prior toxicity data.
- 4Fj and 4Fk strains show the highest leak; alternative strains
  reduce off-target induction.
- Suggests safer strategies: avoid OSKM expression in specific organs
  via promoter choice or chimeric strain engineering.
