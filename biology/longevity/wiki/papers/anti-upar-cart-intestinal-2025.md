# Anti-uPAR CAR T cells extend senolytic CAR-T into gut aging

## Summary
Amor lab follow-up to [[papers/senolytic-cart-upar-2024]]. **uPAR+
senescent epithelial cells** accumulate in the **aged gut** and impair
intestinal stem-cell function. Anti-uPAR CAR-T treatment **restores
barrier function, regenerative capacity, and microbiome composition**.
Both **prophylactic and therapeutic** dosing effective; benefits persist
**up to 1 year** from a single dose. Also **protected the intestine from
radiation damage**. Preliminary human intestinal-cell data positive.

## Why it matters
Generalises senolytic CAR-T from metabolic / glucose-tolerance endpoints
to a **stem-cell / regenerative** endpoint in a tissue that drives
systemic aging via the gut-immune-microbiome axis. Strengthens the case
for CAR-T as a durable senolytic platform.

## Key findings
- uPAR+ senescent cells accumulate in aged intestinal epithelium and
  degrade ISC function.
- Single anti-uPAR CAR-T dose: barrier function restored, microbiome
  shifted toward youthful composition.
- Year-long persistence; protective in both prevention and therapy
  arms.
- Radioprotective: shielded intestine from radiation damage.
- Effective in human intestinal cells (preclinical pilot).
