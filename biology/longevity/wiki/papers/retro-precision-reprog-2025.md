# Cdkn2a-targeted AAV-OSK from Retro Biosciences

## Summary
First peer-reviewed paper from **Retro Biosciences** (June 2025).
Uses AAV-delivered Oct4/Sox2/Klf4 (OSK) under a **Cdkn2a-promoter
cassette** so that reprogramming factor expression is restricted to
**senescent / aged cells** (Cdkn2a/p16 positive). In aged mice, a single
AAV injection improved lifespan, reduced inflammation, restored tissue
integrity, and accelerated wound healing — all without the
reprogramming-induced toxicity that hits whole-body OSK delivery.

## Why it matters
Operationalises the "reprogram only the cells that need it" idea that
the field has discussed since the chemical-cocktail and cyclic-OSKM
work. Pairs naturally with [[papers/lipid-droplets-reprog-2025]]
(toxicity in liver/intestine when reprogramming is non-targeted) and
[[papers/mouse-strains-osk-induction-2025]] (showing the same off-target
problem in different reprogrammable strains). Retro also separately
applied an OpenAI-collaboration protein-engineering model to design
**OSK-variant transcription factors** with markedly higher reprogramming
efficiency and lower DNA-damage signature (reported alongside).

## Key findings
- AAV with Cdkn2a-promoter OSK cassette: targets senescent / aged cells
  only.
- Single dose in aged mice: improved lifespan, reduced systemic
  inflammation, restored tissue integrity, faster wound healing.
- No detectable on-target toxicity in the cell populations OSK was
  restricted from.
- Companion engineering work: AI-redesigned Yamanaka-factor variants
  achieve >50x stem-cell-marker induction and reduced DNA damage vs.
  wild-type.
