# Senolytic CAR T cells targeting uPAR — single dose, year-long effect

## Summary
Engineered T cells with a CAR targeting **uPAR (urokinase plasminogen
activator receptor)**, a senescence-associated cell-surface antigen.
**uPAR rises with age** across liver, adipose, pancreas, and muscle. A
single CAR-T infusion in mice **persisted and expanded for >12 months**,
continuously eliminating senescent cells. Improved glucose tolerance and
exercise capacity in aged mice; **prevented metabolic dysfunction** in
young mice fed a high-fat diet (prophylactic use).

## Why it matters
First demonstration that **CAR-T senolysis is durable** ("living
drugs"): a one-shot intervention that keeps clearing senescent cells for
a year or more, sidestepping the dosing-frequency problem of
small-molecule senolytics ([[papers/smerbarreto2023-senolytics]],
[[papers/senolytic-mci-ebiomed-2025]]). Establishes the platform now
extended to gut aging in [[papers/anti-upar-cart-intestinal-2025]].

## Key findings
- uPAR expression rises with chronological age in multiple tissues.
- Single CAR-T dose persists and expands for **>12 months**.
- Improved glucose tolerance and exercise capacity in aged mice
  (therapeutic).
- Prevented HFD-induced metabolic dysfunction in young mice
  (prophylactic).
- Selective for senescent populations; minimal off-target damage in
  the windows assayed.

## Caveats
- CAR-T manufacturing and infusion are far costlier than a small
  molecule; clinical translation will need outcomes that justify it.
- uPAR is also expressed on some normal myeloid cells; long-term
  selectivity needs continued safety follow-up.
