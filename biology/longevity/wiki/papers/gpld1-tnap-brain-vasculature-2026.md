# GPLD1 → TNAP: liver exerkine rejuvenates the brain through the vasculature

## Summary
Villeda lab follow-up to the **GPLD1 (2020 Cell)** paper. **GPLD1**, a
liver-derived **exercise-induced enzyme**, restores cognitive function
in aging and Alzheimer's mouse models by **cleaving GPI-anchored
TNAP** on **brain endothelial cells**. This restores **blood-brain
barrier integrity** and rescues memory. **TNAP inhibition alone** also
rescues cognition — making TNAP a **druggable single-target axis** for
brain rejuvenation downstream of exercise.

## Why it matters
Identifies the **mechanistic substrate** that the 2020 GPLD1 paper
predicted: **TNAP** on brain endothelium. Provides a **drug target**
that bypasses the need to deliver an enzyme. Adds a vascular axis to
the brain-rejuvenation toolkit alongside [[papers/fgf17-young-csf-2022]]
(parenchymal) and [[papers/klotho-skl-aav-2025]] (neurogenic /
microglial).

## Key findings
- GPLD1 (liver-derived) increases with exercise and rescues cognition
  in aged mice when delivered systemically.
- GPLD1 cleaves **TNAP** off brain endothelial cells.
- BBB function restored; memory restored in aged and **5xFAD** AD mice.
- **TNAP inhibition** (independent of GPLD1 delivery) reproduces the
  cognitive rescue and reduces Aβ pathology.
- Defines an **exercise → liver → BBB → cognition** axis with a single
  druggable node.

## Computational leverage
- TNAP is a well-characterised drug target with existing inhibitors
  (e.g. SBI-425); a CS contributor could prioritise repurposing
  candidates against it via [[papers/network-repurposing-aging]] or
