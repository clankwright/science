# Pulsed Electromagnetic Fields (PEMF)

> **Summary:** PEMF devices deliver low-frequency electromagnetic pulses to tissue. FDA-cleared devices exist (Class II). Evidence base is real but frequently overclaimed. The NASA in vitro result (2.5-4x neural regeneration) is preclinical only and cannot be directly extrapolated to clinical outcomes.

## Regulatory Status

| Device | Regulatory | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| BEMER | CE + FDA | Cleared for local increase of blood circulation |
| Orthocor | FDA Class II | |

## Evidence

**Tier 1:**
- 2025 RCT (N=91): 36% pain reduction vs control for musculoskeletal pain.
- Multiple orthopedic RCTs for bone healing (FDA cleared that indication in 1979).

**Tier 2 (NASA preclinical — frequently overcited):** [NASA PEMF 2003](../papers/nasa-pemf-2003.md) (Goodwin) found 2.5-4x increase in neural tissue regeneration in vitro at 10 Hz square wave. This is a cell culture study, not a clinical trial. Bone gap closure in that paper is also in vitro. The finding supports mechanism plausibility, not clinical efficacy claims.

**Correct language for citation:** "Preclinical evidence from NASA showing cellular-level regeneration response to PEMF at 10 Hz, 1-20 µT (Goodwin 2003). Clinical translation requires separate RCT evidence."

## Mechanism

Lorentz forces on moving charged particles in tissue → increased ion channel activity → cell membrane polarization changes → downstream effects on proliferation, differentiation, inflammation. Mechanism biologically plausible; exact pathway still debated.

## Limitations

- Enormous parameter space (frequency, intensity, waveform, duration) — most studies use different parameters, making systematic synthesis difficult
- Effect sizes modest in clinical trials vs dramatic preclinical results
- Publication bias likely
