# Biophilic Design and Healing

> **Summary:** Incorporating nature — views, plants, daylight, natural materials — into built environments measurably reduces hospital stay duration, analgesic use, pain, blood pressure, and mortality. Effect sizes are large enough to show in matched cohort studies. The field is called Evidence-Based Design (EBD).

## Tier 1 Evidence

**Nature views and hospital outcomes.** [Ulrich 1984](../papers/ulrich-1984.md) showed that a tree view vs a brick wall reduced hospital stay by 8.5% and analgesic use significantly (N=46 matched pairs, Surgery). A 2025 meta-analysis of 28 studies ([Gaston 2025](../papers/gaston-2025.md)) replicated and extended this finding.

**Plants in hospital rooms.** [Park & Mattson 2008](../papers/park-mattson-2008.md) (N=90 RCT) found that plants in a recovery room reduced blood pressure, heart rate, pain ratings, and anxiety compared to no-plant control.

**Forest bathing and cortisol.** [Li 2009](../papers/li-2009.md) measured cortisol across 24 Japanese forests. Forest environment consistently produced -13-16% cortisol reduction versus urban control, with increased NK cell activity.

## Tier 2 Evidence

**Biophilic design systematic review.** [Frontiers 2024](../papers/frontiers-biophilic-2024.md) PRISMA review found biophilic elements → reduced hospitalization time, pain reports, and mortality across multiple facility types.

**Salutogenic architecture in elderly.** [Salutogenic Arch 2024](../papers/salutogenic-arch-2024.md) (26 papers) found biophilic and salutogenic design principles associated with higher sense of coherence in elderly populations.

**Horticultural therapy.** [Meta-analysis 2023](../papers/horticultural-therapy-2023.md) found horticultural therapy → improved quality of life, reduced anxiety and depression in elderly cohorts.

**WELL buildings vs LEED.** [Cell Reports 2024](../papers/cell-reports-well-2024.md) (3,268 surveys, matched) found WELL-certified buildings produced 39% higher occupant satisfaction than LEED-certified buildings, with health outcome improvements.

## Key Mechanisms

1. **Stress pathway**: nature views reduce cortisol and sympathetic arousal → faster wound healing ([Kiecolt-Glaser 1995](../papers/kiecolt-glaser-1995.md)), shorter recovery.
2. **Attention restoration**: natural environments restore directed attention capacity (Kaplan & Kaplan theory, widely replicated).
3. **Physiological regulation**: plants and views reduce blood pressure and heart rate directly.
