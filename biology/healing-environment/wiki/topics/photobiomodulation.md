# Photobiomodulation (Low-Level Laser Therapy)

> **Summary:** Photobiomodulation (PBM), also called low-level laser therapy (LLLT), uses red and near-infrared light (600–1100 nm) to stimulate cellular repair via cytochrome c oxidase in the mitochondrial electron transport chain. It has the largest clinical evidence base of any energy-based therapeutic modality — multiple Cochrane reviews, 22 FDA clearances (Erchonia alone), CE marking, TGA, and NATO approval for specific devices.

## Regulatory Status

| Manufacturer | Clearances | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Erchonia | 22 FDA 510(k) clearances + CE | Broadest cleared indication range |
| THOR Photomedicine | FDA + CE + TGA + NATO | Used in military medicine |

These are cleared for specific indications within their intended use. Off-label use is common in research but distinct from cleared use.

## Mechanism

Cytochrome c oxidase (complex IV of mitochondrial electron transport chain) absorbs red/NIR photons → increased ATP production → reduced reactive oxygen species → cellular repair signaling. Established at molecular level; not disputed.

## Evidence

**Tier 1 (multiple Cochrane reviews):** PBM has Cochrane-reviewed evidence for:
- Neck pain (Chow 2009 Cochrane)
- Wound healing
- Musculoskeletal pain

Cite Cochrane reviews directly, not commercial manufacturer data. The "1,700+ RCTs" figure cited in industry sources is plausible but unverifiable as a single count; Cochrane-level evidence exists for specific indications.

**Tier 2:** Systematic analyses across pain management, wound healing, neuropathy. Evidence strongest for short-term pain reduction and superficial wound healing.

## Key Parameters (from research)

- Wavelength: 600–700 nm (red) and 780–1100 nm (NIR)
- Power density: 1–500 mW/cm²
- Dose: 0.1–10 J/cm²
- Pulse vs continuous: both used, optimal parameters indication-dependent

## Limitations

- Parameter standardization across studies is poor — makes meta-analysis difficult
- Penetration depth limits deep-tissue applications
- Optimal protocols not established for most conditions
