# Acoustic Environment in Healing Spaces

> **Summary:** Hospital noise levels routinely exceed WHO guidelines by >20 dB(A), causing sleep fragmentation, elevated stress hormones, and impaired recovery. Acoustic design is a high-impact, low-cost intervention.

## Tier 2 Evidence

**Hospital noise levels.** [PMC 2020](../papers/hospital-noise-2020.md): systematic review. Hospital noise routinely exceeds WHO 45 dB(A) daytime recommendation by 20+ dB(A). Nighttime noise frequently above WHO 35 dB(A) nighttime target by similar margins.

**Mechanisms documented:** noise → sympathetic arousal → elevated cortisol and adrenaline → impaired sleep architecture → slowed healing (via [stress-healing](stress-healing.md) pathway).

## WHO Guidelines (for reference)

- Daytime: 45 dB(A) maximum in hospital rooms
- Nighttime: 35 dB(A) maximum
- Reality: most wards measure 65-80 dB(A) peaks routinely

## Interventions with Evidence

1. **Acoustic ceiling tiles**: NRC (noise reduction coefficient) ≥0.75. Reduces reverb and reflected noise.
2. **Single-patient rooms**: removes neighbor noise; strongest single intervention.
3. **Staff communication systems**: replace overhead PA with pager/wireless → eliminate most spike events.
4. **Sound masking**: low-level broadband pink/white noise can mask intermittent spikes.
5. **Noise monitoring with feedback**: real-time displays showing dB levels change staff behavior.
