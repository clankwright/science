# Ekholm 2020 — Weighted Blankets RCT

**Authors:** Ekholm et al.  
**Journal:** Journal of Clinical Sleep Medicine (JCSM)  
**URL:** https://jcsm.aasm.org/doi/10.5664/jcsm.8192

## Summary

Large RCT demonstrating weighted blankets as effective treatment for insomnia, with unusually large effect size (Cohen's d = 1.90) and 12-month follow-up confirming durability.

## Key Findings

- **N=120** randomized placebo-controlled trial
- **Cohen's d = 1.90** — large effect (>0.8 is large, >1.2 is very large)
- 12-month follow-up: benefit sustained
- Weighted blanket condition: significantly reduced insomnia severity, improved sleep quality
- Secondary: reduced anxiety and depression scores

## Why This Matters for Healing Environments

Weighted blankets are:
- **Not a medical device** — no regulatory burden, no CE/FDA required
- **Zero friction for data collection** — sleep improvement measurable via any wearable (Oura, Withings)
- **Low cost** (~€30-80)
- **Evidence strength** exceptional for the cost: d=1.90 is larger than most pharmaceutical sleep interventions

A 2024 meta-analysis (pooled p=0.06, borderline; sensitivity analysis p=0.001) confirms the pattern holds across studies.
