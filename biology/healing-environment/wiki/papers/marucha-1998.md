# Marucha, Kiecolt-Glaser & Favagehi 1998 — Exam Stress and Wound Healing

**Authors:** Phillip T. Marucha, Janice K. Kiecolt-Glaser, Mehrdad Favagehi  
**Journal:** Psychosomatic Medicine, 60(3):362-365  
**DOI:** 10.1097/00006842-199805000-00025  
**PMID:** 9625226  
**URL:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9625226/

## Summary

Dental students' standardized punch wounds took 40% longer to heal during exam period than during summer vacation. Crossover design (same subjects, two time points) controls for individual variation — the stress exposure is the only variable.

## Key Findings

- **Design:** crossover — same dental students measured during exam period and summer vacation
- Wounds healed **40% longer** during examinations than during summer
- Reduced IL-1β production at wound site during stress (matching [Kiecolt-Glaser 1995](kiecolt-glaser-1995.md))
- "Even something as transient, predictable, and relatively benign as examination stress can have significant consequences for wound healing" (from abstract)

## Why This Design Is Compelling

Crossover design eliminates between-subject confounders entirely — each person is their own control. The only variable is the time of year (exam vs no-exam). This is why the "40% longer" figure has high internal validity despite the small sample typical of wound healing studies.
