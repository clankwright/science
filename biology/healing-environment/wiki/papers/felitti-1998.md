# Felitti 1998 — ACE Study

**Authors:** Vincent J. Felitti, Robert F. Anda, et al. (CDC collaboration)  
**Journal:** American Journal of Preventive Medicine, Vol. 14(4), pp. 245-258  
**DOI:** 10.1016/S0749-3797(98)00017-8  
**URL:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9635069/

## Summary

The foundational study linking Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs) to adult disease and mortality. N=9,508 Kaiser Permanente members. Dose-response relationship: more ACE categories → higher risk of virtually every major cause of adult death.

## Key Findings

- **N=9,508** adult Kaiser Permanente members, retrospective survey + medical records
- 7 ACE categories measured: abuse (emotional, physical, sexual), household dysfunction (domestic violence, substance abuse, mental illness, criminal household member)
- Dose-response: each additional ACE category increases risk of ischemic heart disease, cancer, stroke, COPD, liver disease, and others
- 4+ ACEs vs 0 ACEs: ischemic heart disease ~2.2x, stroke ~2.4x, COPD ~3.9x, liver disease ~4.4x
- Smoking, obesity, and substance use partially mediate the association (ACE → coping behaviors → disease) but biological pathway also direct

## The Allostatic Load Model

ACEs → chronic stress activation of HPA and sympathetic nervous system → allostatic load → accelerated cellular aging and immune dysregulation → earlier and more severe disease onset. This is the mechanistic bridge to the [healing environment](../topics/biophilic-design.md) literature: if you can reduce allostatic load through environment, you slow the disease trajectory.

## Notes

- The "20-year lifespan reduction" figure sometimes associated with this study comes from later follow-up analyses, not the original 1998 paper — cite those separately if needed.
- The ACE questionnaire (10-item) is now widely used as a clinical screening tool.
