# Ulrich 1984 — View Through a Window May Influence Recovery from Surgery

**Authors:** Roger S. Ulrich  
**Journal:** Science, Vol. 224, pp. 420-421  
**DOI:** 10.1126/science.6143402  
**URL:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/6143402/

## Summary

Patients recovering from cholecystectomy (gallbladder surgery) assigned to rooms with a tree view vs brick wall view had meaningfully better outcomes. This is the founding study of evidence-based healthcare design.

## Key Findings

- **N=23 matched pairs** (46 total patients; same room, consecutive assignment, matched for age, sex, weight, smoking, year)
- Tree-view patients: **8.5% shorter hospital stay** (7.96 vs 8.70 days)
- Tree-view patients: fewer negative nursing notes ("patient upset", "needs much encouragement")
- Tree-view patients: **fewer analgesics** (fewer strong doses, more weak doses)
- Tree-view patients: lower post-surgery complications score

## Methodology Notes

Strong matched-pairs design controls for confounders. Same room, same nurses, same surgical team — only the window view differed. Effect is conservative (both groups had some daylight, just different view content).

## Significance

Launched the field of evidence-based healthcare design. Replicated and extended by [Gaston 2025](gaston-2025.md) meta-analysis of 28 studies.
