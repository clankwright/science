# Goodwin 2003 — NASA PEMF Neural Tissue Regeneration

**Author:** Thomas J. Goodwin, Ph.D.  
**Report:** NASA/TP-2003-212054, 4-year study  
**URL:** https://ntrs.nasa.gov/api/citations/20030075722/downloads/20030075722.pdf

## Summary

In vitro study (nerve cell cultures in petri dishes) showing 2.5-4x increase in neural tissue regeneration under PEMF at specific parameters. Frequently cited but also frequently overclaimed — the results are preclinical only.

## Key Findings

- **In vitro** (nerve cell cultures, not animal or human subjects)
- 10 Hz square wave PEMF at 1-20 µT
- 2.5-4x increase in neural tissue regeneration rate vs control cultures
- Bone gap closure findings are also in cell culture

## Critical Context

This is a cell culture study. Extrapolation to clinical human outcomes requires:
1. In vivo animal studies confirming the effect survives in living tissue
2. Human RCTs with equivalent parameters
3. Confirmation that therapeutic devices reproduce equivalent field parameters at tissue depth

The finding supports mechanism plausibility for PEMF in tissue regeneration. It does not demonstrate clinical efficacy.

**Correct citation language:** "Preclinical evidence from NASA showing cellular-level regeneration response to PEMF at 10 Hz, 1-20 µT (Goodwin 2003). Clinical translation requires RCT evidence."

**Do not use:** "NASA confirmed PEMF accelerates healing" — the study does not support this claim for clinical settings.
