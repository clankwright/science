# Kiecolt-Glaser 1995 — Wound Healing Impaired by Caregiver Stress

**Authors:** Janice K. Kiecolt-Glaser, Phillip T. Marucha, William B. Malarkey, Ana M. Mercado, Ronald Glaser  
**Journal:** The Lancet, Vol. 346, pp. 1194-1196  
**DOI:** 10.1016/S0140-6736(95)92899-5  
**PMID:** 7475659  
**URL:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/7475659/

## Summary

Women caring for relatives with Alzheimer's disease showed impaired wound healing compared to matched controls, demonstrating that chronic psychological stress (caregiver burden) directly slows biological healing via immune pathways.

## Key Findings

- **N=13** Alzheimer's caregivers + **13 matched controls** (case-control study)
- Wound healing was measurably slower in caregivers than controls
- Mechanism: reduced IL-1β mRNA expression at wound site — cytokine suppression
- Confirms the psychoneuroimmune pathway from chronic stress → immune suppression → impaired healing

## Important: Not the Same as the "40% Slower" Study

The "wounds heal 40% longer under examination stress" finding comes from a related but distinct paper:
**Marucha, Kiecolt-Glaser & Favagehi 1998** (Psychosomatic Medicine, PMID 9625226) — dental students, crossover design, exam period vs summer vacation.

Both papers establish the same mechanism (stress impairs wound healing), but with different populations and study designs:
- 1995 Lancet: chronic caregiver stress, case-control, N=13 vs 13
- 1998 Psychosom Med: acute exam stress, crossover (same subjects), dental students, 40% longer healing
