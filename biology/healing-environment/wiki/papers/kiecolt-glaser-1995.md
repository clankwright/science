# Kiecolt-Glaser 1995 — Slowing of Wound Healing by Psychological Stress

**Authors:** Janice K. Kiecolt-Glaser, Phillip T. Marucha, William B. Malarkey, Ana M. Mercado, Ronald Glaser  
**Journal:** The Lancet, Vol. 346, pp. 1194-1196  
**DOI:** 10.1016/S0140-6736(95)91898-4  
**URL:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/7475659/

## Summary

Standardized punch biopsy wounds healed 40% slower in participants under chronic psychological stress versus matched controls. Direct demonstration of the psychoneuroimmune pathway from stress to healing speed.

## Key Findings

- **N=13** dental students: wounds made during summer vacation vs during exam period (same individuals, crossover)
- Wounds during exams healed in **average 40% longer** than during vacation
- Mechanism: lower IL-1β mRNA expression at wound site during stress — cytokine suppression
- No difference in nutritional status or sleep (controlled) — effect is psychological

## Why 40% Is Remarkable

40% slower healing means a 10-day recovery becomes a 14-day recovery. At hospital costs of €300-500/day, this has direct economic implications. More importantly, slower healing = longer infection exposure window.

## Methodology Notes

Crossover design (same participants, two time points) controls for individual variation. Standardized biopsy location (hard palate) and wound size eliminates wound variability. High internal validity despite small N.
