## Concepts

- [UFO/UAP Overview](concepts/ufo-uap-overview.md) — An unidentified flying object (UFO), more recently rebranded as an unidentified anomalous phenomenon (UAP), denotes any airborne or transmedium object whose provenance and characteristics elude immediate identification as conventional aircraft, atmospheric artifacts, sensor malfunctions, or prosaic human activities.
- [Ufology](concepts/ufology.md) — Ufology is the systematic study of unidentified flying objects (UFOs), encompassing the collection and analysis of reports concerning anomalous aerial phenomena that resist immediate identification. Government-led inquiries resolved approximately 94% of cases as misperceptions, while the remaining 5-6% persisted as unidentified.
- [Identification Studies of UFOs](concepts/identification-studies.md) — Identification studies of UFOs encompass the systematic methodologies used by governments, military agencies, and civilian researchers to investigate and resolve reports of unidentified aerial phenomena, from Project Blue Book through the modern AARO.
- [UFO Sightings in Outer Space](concepts/ufo-sightings-in-space.md) — Sporadic reports by astronauts and cosmonauts of unidentified objects encountered during crewed missions from the 1960s Gemini program onward. Official NASA analyses consistently attribute such sightings to prosaic explanations: jettisoned rocket stages, ice particles, thermal blankets, thruster exhaust, and sensor artifacts.
- [Black Triangle UFOs](concepts/black-triangles.md) — A category of unidentified aerial phenomena described as massive, equilateral triangular craft, typically black or matte dark, capable of silent hovering and sudden acceleration. Major clusters include the 1982-1986 Hudson Valley sightings, the 1989-1990 Belgian wave, and the 1997 Phoenix Lights.
- [Extraterrestrial Hypothesis (ETH)](concepts/extraterrestrial-hypothesis.md) — The extraterrestrial hypothesis proposes that some unidentified flying objects represent physical spacecraft operated by intelligent beings from beyond Earth. Official U.S. investigations from Project Blue Book through AARO have found no empirical evidence supporting extraterrestrial origins.
- [Interdimensional Hypothesis (IDH)](concepts/interdimensional-hypothesis.md) — The interdimensional UFO hypothesis posits that unidentified flying objects originate from coexisting dimensions or parallel realities rather than distant extraterrestrial locations. First advanced by Meade Layne in the late 1940s and developed through John Keel's "ultraterrestrial" model and Jacques Vallee's "control system" framework.

## Entities

- [Area 51](entities/area-51.md) — Officially designated as a remote detachment of Edwards Air Force Base at Groom Lake, Nevada, Area 51 is the U.S. military's premier classified flight-test facility. Established in 1955 for the U-2 spy plane program, its extreme secrecy inadvertently fueled decades of UFO mythology.
- [Bob Lazar](entities/bob-lazar.md) — Robert Scott Lazar gained prominence in 1989 by claiming he worked on reverse-engineering extraterrestrial spacecraft at "S-4," a purported facility adjacent to Area 51. Independent investigations have failed to verify his educational credentials from MIT and Caltech.
- [Papoose Lake](entities/papoose-lake.md) — A dry lake bed in Lincoln County, southern Nevada, approximately four miles south of the Groom Lake (Area 51) facility. The site gained prominence from Bob Lazar's 1989 claims of a concealed facility designated "S-4." Declassified satellite imagery consistently shows an undeveloped playa with no evidence of structures.
- [Jeremy Corbell](entities/jeremy-corbell.md) — Jeremy Kenyon Lockyer Corbell is an American investigative filmmaker and ufologist known for producing UAP documentaries and for sourcing and releasing authenticated U.S. military UAP footage, including the USS Russell "pyramid" video and USS Omaha "sphere" video.
- [Grey Alien](entities/grey-alien.md) — The Grey alien is the dominant extraterrestrial archetype in ufology and abduction narratives, typically described as a 3-to-4-foot humanoid with smooth grey skin, an enlarged hairless cranium, and large almond-shaped black eyes. No verifiable physical artifacts or biological samples confirm their existence.
- [Alleged Extraterrestrial Beings](entities/alleged-extraterrestrial-beings.md) — A catalog of purported non-human entity types reported in UFO close encounters, abduction narratives, and contactee accounts, featuring recurring archetypes including Greys, Nordics, Reptilians, Insectoids, and Tall Whites. No verifiable evidence confirms any of these entities' existence.
- [MUFON (Mutual UFO Network)](entities/mufon.md) — Founded in 1969, MUFON is the largest and most prominent civilian nonprofit organization dedicated to the scientific investigation of UFO/UAP sightings worldwide, operating a global network of trained field investigators across 50+ countries.
- [NUFORC (National UFO Reporting Center)](entities/nuforc.md) — Established in 1974 and based in Seattle, NUFORC functions as a centralized public database for UFO/UAP sighting reports in the United States, focusing on aggregation and transparency rather than investigation.
- [AARO (All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office)](entities/aaro.md) — A U.S. Department of Defense organization established in July 2022 to centralize investigations into unidentified anomalous phenomena across all domains. Its March 2024 Historical Record Report concluded there is no verifiable evidence of extraterrestrial technology or government cover-ups.
- [Luis Elizondo](entities/luis-elizondo.md) — Former intelligence officer who directed AATIP (2007-2012) and triggered the 2017 NYT UAP disclosure
- [NASA UAP Study](entities/nasa-uap-study.md) — NASA's 2023 Independent UAP Study Team; found no ET evidence, recommended standardized data collection
- [Galileo Project](entities/galileo-project.md) — Harvard-based systematic scientific search for UAP and interstellar objects, led by Avi Loeb
- [GEIPAN](entities/geipan.md) — French government unit (CNES) investigating UAP since 1977; classifies ~20% of cases as unidentified
- [J. Allen Hynek](entities/j-allen-hynek.md) — Astronomer and Project Blue Book consultant who created the Close Encounters classification system and founded CUFOS
- [Jacques Vallee](entities/jacques-vallee.md) — French-American researcher who pioneered the interdimensional hypothesis and control system framework for UAP
- [UFO Organizations: International](entities/ufo-organizations-international.md) — A survey of UFO/UAP research organizations operating outside the United States, spanning government-sanctioned programs (France's GEIPAN, Chile's CEFAA, Uruguay's CRIDOVNI), large civilian networks (BUFORA, UFO-Sverige, Kosmopoisk), and regional groups worldwide.

## Events

- [Storm Area 51](events/storm-area-51.md) — A viral Facebook event created in June 2019 that amassed over 2 million RSVPs calling for a mass incursion into Area 51 on September 20, 2019. Actual attendance was roughly 1,500 people, with no perimeter breaches. It remains a case study in the gap between internet virality and real-world mobilization.
- [UFO Report: ODNI Preliminary Assessment (2021)](events/ufo-report-2021.md) — The first unclassified U.S. intelligence community assessment of UAP, released June 25, 2021, analyzing 144 reports from U.S. military sources. Only one case was resolved; eighteen incidents featured anomalous behaviors including apparent near-instantaneous acceleration and hypersonic velocities without sonic booms.
- [Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program (AATIP)](events/aatip.md) — A Defense Intelligence Agency program established in 2007 with approximately $22 million to study anomalous aerospace phenomena. Its existence was revealed by a December 2017 New York Times article that also released three Navy videos, marking a paradigm shift that reframed UFOs from fringe speculation to national security imperatives.
- [Congressional Hearings on UAP (2023-2025)](events/congressional-hearings.md) — Between 2023 and 2025, the U.S. House Oversight Committee held a series of hearings on UAP. The most consequential was the July 2023 hearing featuring David Grusch, who testified under oath that the U.S. government operates a program to retrieve crashed UAP craft of non-human origin. AARO found no verifiable evidence to substantiate these claims.
- [Project Blue Book](events/project-blue-book.md) — The U.S. Air Force's systematic study of unidentified flying objects, operating from 1952 to 1969. It examined 12,618 UFO reports, deeming 701 cases (5.6%) unexplained. Its closure created a decades-long institutional vacuum not filled until AATIP (2007) and AARO (2022).
- [Roswell Incident (1947)](events/roswell.md) — The 1947 recovery of debris near Roswell, New Mexico, initially reported as a "flying disc" before being attributed to a weather balloon. A 1994 Air Force report confirmed the debris originated from Project Mogul, a classified high-altitude balloon program for detecting Soviet nuclear tests.
- [Belgian UFO Wave (1989-1990)](events/belgian-ufo-wave.md) — Widespread sightings of large, silent, triangular objects over Belgium, culminating in F-16 intercept attempts and SOBEPS investigation. The most widely circulated photograph was confirmed as a hoax in 2011 by its creator.
- [USS Nimitz Encounter (2004)](events/uss-nimitz-2004.md) — The most extensively documented military UAP case in the public record. F/A-18 pilots observed and pursued a white, Tic Tac-shaped object that demonstrated rapid acceleration and instantaneous direction changes. The encounter was corroborated by ship-based radar and captured on FLIR video.
- [Rendlesham Forest Incident (1980)](events/rendlesham-forest.md) — An incident in late December 1980 near RAF Woodbridge in Suffolk, England, when U.S. Air Force personnel reported encounters with anomalous lights and a craft-like object in the adjacent forest over multiple nights. Often called "Britain's Roswell."
- [Phoenix Lights (1997)](events/phoenix-lights.md) — Mass sighting of a V-shaped formation and subsequent flares over Phoenix, Arizona; one of the most witnessed UAP events in U.S. history
- [UFO-Related Hoaxes](events/ufo-hoaxes.md) — Deliberate fabrications of sightings, photographs, physical traces, or encounters purporting to demonstrate UFOs or extraterrestrial visitation, motivated by notoriety, financial gain, or psychological factors. Cataloged chronologically from the 1835 Great Moon Hoax through modern CGI and AI fabrications.

## Culture

- [UFOs in Fiction](culture/ufos-in-fiction.md) — Depictions of unidentified flying objects, extraterrestrial visitors, and alien encounters have pervaded literature, film, television, comics, and video games since the late 19th century, serving as a mirror for society's deepest anxieties about technology, sovereignty, identity, and the unknown.
- [Alien Invasion Trope](culture/alien-invasion-trope.md) — One of science fiction's most enduring narrative frameworks, originating with H.G. Wells' *The War of the Worlds* (1897-1898) and evolving through Cold War infiltration paranoia, blockbuster spectacles, and contemporary explorations of surveillance and ecological anxiety.
- [UFO Religions](culture/ufo-religions.md) — New religious movements that integrate beliefs in extraterrestrial intelligences with salvific or revelatory spiritual narratives. While numerically marginal, they have produced tragic outcomes, most notably the 1997 Heaven's Gate mass suicide.
- [Catalog of UFO Religions](culture/ufo-religions-list.md) — A catalog of notable UFO religious movements, from enduring organizations like the Aetherius Society and Raelism to defunct groups like Heaven's Gate and Chen Tao, as well as borderline classifications such as Scientology, the Nation of Islam, and Falun Gong.
- [Ancient Aliens (TV Series)](culture/ancient-aliens.md) — An American documentary television series exploring the pseudoscientific ancient astronauts hypothesis, airing 21 seasons and 279 episodes as of November 2025. Its principal contributor Giorgio A. Tsoukalos became one of the most recognizable faces in popular UFO culture.
