# Phoenix Lights

> **Summary:** The Phoenix Lights incident of March 13, 1997, involved two distinct events witnessed by thousands of people across Nevada and Arizona. The primary event, beginning around 7:55 p.m. MST, consisted of a massive, silent V-shaped formation of lights traversing the sky from northwest to southeast, described by witnesses as spanning up to a mile across and obscuring stars. A secondary event around 10:00 p.m. involved stationary lights that the U.S. Air Force attributed to LUU-2B/B illumination flares dropped during training exercises. The incident remains one of the most widely witnessed and debated UFO events in modern history, with estimates of 10,000 to 20,000 observers for the primary event.

## The Primary Event (7:55-8:40 p.m.)

On the evening of March 13, 1997, thousands of witnesses across Nevada and Arizona reported sightings of a large, V-shaped formation of lights traversing the night sky silently from northwest to southeast. The formation was first observed near Henderson, Nevada, around 7:55 p.m. MST and reached the Phoenix metropolitan area by approximately 8:15 to 8:40 p.m.

Witness descriptions were remarkably consistent:

- **Size:** Immense, estimated at spanning up to a mile or more across
- **Configuration:** Five to seven stationary lights in a rigid, boomerang or triangular pattern
- **Behavior:** Moved slowly or hovered without propulsion noise, reportedly obscuring stars behind it, lacking visible fuselage or blinking navigation lights
- **Speed:** Estimated at 100 to 300 mph with uniform velocity
- **Sound:** Silent throughout observation

Estimates of observers range from thousands to 10,000 to 20,000 people, including pilots, police officers, and civilians who captured video footage showing the lights' deliberate motion. The witnesses' diversity and numbers make this one of the largest mass UFO sightings on record.

## Governor Fife Symington's Account

Arizona Governor Fife Symington III, a former U.S. Air Force officer, observed the formation from his Phoenix residence and described it as a "large and luminous" craft of "enormous size" that departed at high velocity.

Symington's response evolved significantly over a decade:

- **June 18, 1997:** Held a press conference featuring an aide dressed in an alien costume to mock UFO claims, dismissing public concerns
- **2007:** Publicly reversed his position, affirming the sighting's authenticity, rejecting prosaic explanations, and stating it represented "something extraordinary that defies explanation" not matching any known aircraft or flares

## The Secondary Event (10:00 p.m.)

A separate event around 10:00 p.m. involved a linear arrangement of lights descending and extinguishing sequentially over the area south of Phoenix. The U.S. Air Force attributed these lights to LUU-2B/B illumination flares released by A-10 Thunderbolt II aircraft from the Maryland Air National Guard during Operation Snowbird training exercises over the Barry M. Goldwater Range.

The military's July 1997 statement identified the flares as slow-falling, long-burning illumination rounds dropped from aircraft at 10,000 to 20,000 feet altitude. Video evidence of the later lights is broadly consistent with this explanation.

## Military Explanations

The Air Force offered two explanations for the evening's events:

1. **V-formation:** Attributed to a formation of seven A-10 Thunderbolt II aircraft flying in close echelon, returning to Davis-Monthan Air Force Base from a training mission at the Barry M. Goldwater Range
2. **Stationary lights:** Attributed to illumination flares from Operation Snowbird training exercises involving over 250 aircraft

## Contested Evidence

Witnesses and independent analysts contend that the military explanations, particularly the flare attribution, do not account for the primary V-formation event. Key discrepancies noted include:

- The earlier formation's reported low altitude, uniform speed, and lack of descent or flicker are inconsistent with drifting pyrotechnics under prevailing winds
- Consistent inter-light spacing and trajectory were incompatible with independently falling flares
- No radar or sonic signatures were detected for the primary formation
- The formation was described as a single, rigid structure rather than separate aircraft in echelon
- The temporal separation between the two events (roughly two hours) suggests distinct phenomena

No formal federal investigation beyond the Air Force's initial assessment was conducted, despite calls from witnesses and Governor Symington for transparency.

## Connection to Black Triangle Sightings

The Phoenix Lights incident is frequently discussed in the context of [black triangle UFO sightings](../concepts/black-triangles.md), which constitute a distinct category of reports spanning decades. The V-shaped or triangular configuration, enormous reported size, silent operation, and slow or hovering flight are consistent with the black triangle pattern documented in events like the [Belgian UFO wave](../events/belgian-ufo-wave.md) of 1989-1990.

The incident also fits within the broader category of cases where the [extraterrestrial hypothesis](../concepts/extraterrestrial-hypothesis.md) has been applied but remains unsubstantiated; skeptical analyses attribute the primary event to misidentification of conventional aircraft in formation, while proponents cite the witness consistency and scale as evidence against prosaic explanations.
