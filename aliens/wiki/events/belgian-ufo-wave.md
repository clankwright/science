# Belgian UFO Wave (1989-1990)

> **Summary:** The Belgian UFO wave of 1989-1990 involved widespread sightings of large, silent, triangular objects over Belgium, culminating in F-16 intercept attempts and investigation by the Belgian Society for the Study of Space Phenomena (SOBEPS). The most widely circulated photograph, taken near Petit-Rechain in April 1990, was confirmed as a hoax in 2011 by its creator, Patrick Marechal, who fabricated it using a polystyrene model. The available sources provide limited detail on the wave itself; this article synthesizes what is present from the hoaxes source and flags the need for dedicated sourcing.

**Sources:** [[raw/grokipedia/List_of_UFO-related_hoaxes.md]]

## Overview

Beginning in November 1989, thousands of witnesses across Belgium reported sightings of large, silent, triangular craft with lights at each vertex and a central light. The wave continued through 1990, generating significant public and institutional attention.

## F-16 Intercepts

Belgian Air Force F-16 fighters were scrambled to intercept reported objects. Radar contacts were obtained, though the objects reportedly demonstrated capabilities exceeding known aircraft performance. The Belgian military took the sightings seriously enough to cooperate with civilian investigators, an unusual posture for a NATO military.

## SOBEPS Investigation

The Belgian Society for the Study of Space Phenomena (SOBEPS) conducted a systematic civilian investigation, collecting witness testimony and coordinating with military authorities. Their work represented one of the more rigorous civilian investigative efforts of the period.

## The Petit-Rechain Photo Hoax

The single most famous piece of physical evidence from the wave was a photograph taken near Petit-Rechain on April 4, 1990, showing a triangular object with lights. This image was widely reproduced in media and UFO literature as compelling evidence.

In July 2011, Patrick Marechal publicly confessed to fabricating the photograph using a painted polystyrene (styrofoam) panel with embedded lights, photographed against a dark background. The hoax had circulated for over 20 years as apparently authentic evidence, undermining the photographic record of the entire wave.

## Assessment

The Belgian wave is notable for the volume of witnesses, military engagement, and the subsequent confirmation that its most prominent evidence was fabricated. The sightings themselves remain a subject of debate: some researchers connect them to the broader [black triangle](../concepts/black-triangles.md) phenomenon, while skeptics point to conventional aircraft, atmospheric effects, and social contagion.
