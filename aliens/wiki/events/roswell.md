# Roswell Incident (1947)

> **Summary:** The Roswell incident refers to the 1947 recovery of debris near Roswell, New Mexico, initially reported by the Army Air Forces as a "flying disc" before being retracted and attributed to a weather balloon. A 1994 Air Force report confirmed the debris originated from Project Mogul, a classified high-altitude balloon program designed to detect Soviet nuclear tests. The incident became a cornerstone of UFO mythology in the 1970s-1980s, fueling claims of alien spacecraft recovery and government cover-ups, including the fabricated MJ-12 documents. The available sources provide limited detail on the incident itself; this article synthesizes what is present and flags areas requiring additional sourcing.

## The Incident

In 1947, debris was recovered near Roswell, New Mexico. Initial Army Air Forces communications described the recovery of a "flying disc," generating immediate press coverage. The statement was rapidly retracted, with officials attributing the debris to a weather balloon.

## Project Mogul Explanation

A 1994 U.S. Air Force report confirmed the debris was from a classified Project Mogul high-altitude balloon, designed for detecting Soviet nuclear test acoustics at altitude. The balloon trains used unconventional materials (including radar reflectors and balsa wood frames) that were unfamiliar to the rancher who discovered the debris, contributing to the initial misidentification.

## UFO Mythology

Conspiracy theories alleging alien spacecraft recovery originated in the late 1940s and gained significant traction in the 1970s through books and media claiming that:
- A crashed alien spacecraft and bodies were recovered
- Materials were transported to [Area 51](../entities/area-51.md) or other classified facilities for analysis
- The government maintained a deliberate cover-up

Despite the Air Force's comprehensive 1994 report confirming Project Mogul as the source, the narrative persists. Witnesses claimed bodies were recovered and autopsies conducted, claims amplified by the debunked 1995 [Alien Autopsy](../events/ufo-hoaxes.md) footage produced by Ray Santilli.

## MJ-12 Documents

The "Majestic 12" (MJ-12) documents, purporting to describe a secret committee established by President Truman to manage the Roswell recovery, have been assessed as fabrications. The sources reviewed do not provide extensive detail on MJ-12; additional sourcing is needed for a complete treatment.
