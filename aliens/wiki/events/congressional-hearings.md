# Congressional Hearings on UAP (2023-2025)

> **Summary:** Between 2023 and 2025, the U.S. House Oversight Committee held a series of hearings on unidentified anomalous phenomena that brought unprecedented congressional attention to UAP. The most consequential was the July 26, 2023 hearing featuring David Grusch, a former Air Force intelligence officer who testified under oath that the U.S. government operates a multi-decade program to retrieve crashed UAP craft of non-human origin. AARO found no verifiable evidence to substantiate these claims, attributing allegations to misinterpretations of classified programs. Subsequent hearings in November 2024 and September 2025 continued examining transparency deficits but produced no corroborated physical evidence of non-human technology.

## July 26, 2023: House Oversight Committee

### Setting

The hearing, titled "Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena: Implications on National Security, Public Safety, and Government Transparency," was held before the House Oversight Committee's Subcommittee on National Security, the Border, and Foreign Affairs.

### David Grusch Testimony

Former Air Force intelligence officer David Grusch, who served as a representative to the UAP Task Force until 2022, testified under oath:

- The U.S. government operates a multi-decade program to retrieve crashed UAP craft of "non-human origin"
- Non-human "biologics" have been recovered from crash sites
- His claims were based on information from over 40 witnesses during his official duties
- He had not personally viewed such materials
- He alleged retaliation for his disclosures
- He cited classification restrictions for withholding specifics in the public session, deferring details to secure channels

### AARO Response

The All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office ([AARO](../entities/aaro.md)) responded that:
- It had found no verifiable information to substantiate claims of reverse-engineering non-human technology or covert retrieval programs
- Director Sean Kirkpatrick noted Grusch's refusal to provide details despite invitations for interviews, including one on November 14, 2023
- Allegations often traced to misinterpretations of classified U.S. programs rather than extraterrestrial origins

### Assessment

Grusch's testimony was based on second-hand accounts (hearsay) from named and unnamed witnesses. He provided no direct evidence during the public session. The hearing amplified calls for declassification but highlighted fundamental evidentiary gaps: assertions unaccompanied by empirical data, contrasting official denials grounded in systematic records reviews.

## November 13, 2024: "Exposing the Truth"

The House Oversight hearing "Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena: Exposing the Truth" examined:
- Alleged covert programs, including one code-named "Immaculate Constellation"
- Witnesses reported first-hand UAP encounters
- No corroborated non-human recoveries were presented
- Focus on transparency deficits and excessive classification impeding congressional oversight

## September 9, 2025: Whistleblower Protection

The Subcommittee on Government Operations and the Federal Workforce convened "Restoring Public Trust Through UAP Transparency and Whistleblower Protection." Key developments:

- Military whistleblowers presented new alleged evidence, including video of a Hellfire missile reportedly bouncing off a UAP without effect
- Witnesses urged declassification to address over-classification hindering oversight
- AARO maintained its position: no evidence for extraterrestrial programs; unresolved cases attributed to prosaic explanations or foreign adversaries
- Acknowledged transparency shortfalls in interagency data sharing

## Recurring Themes Across Hearings

1. **Transparency deficits:** Excessive classification genuinely impedes congressional access to UAP-related programs
2. **Evidentiary gap:** Whistleblower claims of non-human recoveries remain unsubstantiated against AARO's exhaustive archival searches
3. **Institutional tension:** Congressional desire for disclosure versus executive branch classification imperatives
4. **Empirical priority:** AARO consistently favors investigating UAP as potential adversarial drone incursions over speculative biologics retrievals lacking forensic verification

## Broader Context

These hearings represent the most sustained congressional engagement with UFO/UAP phenomena since the closure of [Project Blue Book](../events/project-blue-book.md) in 1969. They were enabled by the institutional pathway created by [AATIP](../events/aatip.md)'s 2017 disclosure, the [2021 ODNI report](../events/ufo-report-2021.md), and the establishment of [AARO](../entities/aaro.md).

The hearings have revealed genuine problems with government transparency and over-classification, even as the specific claims of non-human technology recoveries remain without physical corroboration.
