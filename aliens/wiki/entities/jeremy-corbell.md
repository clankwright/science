# Jeremy Corbell

> **Summary:** Jeremy Kenyon Lockyer Corbell (born February 3, 1977) is an American investigative filmmaker, contemporary artist, and ufologist based in Los Angeles. He is best known for producing documentaries on UAP topics, including *Bob Lazar: Area 51 & Flying Saucers* (2018) and *Hunt for the Skinwalker* (2018), and for sourcing and releasing authenticated U.S. military UAP footage, including the USS Russell "pyramid" video (2019), USS Omaha "sphere" video (2019), and the Iraq "jellyfish" footage (2018). As co-host of the *Weaponized* podcast with journalist George Knapp, Corbell has become a central figure in the UAP disclosure movement, though he draws persistent criticism for alleged sensationalism and insufficient verification of extraordinary claims.

## Early Life and Martial Arts

Corbell grew up as the middle child among three brothers. He began formal jujitsu training at age nine (c. 1986), eventually attaining a black belt in traditional jujitsu. He developed a specialized discipline called "Quantum Jujitsu," integrating conventional martial arts with philosophical elements, and taught jujitsu and yoga at the University of California, Santa Cruz. A near-fatal respiratory disease (Valley fever contracted while traveling in Nepal) severely limited his physical capacity, prompting a career pivot to creative fields.

## Artistic Career

Corbell entered visual art in 2004 during his Valley fever recovery, painting on vintage doors salvaged from Los Angeles demolitions. His mixed-media style evolved into immersive installations incorporating photography, newsprint, and layered assemblages, often using reclaimed materials.

Key exhibitions include:
- **"ICON: Life Love & Style of Sharon Tate"** (debuted August 9, 2009, 40th anniversary of Tate's death, at High Profile Productions in Culver City, CA): An 8,000-square-foot installation featuring enlarged Polaroid images of a model in Tate's original wardrobe (Christian Dior, Yves Saint Laurent). Created with the Tate family's endorsement, it expanded to 350 pieces by the 50th anniversary in 2019.
- **"Women in Sunglasses"**: Immersive female portraits blending photography, fashion, and sculptural components.
- **"The Pope"**: Incorporated *Los Angeles Times* clippings on the Catholic Church's stem-cell research positions.

## Filmmaking and Documentaries

### Early Films

- **Moonwalker**: Centers on Apollo 14 astronaut Edgar Mitchell and his advocacy for extraterrestrial intelligence, including claims of government concealment regarding non-human entities.
- **Patient Seventeen** (October 10, 2017): Corbell's first major documentary release, documenting podiatrist Roger Leir's surgical removals of purported alien implants from patients. The film integrates raw surgical footage, metallurgical and isotopic analyses (reportedly showing iridium isotopes atypical for human technology), and interviews, though critics note the findings lack conclusive replication.

### Bob Lazar: [Area 51](area-51.md) & Flying Saucers (2018)

Released December 4, 2018 (97 minutes), this film examines [Bob Lazar](bob-lazar.md)'s assertions of reverse-engineering extraterrestrial craft at [S-4](papoose-lake.md) near [Area 51](area-51.md). It includes rare interviews with Lazar, commentary from George Knapp, and footage of an FBI raid on Lazar's home in 2017 related to exotic materials. The film received approximately 6,800 IMDb ratings averaging 5.6/10, reflecting polarized reception.

### TMZ Presents: UFO Revolution (2023-2025)

A three-part docuseries (later renewed for a second season, January 2025) examining U.S. government UAP disclosures. Season 2 follows Corbell's efforts in Washington, D.C., including a new whistleblower reveal and scrutiny of the "Jellyfish" UAP incident.

### S4: The Bob Lazar Story (2026)

Directed by Luigi Vendittelli, produced by Project Gravitaur, narrated by Lazar. Released April 3, 2026, featuring 3D recreations of S-4. Corbell posted a teaser on X in March 2026.

## Notable Footage Releases

Corbell has released several U.S. military UAP videos obtained through anonymous sources, with some authenticated by the Pentagon:

| Date | Footage | Details | Pentagon Status |
|------|---------|---------|-----------------|
| April 2021 | USS Russell "pyramids" | Night-vision footage from 2019 off California showing pyramid-shaped objects hovering above the ship | Confirmed authentic April 16, 2021; under UAP Task Force review |
| May 2021 | USS Omaha "sphere" | Infrared video from July 15, 2019, showing a spherical object descending and submerging without splash; radar data indicated up to 14 objects swarming vessels over three days | Not disputed; classified as "unidentified" |
| January 2024 | Iraq "jellyfish" | Thermographic footage from a 2018 U.S. forward operating base depicting an object with appendages traversing from sky to ground | DoD acknowledged awareness but neither confirmed nor debunked |
| July 2025 | Disc-shaped UAP | 2020 military footage showing a disc exhibiting controlled flight without visible propulsion | No official verification as of October 2025 |

Corbell's verification process typically involves cross-referencing metadata, consulting original operators, and ruling out hoaxes through forensic analysis.

## Weaponized Podcast

Launched in early 2023, *Weaponized* is a weekly multi-platform podcast co-hosted with investigative journalist George Knapp, focused on UAP, government secrecy, and unexplained events. The program features exclusive interviews with witnesses, declassified documents, and analyses of military footage. Corbell and Knapp have presented joint research at events including the 2023 McMenamins UFO Festival.

## Congressional and Disclosure Advocacy

- **July 26, 2023:** Corbell submitted "The UAP Puzzle" to the U.S. House Oversight Committee's UAP hearing, arguing withheld UAP information has been intentionally obscured.
- **November 13, 2024:** Corbell submitted a 12-page whistleblower report detailing the alleged "Immaculate Constellation" unacknowledged Special Access Program to journalist Michael Shellenberger, who referenced it during House Committee on Oversight and Accountability testimony. Rep. Nancy Mace presented the report but credited only Shellenberger, omitting Corbell's role, which Corbell publicly criticized as an "erasure" endangering whistleblowers.
- **2025:** Published an open letter to [AARO](aaro.md), criticizing its transparency and treatment of whistleblowers, demanding full release of 2017 "Jellyfish" UAP footage from Lake Habbaniyah, Iraq, and calling for AARO's new director to denounce alleged slander against whistleblowers David Grusch and Matthew Brown.
- **2025:** Spoke at Contact in the Desert and RenderCon on intersections of UAP evidence with artificial superintelligence.

## Criticisms and Controversies

### Sensationalism Accusations

Critics accuse Corbell of prioritizing dramatic narratives over empirical verification. Specific disputes include:

- **"Pyramid" footage (2021):** Skeptical analysts (including Mick West) proposed the triangular formations as bokeh effects from distant aircraft lights captured through the camera's aperture in low-light conditions, consistent with commercial flight data.
- **"Jellyfish" footage (2024):** Physicists and aviation experts proposed prosaic explanations, including a mylar balloon with attached debris or a bird in thermal bloom, citing the object's apparent lack of structural mass and absence of propulsion signatures.
- **"Mojave Triangle" video (2023):** Aviation databases and eyewitness cross-examinations suggested misidentification of a stealth aircraft or drone, with the reported lack of noise attributable to high-altitude operation.

The [AARO](aaro.md) 2024 assessment found no verifiable evidence of non-human intelligence across reviewed UAP cases, including those popularized by Corbell.

### Broader Criticisms

Analyses of the UAP attention economy suggest Corbell's media ventures contribute to "infotainment" dynamics where whistleblower testimonies are amplified for audience engagement. Critics contend his investigative style, rooted in rapid dissemination via platforms like TMZ and podcasts, prioritizes viral impact over causal scrutiny. Post-release debunkings have reverted some incidents to conventional explanations (lens flares, drones, atmospheric phenomena).

### Defense

Corbell maintains his releases advance transparency by bypassing institutional gatekeeping, and his track record includes multiple Pentagon-authenticated videos. His work has corroborated official acknowledgments and sustained momentum in policy advocacy for [UAP disclosure](../events/congressional-hearings.md).
