# UFO Organizations: International

> **Summary:** A survey of UFO/UAP research organizations operating outside the United States, spanning government-sanctioned programs (France's GEIPAN, Chile's CEFAA, Uruguay's CRIDOVNI), large civilian networks (BUFORA, UFO-Sverige, Kosmopoisk), and regional groups across Europe, South America, Asia, Oceania, and Africa. These organizations reflect varied national approaches to anomalous aerial phenomena, from France's unique government integration under CNES to Russia's expedition-based Kosmopoisk to Belgium's SOBEPS, which conducted the most thoroughly documented investigation of the 1989-1990 Belgian UFO wave. Comprehensive reviews, including AARO's historical report, affirm no empirical evidence exists for extraterrestrial technology, with most organizations finding 90%+ of cases attributable to prosaic explanations.

## European Organizations

### France: GEIPAN

GEIPAN (Groupe d'Etudes et d'Informations sur les Phenomenes Aerospatiaux Non-identifies) is the most prominent government-sanctioned UAP research body in the world, operational since 2005 under the French space agency CNES. It succeeded two earlier programs:

- **GEPAN (1977-1988):** Handled initial official inquiries into 1,000+ cases, established during a sighting wave
- **SEPRA (1999-2004):** Interim centralized reporting body

GEIPAN maintains a database of approximately 8,000 reports (as of 2023), classifying them as identified (atmospheric phenomena, aircraft) or unexplained (about 5% of cases, lacking sufficient data for resolution). It employs four full-time staff plus volunteers, emphasizes empirical verification, and has provided public access to declassified files since 2007.

French civilian groups include CNEGU (Comite Nord-Est des Groupes Ufologiques, formed 1978), a regional network whose activities waned over time, and France Ufologie (established 1992), focused on data compilation and public education.

### Belgium: SOBEPS

The Societe Belge d'Etude des Phenomenes Spatiaux (SOBEPS), established in the 1970s and active until approximately 2003, specialized in field investigations of UFO reports. Its most significant work involved the 1989-1990 [Belgian UFO wave](../events/belgian-ufo-wave.md): over 13,500 witnesses reported triangular objects, some corroborated by ground radar and pursued by F-16 fighters on March 30-31, 1990. SOBEPS analyses deemed a subset of these sightings as defying conventional aircraft or atmospheric explanations, making it one of the most thoroughly documented mass UFO events in history.

### Germany: CENAP

The Centrales Erforschungsnetz aussergewohnlicher Himmelsphansomene (CENAP), founded in 1976 by Werner Walter and Hansjurgen Kohler following Walter's 1973 sighting, operates as a research network investigating anomalous aerial phenomena through empirical analysis and witness interviews. It maintains a hotline that has logged over 10,000 reports since inception, with 800 received in 2023 alone. CENAP emphasizes prosaic explanations over extraterrestrial hypotheses.

### Italy: CISU

The Centro Italiano Studi Ufologici (CISU), formed in 1985 by consolidating regional groups, operates as a nonprofit archiving sightings, publishing the bimonthly *UFO - Rivista di Informazione Ufologica*, and conducting cataloged investigations into Italian cases spanning decades. It prioritizes data verification over speculative interpretations.

### United Kingdom: BUFORA and Others

- **British UFO Research Association (BUFORA):** Established 1962 through federation of regional groups; longest-standing UK national UFO body. Emphasizes scientific evaluation over speculative interpretations, with trained field investigators and annual conferences. As of 2024, the sole national UFO research entity in the UK.
- **British Ufology (formalized 2015):** Scientific-oriented studies of UK encounters.
- **Welsh UFO Research Network (WUFORN):** Regional Welsh investigations with confidentiality protocols.
- **British Flying Saucer Bureau:** Defunct; ceased prior to 2005.

### Sweden: UFO-Sverige and AFU

- **UFO-Sverige (Riksorganisationen UFO-Sverige):** Founded Easter 1970 as Sweden's principal science-oriented UFO organization. Collects approximately 250 reports per year, attributing over 90% to identifiable causes. Maintains a national reporting center and conducts field investigations.
- **Archives for the Unexplained (AFU):** Established 1973 in Norrkoping as a foundation preserving documentation on UFOs, ufological studies, forteana, and cryptozoology. Its collections span 4.2 kilometers of shelving as of 2024, documenting approximately 150 historical UFO groups in Sweden alone. Prioritizes archival preservation over active investigation.

### Estonia: EUFORA

The Estonian UFO Research Association (EUFORA), registered September 12, 2023, investigates UFO phenomena through scientific methods including a UFO register database. It succeeded earlier efforts by Igor Volke (1950-2024), who began systematic investigations in 1972 and amassed approximately 800 cases over five decades. Following Volke's death on November 14, 2024, the Igor Volke Anomalistika Keskus was established to preserve his archives.

### Netherlands: UAP Coalition Netherlands

A recent nonprofit targeting aviation, law enforcement, and military personnel with direct UAP encounters. Facilitates anonymous reporting and interdisciplinary review without presuming non-human origins.

### Russia: Kosmopoisk

Established in 1980, Kosmopoisk conducts annual expeditions to reported UFO sighting locations, collects testimonies, and examines physical traces. In July 2015, it documented crop circles near Krasnodar. The organization maintains skepticism toward extraterrestrial hypotheses, often correlating sightings with meteorological events or military tests. Soviet-era studies attributed 90%+ of phenomena to rocket exhausts and atmospheric optics.

Other Russian entities include the International Information Center for UFO Investigation in Saint Petersburg, led by Valery Uvarov, which compiles declassified military data. Post-Soviet state interest has been handled through ad hoc commissions of the Russian Academy of Sciences.

### Belarus: UFOCOM

The Belarusian Ufological Committee (UFOCOM), founded May 1, 2001, in Gomel, operates informally with approximately 20 active members across branches in Minsk, Gomel, and Vitebsk (~50 total network). Conducts annual field expeditions and collaborates internationally with Kosmopoisk since 2005. Published *Tainstvennaya Belarus* exploring mythological and anomalous elements in Belarusian history.

## South American Organizations

### Brazil

- **Comissao Brasileira de Ufologos (CBU):** Established 1997, campaigns for declassification of Brazilian Air Force UFO documents dating to events like 1977-1978 Operacao Prato. The Brazilian Air Force has recorded over 700 UFO incidents since the 1950s, including the notable 1986 Official UFO Night involving 21 radar-tracked objects.
- **Regional groups:** GEPURP (Sao Jose do Rio Preto), Ufologia Integral Brasil (UIB)

### Chile: CEFAA

The Committee for the Study of Anomalous Aerial Phenomena, formed 1997 under the Directorate General of Civil Aeronautics, serves as Chile's official UAP body. It has declassified videos including a 2014 Navy helicopter sighting of an infrared object with unusual thermal signatures unexplained by conventional sources.

### Argentina: CEFAE

The Commission for the Study of Aerospace Phenomena, established May 2011 within the Argentine Air Force, analyzed nearly 40 sightings (attributing most to conventional explanations) before disbanding in February 2016 amid criticisms of inactivity.

### Uruguay: CRIDOVNI

The Commission for the Reception and Investigation of Reports of Unidentified Flying Objects, created August 7, 1979, by the Uruguayan Air Force, actively processes complaints with seven permanent members as of 2023. Complemented by the civilian CIOVI (est. April 29, 1958).

### Peru

Reopened an official UFO investigation office in 2014, though operational details remain limited in public records.

## Asian Organizations

### Japan

- **Japan Flying Saucer Research Association (est. 1955):** First organized Japanese UFO group, founded by Arai Kin'ichi. Members included author Yukio Mishima (joined 1956, personally skeptical). Largely defunct.
- **Cosmic Brotherhood Association (late 1950s):** Esoteric branch advocating telepathic communication with aliens via mantra "Bentra, Bentra." Aligned with contactee movements rather than scientific methodology.
- **International UFO Laboratory (opened June 24, 2021):** Located in Iino, Fukushima Prefecture, supported by local government for tourism and research. Catalogs incidents from the "UFO Village" area reported since the 1970s.

### China

China UFO Research Organization (CURO, est. 1978), evolved into China UFO Research Association (CURA, 1980) with provincial branches and ties to the China Association for Science and Technology. Activities faced restrictions post-2010s.

### India, Indonesia, Philippines, South Korea, Malaysia

Multiple civilian organizations operate across Asia, including India's TOP Research Group, IARF Centre of Excellence for UFOs (ICEU, est. 2021), and Alien & UFO Research Organisation of India (AURO). Indonesia's BETA-UFO (est. 1997) claims 19,000+ registered members. The UFO Society of the Philippines (est. 2010) and Korea UAP Society maintain regional focus.

## Oceanian Organizations

### Australia

- **UFO Research Queensland (UFORQ, est. 1956):** Oldest, based in Brisbane
- **UFO Research (NSW) Inc. (est. 1991):** Based in Sydney, publishes *UFO Reporter*
- **Victorian UFO Research Society (VUFORS, 1950s origins):** Published *Australian UFO Bulletin* quarterly
- **Australian Centre for UFO Studies (ACUFOS, late 1970s):** Inspired by J. Allen Hynek's work; dissolved mid-1980s

## African Organizations

### South Africa

Primarily small civilian groups including:
- **South African UFO Resource (SAUFOR):** Founded by Cristo Louw, observed rising sightings since 2010
- **UFO Research South Africa (UfoRSA):** Hotline and website for sighting submissions
- **MUFON SA (late 1990s):** Pretoria-based branch, now less active
- **ECUFOR (Port Elizabeth):** Handled case assessments in early 2000s

## Skeptical and Debunking Organizations

- **Committee for Skeptical Inquiry (CSI, est. 1976):** Publishes *Skeptical Inquirer*; applies scientific skepticism to UFO claims, converting UFOs to IFOs through prosaic identification. Affiliated with Center for Inquiry.
- **Project Sign (1947-1949), Project Grudge (1949-1952), [Project Blue Book](../events/project-blue-book.md) (1952-1969):** Sequential U.S. Air Force investigations examining 12,618 sightings total; ~94% explained, 701 unidentified. Concluded UFOs posed no national security threat.
- **Condon Committee (1966-1968):** University of Colorado academic effort examining 59 cases; found no compelling evidence for anomalous phenomena, recommended ending government UFO investigations.
