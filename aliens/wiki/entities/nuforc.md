# NUFORC (National UFO Reporting Center)

> **Summary:** The National UFO Reporting Center (NUFORC), established in 1974 and based in Seattle, Washington, functions as a centralized public database for UFO/UAP sighting reports in the United States. Unlike investigative organizations such as [MUFON](mufon.md), NUFORC focuses on aggregation and transparency, making sightings, images, videos, and maps publicly accessible without initial credibility vetting. In the first half of 2025 alone, NUFORC received over 3,000 reports (2,000+ UFO sightings), reflecting a surge attributed partly to reduced stigma following government UAP disclosures. The center has been cited in analyses of U.S. sighting hotspots and serves as a primary resource for researchers studying geographic and temporal patterns.

## History and Mission

NUFORC was established in 1974, five years after the termination of [Project Blue Book](../events/project-blue-book.md) left a void in centralized UFO data collection. Based in Seattle, Washington, the center was conceived as a neutral repository for public-submitted UFO/UAP sighting reports, prioritizing accessibility and comprehensive data capture over investigative analysis.

The center's approach differs fundamentally from organizations like [MUFON](mufon.md) or the Center for UFO Studies (CUFOS): NUFORC does not conduct field investigations but instead focuses on making raw sighting data publicly available to support independent researchers.

## Operations

### Reporting System

NUFORC accepts reports from the general public through phone hotlines and online submission forms. Reports are logged with details including:
- Date, time, and location of sighting
- Duration and description of observed phenomena
- Witness information
- Supporting media (photographs, videos)

Reports are published without initial vetting for credibility, reflecting the center's philosophy of comprehensive data capture. This approach allows researchers to apply their own analytical frameworks while ensuring no reports are filtered out prematurely.

### Database and Public Access

The NUFORC database is publicly accessible online, featuring sightings, images, videos, and interactive maps. It represents one of the largest aggregated UFO sighting datasets in the United States. The center notes that underreporting remains likely due to persistent social stigma around UFO sightings, even as reporting rates have increased following government disclosures.

## Volume and Trends

In the first half of 2025, NUFORC received over 3,000 reports, with more than 2,000 classified as UFO sightings, outpacing [MUFON](mufon.md)'s totals for the same period. The surge in reporting has been attributed to:
- Reduced stigma following government UAP disclosures and [congressional hearings](../events/congressional-hearings.md)
- Increased public awareness of UAP topics in mainstream media
- The establishment of official reporting mechanisms (e.g., [AARO](aaro.md)) legitimizing the topic

NUFORC data has been cited in analyses identifying U.S. sighting hotspots correlating with low-light-pollution areas, suggesting that observational conditions play a significant role in reporting frequency.

## Role in Research

NUFORC's value lies primarily in its comprehensive, unfiltered dataset, which enables:
- Geographic and temporal pattern analysis
- Correlation studies with environmental factors (light pollution, weather, military activity)
- Baseline comparisons for evaluating UAP report characteristics
- Historical trend analysis across decades of sighting data

The center avoids endorsing specific explanations for reported phenomena, positioning itself as infrastructure for research rather than an advocacy organization.

## Limitations

- No initial credibility vetting means the database includes misidentifications, hoaxes, and low-quality reports alongside potentially significant observations
- Lack of field investigation capability means raw reports are not independently verified
- Self-selection bias in reporting (witnesses must actively choose to submit)
- Geographic concentration in the U.S. limits international coverage
