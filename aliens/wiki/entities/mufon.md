# MUFON (Mutual UFO Network)

> **Summary:** The Mutual UFO Network (MUFON), founded in 1969 and headquartered in Cincinnati, Ohio, is the largest and most prominent civilian nonprofit organization dedicated to the scientific investigation of UFO/UAP sightings worldwide. Operating a global network of trained field investigators across 50+ countries, MUFON processes hundreds of reports monthly, publishes the MUFON Journal, hosts annual symposia, and maintains an active case management system. It represents one of the few civilian organizations that survived the post-Blue Book era to remain operationally significant through the 2020s UAP disclosure wave. Critics note inconsistent methodologies in some investigations and an emphasis on extraterrestrial hypotheses over prosaic alternatives.

## History

MUFON was established in 1969, the same year the U.S. Air Force terminated [Project Blue Book](../events/project-blue-book.md). Its founding reflected a growing perception among civilian researchers that government UFO investigations had been insufficiently rigorous or transparent. The organization emerged partly from researchers who had been affiliated with the Aerial Phenomena Research Organization (APRO), which split in 1969.

MUFON quickly became the primary successor to earlier civilian groups like NICAP (National Investigations Committee on Aerial Phenomena, est. 1956, dissolved late 1970s) and APRO (est. 1952, ceased 1988), absorbing some members and files from these defunct organizations.

## Structure and Operations

### Field Investigation Network

MUFON maintains trained field investigators in over 50 countries who analyze reports using standardized protocols, including:
- Witness interviews
- Photographic and video evaluation
- Physical evidence collection
- Site inspections

### Database and Reporting

As of 2025, MUFON's active case management system processes hundreds of reports monthly. The organization documented robust UFO activity in the first seven months of 2025, though it was outnumbered by [NUFORC](nuforc.md)'s totals for the same period. The database represents one of the most comprehensive civilian repositories of UFO sighting data globally.

### Publications

The MUFON Journal is the organization's primary publication, with the October 2025 issue covering ongoing analyses. MUFON also publishes annual reports, investigator handbooks, and symposium proceedings.

### Symposia and Public Engagement

MUFON hosts annual symposia bringing together researchers, investigators, and public audiences. Members have provided expert testimony on non-human technology indicators during [congressional hearings](../events/congressional-hearings.md), reflecting the organization's evolving role from fringe advocacy to recognized participant in UAP policy discussions.

## Methods and Approach

MUFON's investigation methodology emphasizes empirical data collection through standardized reporting forms and trained field investigators. Reports are categorized using classification systems (including conversion of UFOs to identified flying objects, or IFOs, when prosaic explanations are established). The organization aims to distinguish genuine anomalies from misidentifications, though critics argue its institutional culture sometimes favors extraordinary explanations over mundane ones.

## Criticisms

- Inconsistent methodologies across the volunteer investigator network
- Organizational emphasis on extraterrestrial hypotheses over prosaic alternatives in some cases
- Quality control challenges given reliance on volunteer labor
- Competition with government-funded bodies like [AARO](aaro.md) that apply more rigorous analytical standards

## Relationship to Other Organizations

MUFON's role in the broader UFO/UAP landscape:
- Predates and outlasted most other civilian UFO groups in the U.S.
- Coexists with [NUFORC](nuforc.md) (reporting-focused) and CUFOS (archive-focused)
- Provincial branches (e.g., British Columbia MUFON) extend its reach internationally
- Distinguished from newer advocacy groups like Americans for Safe Aerospace (ASA, est. 2023), which focuses specifically on aviation safety aspects of UAP
