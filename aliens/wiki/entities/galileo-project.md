# The Galileo Project

> **Summary:** The Galileo Project is a systematic scientific research initiative launched in 2021 by Harvard astronomer Avi Loeb to study unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAP) and potential extraterrestrial technological signatures using calibrated, ground-based instruments. The project deploys arrays of wide-field telescopes, radar systems, audio sensors, and spectrometers for automated detection of transient aerial objects, applying machine learning to distinguish UAP from known objects such as birds, drones, and satellites. In November 2024, the project released commissioning data on over 500,000 sky objects.

## Origins and Motivation

The Galileo Project was initiated in 2021 by Avi Loeb, a professor of astronomy at Harvard University. The project emerged from the recognition that decades of UAP research had relied primarily on anecdotal eyewitness testimony and low-resolution imagery, yielding no paradigm-shifting discoveries. Loeb proposed replacing this approach with proactive, instrumented surveillance capable of generating verifiable data.

The project's founding was informed by the broader shift in UAP discourse following the 2017 Pentagon disclosures, the [2021 ODNI preliminary assessment](../events/ufo-report-2021.md), and the growing acknowledgment by government bodies that sensor limitations and data gaps, rather than confirmed anomalies, accounted for most unresolved cases.

## Methodology and Instrumentation

The Galileo Project proposes deploying multimodal ground-based observatories to address deficiencies in real-time, high-fidelity data collection. The instrumentation includes:

- **Wide-field telescopes:** Optical and infrared cameras for all-sky monitoring
- **Narrow-field instruments:** For spectral and polarimetric analysis of detected objects
- **Passive multistatic radar:** Capable of measuring velocities up to tens of km/s
- **Radio spectrum analyzers:** For detecting emissions from anomalous objects
- **Acoustic sensors:** Covering infrasonic to ultrasonic ranges
- **Environmental monitors:** Including magnetometers with sub-nanotesla noise levels and particle counters

These systems are designed for triangulation, real-time data fusion, and hypothesis testing, such as parallax measurements via telescope arrays to distinguish atmospheric from extraterrestrial objects. Machine learning classification algorithms filter transients against known celestial and terrestrial sources.

Initial peer-reviewed methodology papers have highlighted the necessity for such instrumentation to test hypotheses empirically, noting that prior reliance on anecdotal evidence has hindered progress in the field.

## Results and Data Releases

In November 2024, the Galileo Project released commissioning data covering over 500,000 sky objects, enabling statistical filtering of transients against known celestial and terrestrial sources. Plans include expanding sensor networks to achieve real-time tracking capabilities.

The project has also proposed scaling commercial satellite-based approaches for broader anomaly detection, using AI to flag non-ballistic motions indicative of unconventional propulsion in orbital or near-space regimes.

## Complementary Efforts

The Galileo Project operates alongside other instrumented approaches to UAP research:

- The University at Albany's UFODAP system, tested in 2025, utilizes networked sensors for precise triangulation of aerial anomalies to quantify velocities and trajectories beyond eyewitness limits
- The [NASA UAP Independent Study](../entities/nasa-uap-study.md) similarly recommended leveraging satellite imagery and environmental sensors for rigorous analysis
- [AARO](../entities/aaro.md)'s Gremlin System, a multisensor networked kit for detecting UAP across domains, with deployments planned for fiscal year 2025

## Assessment

The Galileo Project represents the most prominent academic effort to apply systematic scientific instrumentation to UAP research. Its approach aligns with the evidence-based methodology advocated by researchers like [Jacques Vallee](../entities/jacques-vallee.md), who emphasized the need for rigorous skepticism, primary data collection, and interdisciplinary perspectives. To date, the project has not reported any confirmed anomalous detections that defy prosaic explanation, consistent with the broader pattern where instrumental scrutiny reveals unexplained cases stem from incomplete datasets rather than confirmed anomalies.
