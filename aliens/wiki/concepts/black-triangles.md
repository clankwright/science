# Black Triangle UFOs

> **Summary:** Black triangle UFOs are a category of unidentified aerial phenomena described as massive, equilateral triangular craft, typically black or matte dark, capable of silent hovering and sudden acceleration, featuring steady white lights at each apex and often a pulsating red or orange light beneath. Reports surged from the late 1980s onward, with estimated sizes ranging from 100 to 1,000 feet per side. Major clusters include the 1982-1986 Hudson Valley sightings, the 1989-1990 Belgian wave (13,500+ witnesses, F-16 radar contacts), and the 1997 Phoenix Lights. Explanations range from misidentified stealth aircraft (F-117, B-2) and military formations to the UK's Project Condign "buoyant charged plasma" model, though a subset of reports resists prosaic attribution.

## Physical Description

Black triangle UFOs are reported as large aerial objects with distinct triangular silhouettes, typically observed at night. Key characteristics from witness accounts:

- **Shape**: Equilateral or isosceles triangle with straight edges
- **Surface**: Uniformly black or matte dark, nearly invisible except when backlit or silhouetted
- **Size**: Estimates range from 30 meters to over 100 meters per side; some reports liken scale to a football field
- **Lights**: Three prominent lights at vertices (steady white or amber), often with a central pulsating red beacon
- **Surface details**: Smooth, non-reflective underside lacking visible seams, rivets, or propulsion outlets

### Reported Performance

- **Silent operation**: No detectable engine noise despite reported sizes of 100-300 feet
- **Precise hovering**: Remaining stationary for extended periods at various altitudes
- **Slow controlled gliding**: 10-50 mph, allowing observers to track triangular outlines
- **Abrupt acceleration**: Transitions from hover to 600+ mph; extreme g-forces, sharp 90-degree turns
- **No visible exhaust** or aerodynamic strain during maneuvers

## Major Sighting Waves

### Hudson Valley, New York (1982-1986)

The earliest concentrated North American wave. Thousands of witnesses, including police and pilots, described a massive craft (300-1,000 feet across) with a V- or boomerang-shaped silhouette, silent operation, and clusters of steady lights along the leading edge.

Peak event: March 24, 1983, when over 300 residents across Westchester, Dutchess, and Putnam counties reported the object between 8:40 and 9:30 p.m., causing traffic jams on the Taconic State Parkway. Retired Yorktown Police Lieutenant Kevin Soravilla recounted a triangular craft ~100 yards wide banking at 45 degrees before speeding away. No military flights were logged from nearby Stewart Air Force Base. The FAA received numerous calls but provided no conclusive identification.

Skeptical analyses attribute some sightings to formations of ultralight aircraft, but this fails to fully explain the reported size, silence, and hovering capabilities.

### Belgian UFO Wave (1989-1992)

The most extensively documented international cluster. Over 2,000 sightings were documented through 1992, with the peak between November 1989 and April 1990. Reports described craft approximately 30-40 meters per side.

**November 29, 1989**: More than 30 independent witness groups across multiple locations, including police officers in Eupen, reported low-flying triangular craft.

**March 30-31, 1990 (F-16 intercept)**: The Belgian Air Force deployed two F-16 fighters from Beauvechain Air Base. Ground radars at Glons and Semmerzake registered 13 targets exhibiting:
- Rapid descents from 9-10 km altitude to near sea level in 30-40 seconds
- Accelerations from 150 km/h to over 1,100 km/h
- Instantaneous direction changes exceeding 40 g-forces
- Radar locks maintained only 10-60 seconds before breaks

Major General Wilfried De Brouwer confirmed the Air Force investigations yielded no identification. The SOBEPS (Societe Belge d'Etude des Phenomenes Spatiaux) compiled extensive witness statements and radar data.

**Skeptical assessments**: The Gilmard-Salmon report attributed radar contacts to "angel echoes" from atmospheric interference and Bragg scattering. The widely circulated Petit-Rechain photograph was confessed as a hoax in 2011, fabricated using a polystyrene model with flashlights. Initial Eupen observations may have been a Bell UH-1 Huey helicopter, with Venus matching a reported stationary light. Analyst Philip J. Klass argued media amplification created a psychosocial feedback loop.

### Phoenix Lights (March 13, 1997)

Thousands of witnesses across Nevada and Arizona reported a V-shaped formation of lights traversing silently from northwest to southeast, beginning near Henderson, Nevada (~7:55 p.m. MST) and reaching Phoenix by ~8:15-8:40 p.m. Descriptions portrayed an immense craft (estimates up to a mile wide) with five to seven stationary lights that reportedly obscured stars.

Arizona Governor Fife Symington III initially mocked the reports at a June 1997 press conference (featuring an aide in an alien costume) but in 2007 affirmed the sighting's authenticity, describing it as "something extraordinary that defies explanation."

A separate ~10:00 p.m. event involved lights descending sequentially, which the U.S. Air Force attributed to LUU-2B/B illumination flares from A-10 Thunderbolt II aircraft during Operation Snowbird training at the Barry M. Goldwater Range. Witnesses contend the flares do not explain the earlier cohesive V-formation's low altitude, uniform speed, lack of descent/flicker, and absence of radar or sonic signatures.

### Illinois Incidents (2000-2004)

**January 5, 2000 (St. Clair County)**: Four police officers pursued a black deltoid craft (~600 feet long, 300 feet wide) with corner lights and central red beacon across ~100 km over an hour, passing within 1-2 miles of Scott Air Force Base. The National Institute for Discovery Science (NIDS), funded by Robert Bigelow, hypothesized a DoD prototype. Skeptical analysis identifies a conventional advertising blimp.

**August-October 2004 (Tinley Park)**: Hundreds of residents reported recurring sightings of three red lights in triangular formation, hovering silently or moving slowly. [MUFON](../entities/mufon.md) investigator Sam Maranto concluded the events were genuine but no physical evidence emerged.

### Post-2010 Reports

Sporadic reports continue near military installations:
- November 29, 2021: Large dark silent triangle near Joint Base Lewis-McChord, Washington
- September 28, 2022: Large black silent triangle at Langley Air Force Base, Virginia
- March 23, 2024: Large triangle hovering and rotating at Nellis Air Force Base, Nevada
- May 5, 2025: Football-field-sized black triangle with pale green lights over Houma, Louisiana ([NUFORC](../entities/nuforc.md))

Enigma Labs verified over 700 triangle sightings from late 2022 to 2024.

## Investigations and Official Inquiries

### UK Project Condign (2000, declassified 2006)

The Ministry of Defence's Defence Intelligence Staff conducted a three-year classified review examining over 10,000 pages of sighting data, radar records, and scientific literature. The Condign report:

- Dismissed extraterrestrial hypotheses due to lack of empirical evidence
- Advanced the **"buoyant charged plasma" model**: self-contained ionized atmospheric gases formed by interactions between weather fronts, thunderstorms, or anthropogenic electromagnetic emissions
- These plasmas, akin to expanded ball lightning, could assume geometric shapes through magnetic confinement, appear radar-reflective yet visually opaque, and exhibit hovering, sudden acceleration, and silence
- Noted such formations could induce physiological effects (nausea, time distortion) in close-range encounters
- Concluded 95% of reports were explainable; remainder yielded no actionable intelligence

Post-Condign, the MoD disbanded its UFO unit on December 1, 2009.

### U.S. Government Responses

- **[AATIP](../events/aatip.md)** (2007-2012): Investigated military UAP encounters but yielded no public conclusions specifically attributing black triangles to exotic origins
- **UAPTF** (2020-2022): Analyzed 144 cases, noting triangles among unresolved shapes
- **Pentagon confirmation (April 2021)**: Authenticated a 2019 infrared video of a triangular object with rotating lights off the California coast as genuine military footage
- **[AARO](../entities/aaro.md)** (2022-present): March 2024 report found no verifiable evidence of nonhuman technology; November 2024 update identified 21 unresolved cases (some geometric shapes near sensitive sites)
- **September 2025 hearings**: Military veterans testified about encounters with large, silent triangular craft exhibiting transmedium capabilities

## Explanations and Hypotheses

### Misidentification of Conventional Phenomena

- Stars or planets (especially Venus) aligned in triangular patterns due to parallax and atmospheric scintillation
- Aircraft navigation lights viewed from unusual angles creating illusory triangular frames
- Weather or research balloons appearing as dark shapes under partial illumination
- **NOSS satellite formations**: U.S. Naval Ocean Surveillance System satellites in fixed triangular array, producing bright, evenly spaced lights traversing the sky
- Temperature inversions or superior mirages distorting distant lights

### Stealth Aircraft

The **F-117 Nighthawk** (operational 1983-2008), with faceted angular surfaces and radar-absorbent black coating, was tested in secretive flights over Europe during the late 1980s, coinciding with the [Belgian wave](../events/belgian-ufo-wave.md). Its low-observable design explains intermittent radar detections.

The **B-2 Spirit** (first flown 1989, service 1997), with its broad flying-wing configuration resembling an equilateral triangle from below, matches many post-1990s reports. A 2025 San Antonio sighting initially reported as a mysterious dark triangle was confirmed as a B-2 transiting to Whiteman Air Force Base.

### Military Formation Flying

Multiple aircraft in tight V or delta patterns with navigation lights mimic the "lights at corners" motif. The Phoenix Lights' first event was attributed by the Air Force to seven A-10 aircraft in formation.

### The TR-3B Astra Claim

Described in aviation lore as a large black triangular craft (~600 feet across) employing nuclear-powered magnetic field propulsion using "highly pressured mercury plasma," the TR-3B originated from claims by aviation journalist Edgar Fouche in 1998. No declassified evidence, wreckage, or verifiable prototypes exist. Aviation experts assess the descriptions as unsubstantiated.

U.S. Navy patents filed by Salvatore Pais (2016-2019) describe a triangular hybrid aerospace-undersea vehicle utilizing inertial mass reduction, aligning superficially with reports. No operational prototypes have been demonstrated.

### Non-Human Origins

Proponents argue characteristics (silent hovering for hours, instantaneous acceleration to hypersonic velocities, sizes spanning hundreds of meters) exceed known human engineering. Researcher David Marler analyzed over 17,000 UFO reports noting recurring triangular morphologies. Alternative exotic frameworks include [ultraterrestrial or interdimensional origins](interdimensional-hypothesis.md) and the cryptoterrestrial model. No peer-reviewed analysis has confirmed propulsion mechanisms incompatible with earthly engineering.

## Evidence Assessment

Despite extensive reporting (17,000+ documented cases per Marler), no physical artifacts have been publicly recovered and subjected to independent scientific verification. AARO's March 2024 report explicitly states no empirical evidence exists for the recovery of extraterrestrial technology. Purported "metamaterials" or radar signatures have not yielded peer-reviewed studies demonstrating properties inconsistent with known terrestrial alloys.

The concentration of sightings near military installations, combined with the temporal correlation between stealth program development and reporting peaks, favors the advanced human technology explanation for many cases, though the scale, silence, and reported mile-wide dimensions of some reports remain difficult to reconcile with known engineering.
