# List of _Ancient Aliens_ episodes

                                     *Ancient Aliens* is an American documentary television series that premiered with a two-hour special on the History Channel on March 8, 2009, exploring the pseudoscientific ancient astronauts hypothesis that extraterrestrial visitors influenced early human civilizations through advanced technology and knowledge. The series features episodes that examine historical artifacts, ancient texts, monumental structures, and cultural myths as potential evidence of alien intervention, often drawing on interviews with archaeologists, historians, and proponents of the theory such as Giorgio A. Tsoukalos. As of November 2025, *Ancient Aliens* has aired 21 seasons and 277 episodes, with new content continuing to be produced weekly. The list of episodes organizes this extensive catalog chronologically by season, providing details on titles, air dates, and synopses that highlight the show's focus on connecting modern UFO phenomena to ancient events. Notable recurring themes include the construction of pyramids, the origins of religious deities, and unexplained technological advancements in antiquity, all posited as legacies of extraterrestrial contact.
## Series overview

### Premise and format

*Ancient Aliens* is an American television series that explores the pseudoscientific [ancient astronauts](https://grokipedia.com/page/Ancient_astronauts) hypothesis, proposing that intelligent extraterrestrial beings visited Earth in antiquity and significantly influenced human [civilization](https://grokipedia.com/page/Civilization), [technology](https://grokipedia.com/page/Technology), and cultural development. The show's central [thesis](https://grokipedia.com/page/Thesis) posits that many ancient achievements, such as monumental architecture and advanced knowledge in astronomy and engineering, cannot be fully explained by human ingenuity alone and instead suggest intervention by advanced alien visitors. This premise draws heavily from the works of authors like [Erich von Däniken](https://grokipedia.com/page/Erich_von_D%C3%A4niken), whose book *Chariots of the Gods?* popularized the idea of extraterrestrial contact shaping human history.
Episodes typically follow a documentary-style format with a runtime of approximately 42 minutes, excluding commercials, structured around narrated segments that pose provocative questions about historical mysteries. The narrative is interspersed with interviews from recurring experts, including ancient astronaut theorists such as [Giorgio A. Tsoukalos](https://grokipedia.com/page/Giorgio_A._Tsoukalos) and [Erich von Däniken](https://grokipedia.com/page/Erich_von_D%C3%A4niken), who provide speculative interpretations of archaeological evidence. Reenactments dramatize proposed alien encounters or ancient events, while on-location footage from sites like [Egypt](https://grokipedia.com/page/Egypt) or [Peru](https://grokipedia.com/page/Peru) visually supports the discussions, often culminating in open-ended conclusions that encourage viewers to consider the possibilities of extraterrestrial involvement.
Recurring themes throughout the series focus on interpreting ancient structures, such as the Egyptian pyramids and [Stonehenge](https://grokipedia.com/page/Stonehenge), along with global myths, religious texts, and anomalous artifacts, as potential evidence of alien technology or guidance. For instance, episodes frequently examine how these elements might reflect extraterrestrial engineering or [knowledge transfer](https://grokipedia.com/page/Knowledge_transfer) to early humans. The show's tone is pseudohistorical and conspiracy-oriented, blending factual historical context with unverified speculation, and it is presented primarily for entertainment rather than scientific validation, as evidenced by its emphasis on "what if" scenarios over empirical proof.
### Production and broadcast history

*Ancient Aliens* originated as a two-hour documentary special titled "Chariots, Gods & Beyond," which premiered on the [History Channel](https://grokipedia.com/page/History_Channel) on March 8, 2009. Created by television producer [Kevin Burns](https://grokipedia.com/page/Kevin_Burns), the special was produced by his company, Prometheus Entertainment, and explored the [ancient astronauts](https://grokipedia.com/page/Ancient_astronauts) hypothesis through interviews and archival footage.
The success of the special led to the commissioning of a full series, which debuted on [April 20](https://grokipedia.com/page/April_20), 2010, with Season 1 consisting of 5 episodes. Narrated by [Robert Clotworthy](https://grokipedia.com/page/Robert_Clotworthy) throughout its run, the series features recurring experts such as [Giorgio A. Tsoukalos](https://grokipedia.com/page/Giorgio_A._Tsoukalos), a Swiss-born writer and television presenter who serves as a principal researcher and frequent on-screen contributor, often credited with popularizing the "ancient aliens" meme; and David Childress, an American author and publisher specializing in ancient mysteries and unexplained phenomena, who appears regularly to discuss anomalous artifacts and structures. Other key recurring figures include [Erich von Däniken](https://grokipedia.com/page/Erich_von_D%C3%A4niken), the Swiss author whose 1968 book *Chariots of the Gods?* inspired the show's premise, and a rotating panel of archaeologists, historians, and scientists who provide contrasting viewpoints.
Production evolved from the initial limited order to a consistent annual season format, with episodes typically airing in blocks of 8 to 16 per season. Seasons 4 through 7 (2012–2014) primarily aired on the H2 network, a companion channel to [History](https://grokipedia.com/page/History), while later seasons returned to the flagship channel; international distribution has been handled by A&E Networks, reaching audiences in over 100 countries.
By November 2025, *Ancient Aliens* has aired 279 episodes across 21 seasons, marking it as one of History Channel's longest-running original series. The debut season averaged around 2 million viewers per episode, contributing to strong ratings that prompted ongoing renewals despite occasional shifts in scheduling. Season 21, which premiered on [February 7](https://grokipedia.com/page/February_7), 2025, remains in production, with episodes airing weekly through November 2025, resulting in incomplete episode listings as of this date.

### Season 1 (2009–10)

Season 1 of *Ancient Aliens* premiered on the [History Channel](https://grokipedia.com/page/History_Channel) on April 20, 2010, marking the television debut of the series as a regular program following a 2009 pilot special. The season consisted of five two-hour episodes that laid the groundwork for the ancient astronaut theory, drawing inspiration from Erich von Däniken's 1968 book *Chariots of the Gods?*, which posits that extraterrestrial visitors influenced early human civilizations through advanced technology and knowledge. Episodes explored foundational evidence such as unexplained ancient structures, mythological texts suggesting alien intervention, and archaeological anomalies like the Nazca Lines in [Peru](https://grokipedia.com/page/Peru), which some theorists argue served as landing strips for spacecraft. The season introduced core experts including archaeologist [Giorgio A. Tsoukalos](https://grokipedia.com/page/Giorgio_A._Tsoukalos), author David Childress, and theoretical physicist [Michio Kaku](https://grokipedia.com/page/Michio_Kaku), who appeared recurrently to discuss these themes. The premiere episode achieved record viewership for a new nonfiction series on the network, attracting 1.682 million total viewers.
The episodes were produced by Prometheus Entertainment and directed primarily by Jayden Taylor, with writing credits to John Schueller and others, emphasizing speculative connections between ancient myths and potential extraterrestrial contact. Each installment featured interviews, reenactments, and analysis of artifacts from Sumerian, Egyptian, and South American cultures to argue that gods in ancient lore may have been aliens. Viewer engagement was strong throughout, averaging over 1.5 million per episode, contributing to the series' early success and renewal.

**Episode Synopses**
In "Aliens and the Old West," theorists propose that UFO sightings and mysterious artifacts in 19th-century American folklore, such as petroglyphs depicting star people, indicate extraterrestrial contact with Native Americans and settlers during frontier expansion.
"Aliens and Monsters" explores ancient legends of creatures like the Thunderbird and [Chupacabra](https://grokipedia.com/page/Chupacabra) as possible misinterpretations of alien spacecraft or bio-engineered beings, suggesting extraterrestrials used monstrous forms to interact with early humans.
The episode "Aliens and Sacred Places" argues that global holy sites, including [Machu Picchu](https://grokipedia.com/page/Machu_Picchu) and the Vatican, were constructed as landing zones or energy portals for alien visitors, aligning with geomagnetic anomalies.

In "Aliens and Mysterious Rituals," ancient ceremonies involving psychedelics and sacrifices are theorized as methods to summon or communicate with off-world entities, evidenced by cave art and shamanic traditions worldwide.
"Aliens and the Lost Ark" suggests the biblical [Ark of the Covenant](https://grokipedia.com/page/Ark_of_the_Covenant) was an alien device capable of immense power, used to guide the [Israelites](https://grokipedia.com/page/Israelites) and cause disasters like floods as warnings against disobedience.
"Aliens and the Secret Spaceships of Ezekiel" interprets the prophet Ezekiel's vision of spinning wheels and fiery chariots as a detailed UFO encounter, implying ancient [Hebrews](https://grokipedia.com/page/Hebrews) received technological blueprints from extraterrestrials.
The episode "Aliens and the Dead Sea Scrolls" claims these ancient manuscripts encode [extraterrestrial](https://grokipedia.com/page/Extraterrestrial_life) knowledge about human origins and impending cataclysms, hidden by the [Essenes](https://grokipedia.com/page/Essenes) to preserve alien revelations.
"Aliens and the Great Pyramid" examines the pyramid's construction as alien-engineered, potentially serving as a device to stabilize Earth's pole shifts and prevent global disasters.
"Ghosts in the Machine" theorizes that ancient automatons and oracles, like the Greek [Antikythera mechanism](https://grokipedia.com/page/Antikythera_mechanism), were powered by alien energy sources, mimicking spirits to influence human behavior.
In "The Monoliths," massive stone structures such as [Easter Island](https://grokipedia.com/page/Easter_Island) statues are viewed as alien markers or warning systems about environmental shifts, aligned with celestial events.
"The [Shiva](https://grokipedia.com/page/Shiva) Covenant" links Hindu epics describing atomic blasts and flying machines to ancient nuclear wars initiated or halted by extraterrestrials, serving as cautionary tales against technological misuse.
"Aliens and Deadly Cults" investigates how modern and ancient cults, including those involving mass suicides, may stem from misinterpreted alien contacts promising [salvation](https://grokipedia.com/page/Salvation) from apocalyptic events.
"Aliens and the Undead" connects zombie-like myths and plagues to possible alien experiments or biological warnings, drawing parallels to biblical resurrections as extraterrestrial revivals.
"Aliens, Gods & Heroes" portrays mythological figures like [Zeus](https://grokipedia.com/page/Zeus) and [Gilgamesh](https://grokipedia.com/page/Gilgamesh) as alien astronauts who imparted knowledge but also foretold disasters like floods as divine (extraterrestrial) judgments.
Finally, "Aliens and the Creation of Man" proposes that [human evolution](https://grokipedia.com/page/Human_evolution) was accelerated by alien genetic intervention, with biblical floods representing a reset after failed experiments or as warnings of future cataclysms.
