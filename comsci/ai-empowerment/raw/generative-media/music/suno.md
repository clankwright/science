## Vendor / access

Suno Inc. (Cambridge MA, founded 2022). Web app at suno.com, mobile apps. Discord onboarding deprecated.

## Models / timeline

- **v1, v2** (2023): research-grade, choppy audio.
- **v3** (Mar 2024): the inflection point. First model that produced full songs (vocals + instrumentation, 2 min) that sounded like real music to a casual listener.
- **v3.5** (May 2024): 4 min songs, better structure.
- **v4** (Nov 2024): cleaner vocal stems, better mixing, lyric understanding.
- **v4.5** (May 2025): up to 8 min initial generation, 1200+ genre tags, vocal expressiveness.
- **v5** (Sep 2025): current default.
- **v5.5** (Mar 2026): Voices (sing in your own cloned voice), Custom Models (fine-tune on your own tracks), My Taste (preference learning).

## Pricing

- **Free**: 50 credits/day, no commercial rights, v4.5 only.
- **Pro**: $8/mo annual ($10 monthly), 2500 credits/mo (~500 songs), commercial rights, v5+.
- **Premier**: $24/mo annual ($30 monthly), 10,000 credits (~2000 songs), Studio features (stems separation, advanced editing).

Per-song cost: $0.012-0.016 on paid plans.

## Distinctive trait

The leader in song-form quality. Output is recognizable as a "song" with verses, choruses, bridges, real vocals (not just instrumental). Strong on pop, rock, country, hip-hop. Lyric input lets users supply their own words; AI handles melody and arrangement.

## Limits and lawsuits

RIAA lawsuit filed Jun 24 2024 (Universal, Sony, Warner) alleging Suno trained on copyrighted recordings. Suno later effectively admitted training on copyrighted music under fair-use defense. Settlement status as of Apr 2026: Sony case proceeding, expected pivotal ruling summer 2026. UMG settled with Udio (not Suno) Oct 2025; Warner separately licensed Udio. Suno remains in active litigation.

Practical effect for users: songs generated may inadvertently echo copyrighted melodies; commercial use carries some unresolved legal risk.

## Maturity

Production for short songs and demos; near-production for full album tracks. Vocals still occasionally have artifacts on held notes or rapid lyrics.

## Individual empowerment

A YouTuber generates royalty-free background music in their preferred genre for $0.02/song instead of paying $20-50/track on Epidemic Sound. A hobbyist songwriter who can't sing or play an instrument writes lyrics, hears them sung, and iterates in minutes. A small podcast produces custom intro/outro music in seconds.
