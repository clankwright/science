## Vendor / access

OpenAI. First demoed Feb 2024 (research preview, no public access). Public release Dec 9 2024 to ChatGPT Plus and Pro subscribers in US/Canada. Sora 2 launched in 2025 (date varies by source; ~Sep 2025).

## Pricing / tiers

- **ChatGPT Plus** ($20/mo): up to 50 priority videos at 720p, 5 sec each.
- **ChatGPT Pro** ($200/mo): up to 500 priority videos, 1080p, longer durations, no watermark.
- **Sora 2** (current): API access with per-second pricing comparable to Veo 3 ($0.10-0.40/sec depending on quality).

## Distinctive trait

Sora 1 was the model that broke the "Will Smith eating spaghetti" narrative on physics realism in early 2024. By the public Dec 2024 release it was already arguably behind Kling and Runway Gen-3 for quality, but the integration with ChatGPT (no separate app, prompt with familiar GPT) reduced friction more than competitors. Sora 2 closed the quality gap and introduced sound generation comparable to Veo 3.

The Sora app (iOS, late 2025) added a TikTok-like feed of user-generated AI clips, briefly the most-downloaded app in late 2025.

## Maturity

Production for short clips (5-20 sec). Long-form (1+ min coherent narrative) still leading-edge.

## Limits

- No public API for Sora 1 ever; Sora 2 API constrained.
- Geographic restrictions: not available in EU/UK at launch.
- Watermarking on Plus tier.

## Individual empowerment

A YouTuber generates B-roll for $0.40-2.00/clip instead of licensing stock footage at $50-200/clip. A storyboard artist drafts shot ideas in seconds. A small business produces a 30-second product ad without a videographer.
