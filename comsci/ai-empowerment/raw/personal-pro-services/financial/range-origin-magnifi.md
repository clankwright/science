# Financial / wealth: Range, Origin, Magnifi

The wealth-management AI space split in 2024-2026 into three tiers by customer wealth and price point: HNW (Range), mass-market subscription (Origin), and DIY-investor copilot (Magnifi). Plus advisor-facing infra (wealth.com) covered separately.

## Range
- Vendor: Range Finance. Series C $60M (Nov 2025).
- Audience: high-income earners — explicit floor "if you make over $200k." Targets execs, doctors, tech, entrepreneurs.
- Model: flat-fee membership, *zero* AUM fee. Big differentiator vs traditional advisors who take 0.5-2% AUM annually.
- Tiers (annual): Premium $2,950 / Platinum $5,950 / Titanium $9,950. No hidden fees.
- AUA: $9.5B across 5,000+ HNW members across all 50 states; $400M directly under management.
- AI: "Rai" — built-in AI wealth advisor. Member can ask anything (equity comp, tax, investment, planning) on-demand without waiting for a meeting with the human advisor. Human advisors remain in the loop for execution and complex planning.
- Empowerment angle: previously, equivalent service was a private-banker / family-office relationship at 1%+ of AUM, gated to ~$5M+ liquid net worth. Range opens the same playbook to ~$200k income earners with a few hundred thousand in assets.

## Origin
- Vendor: Origin (useorigin.com).
- Audience: mass-market consumer.
- Pricing: $12.99/mo or $99/yr (with $1 first-year promo at launch).
- Features: budgeting, investment tracking, financial planning projections, estate planning, tax filing, optional CPA connection.
- AI Advisor (Oct 2025 launch per Kitces): the first SEC-regulated AI financial advisor. Personalized advice grounded in the user's linked financial data. Marketing claim: "outsmarts every leading AI model on the CFP exam."
- Distinctive trait: the SEC-regulation status is the moat. Means it can give actual advice (not just "education") within the regulated framework. Origin has 10x'd users in the year leading up to that launch.

## Magnifi (TIFIN)
- Vendor: Magnifi, owned by TIFIN.
- Audience: DIY retail investor (think: someone who would otherwise be on r/investing or running their own portfolio).
- Pricing: $14/mo or $132/yr ($11/mo equivalent). Managed portfolios add 0.23% AUM. Free tier for browsing + commission-free trading.
- Features: conversational search across 15,000+ stocks/ETFs/mutual funds; multi-account aggregation (link brokerage accounts for unified view); portfolio recommendations against your stated goals/risk; commission-free trading.
- $500M+ in self-directed user assets linked to the platform.
- Distinctive trait: search-native investing — "show me low-fee ETFs that exclude tobacco" type natural-language queries that pre-LLM tools couldn't handle. Doesn't try to replace your judgment; supplements it.

## Pre-AI baseline
- A consumer wanting personalized financial planning could:
  - Hire a CFP at 1-2% AUM (gated to ~$250k+ assets), or $1,000-$3,000+ for a one-shot plan.
  - Use a robo-advisor (Betterment, Wealthfront) — cheap but generic, no real "advice."
  - Read Bogleheads and figure it out alone.
- 2026: Range collapses the HNW advisor cost; Origin makes CFP-grade planning available at $99/yr; Magnifi gives retail investors a conversational research tool that previously required a Bloomberg terminal.

## Out of scope: advisor-facing infra
- wealth.com (Ester Intelligence): estate + tax planning for advisors at the largest broker-dealers. Sold B2B; consumer doesn't see it directly.
- Robo-advisors (Betterment, Wealthfront, Schwab Intelligent) added LLM chat layers in 2024-2025. Doesn't really change the value prop — they still allocate based on the same portfolio models.

## Caveats
- "AI gives investment advice" is regulated speech. SEC-registered (Origin) vs unregistered ("educational only") is the line.
- Liability: if you follow AI advice and lose money, no recourse against the AI vendor. Range and Origin are fiduciaries (with documented duty); ChatGPT is not.
- Hallucination on specific securities (e.g. fabricated ticker symbols) and on historical performance claims is well-documented. Treat as a research tool, not a source of truth.
