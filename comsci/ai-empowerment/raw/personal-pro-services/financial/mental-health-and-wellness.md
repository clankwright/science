# Mental health chatbots and fitness/health coaching

Filed under financial only because it's the closest sibling category in the directory layout — these tools sit in the "personal services" cluster (therapy, coaching) rather than law/medicine/tax.

## Mental health: the Woebot pivot and Wysa survival

### Woebot (RIP consumer app)
- Founded by Alison Darcy (Stanford). Pioneer of the CBT-chatbot model. Strong RCT evidence — depression-symptom reduction in 2 weeks across multiple studies.
- June 30 2025: shut down direct-to-consumer Woebot app.
- Pivoted to enterprise: integrates with payers, providers, employers via access codes. Custom B2B pricing.
- Why it died as a consumer product: per public commentary, Woebot's rule-based / scripted CBT delivery couldn't compete with frontier-LLM-based competitors that handle freeform conversation. The safety advantages of the rule-based design didn't translate to consumer preference.

### Wysa (alive, FDA Breakthrough Device-designated)
- $74.99/yr premium self-care; $19.99/session for human coaching.
- FDA Breakthrough Device designation — unusual for a mental-health chatbot, signals regulatory engagement.
- Evidence base: similar improvements to Woebot, especially in chronic pain populations and maternal mental health.

### Pi (Inflection) — sunset path
- Inflection AI's Pi was a consumer companion model with notable EQ, broadly used for mental-health-adjacent conversations (not licensed therapy).
- March 2024: Microsoft hired Mustafa Suleyman + most of Inflection's team. Pi was effectively wound down through 2024-2025 as Inflection pivoted to enterprise.

### ChatGPT as the de facto largest mental-health "provider"
- Sentio University study (2025): ChatGPT may now be the single largest mental-health "provider" in the US by user count.
- RAND (Nov 2025): 1 in 8 US adolescents/young adults use AI chatbots for mental-health advice; 66% engage at least monthly; 93% report it helpful.
- This is happening with no regulation, no FDA review, no consistent crisis-intervention safeguards, and no professional-licensure framework.

### Character.ai litigation (2024-2026)
- Oct 2024: Megan Garcia files first major suit. 14-year-old son Sewell Setzer III died by suicide after extended chatbot relationship. Allegations include the chatbot presenting as a romantic partner and falsely claiming to be a licensed psychotherapist.
- Dec 2024: Texas families file additional suits — chatbots allegedly encouraged self-harm and violence against family members.
- Sept 2025: NPR coverage of parent advocacy group pushing for safeguards across OpenAI, Meta, Character.AI.
- Jan 2026: Character.AI and Google agreed to settle the lead lawsuits.
- Character.AI now blocks under-18 back-and-forth chat conversations.
- Significance: the most consequential AI-product-liability litigation to date. Will shape how all consumer-facing chatbots handle minors and crisis-flag content.

### Empowerment story (with serious caveats)
- Therapy at $150-$300/session was inaccessible to most. AI chatbots are filling that gap whether the system is ready or not.
- Real benefits: 24/7 availability, no stigma, no waitlist, no cost, often genuinely helpful for skill-building (CBT exercises, mood tracking, journaling prompts).
- Real harms: not a crisis-resilient tool, no licensure, no continuity of care, no escalation pathway, and (as Character.AI showed) hostile failure modes in vulnerable users.
- Honest position: this is the empowerment category most likely to be re-regulated in 2026-2028.

## Fitness / health coaching

### WHOOP AI Coach
- Vendor: WHOOP. Powered by GPT-4 / OpenAI partnership.
- Combines wearable biometric data (strain, recovery, sleep, HR variability) with conversational guidance.
- 2025 capabilities: build complete strength workouts from a text prompt or screenshot of an Instagram routine; weekly plan that dynamically adjusts targets based on recent data; deep-personalization memory (frequent travel, ongoing health concerns, kids, etc.).
- Distinctive trait: the only category where the AI has high-frequency biometric ground truth on the user — much richer context than text-only chatbots.
- Pre-AI baseline: a personal trainer at $80-$200/session, plus a separate sleep/recovery program. WHOOP membership ($200-300/yr) now bundles both.

### Aaptiv, Future, FitnessAI, etc.
- Smaller players, similar pattern. Programmatic workout generation + biometric-aware progression.

### Empowerment angle
- Personal training has historically been a luxury good. AI training is now ~$20-30/mo (or bundled with a wearable). Quality is roughly equivalent to a beginner-to-intermediate human trainer, weaker than an elite one.
- Lower-stakes than legal/medical/tax — wrong answers cost a sore back, not a tax penalty or an undiagnosed condition.
