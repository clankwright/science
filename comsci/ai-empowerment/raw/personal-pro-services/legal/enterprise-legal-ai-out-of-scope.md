# Enterprise legal AI — out of scope notes

These are the headline names in the legal-AI conversation but they are *not* consumer-empowerment tools. Flagged here so the wiki page can correctly exclude them.

## Harvey AI — out of scope (enterprise only)
- Vendor: Harvey AI. Series E ($11B valuation, 2025/2026).
- Pricing (estimated; no public list price): ~$1,200/seat/month, 20-seat minimum, 12-month commitment. Annual minimum spend ~$288k.
- Customer base: Am Law 100, Fortune 500 legal departments, Allen & Overy, PwC, etc.
- June 2025: LexisNexis integration — Harvey became the first generative-AI platform with full access to one of the two "must-have" US legal libraries (Lexis). Bundle pushes all-in costs ~33% higher.
- Why out of scope: not just expensive — the sales motion, deployment, and authentication assume a law firm with IT and compliance staff. An individual cannot buy Harvey.

## CoCounsel (Thomson Reuters / formerly Casetext) — mostly out of scope
- Vendor: Thomson Reuters (acquired Casetext Aug 2023).
- Original CoCounsel launched March 2023 as the first AI legal assistant on GPT-4.
- Aug 2025: launched CoCounsel Legal — agentic workflows, Deep Research mode (creates and executes a research plan, returns cited report), bundled with Westlaw and Practical Law.
- Feb 2026: 1M users across 107 countries.
- TR's proprietary legal LLM ("Thomson") slated for summer 2026.
- Why mostly out of scope: priced and sold to firms, not consumers. Some solo-practitioner pricing exists but is still ~$100s/month and gated to bar members in some configurations. Useful background for the wiki because it sets the pro-grade ceiling that consumer tools are measured against.

## Spellbook — out of scope (transactional lawyers)
- Vendor: Spellbook. Lives inside Microsoft Word; reviews/redlines contracts against a firm's playbook.
- Pricing: ~$20-40/user/mo entry, ~$179/user/mo mid-tier, ~$350/user/mo enterprise (6-mo minimum).
- Audience: transactional lawyers at firms / in-house counsel. Consumer can't realistically use it (priced and pitched for legal pros).

## Eve.legal — out of scope (plaintiff law firms)
- Vendor: Eve.legal. Pitched at the plaintiff-side personal-injury firm.
- Sells custom enterprise contracts; case-lifecycle automation.
- Out of scope for individual empowerment.

## EvenUp — out of scope (plaintiff valuation/negotiation)
- Vendor: EvenUp. Series E $150M (Oct 2025) at $2B+. 200+ law firm customers; >$350M in claimed damages generated.
- Targets plaintiff PI firms; focuses on demand-package generation and damages valuation.
- Out of scope for individual empowerment, *but* the secondary effect matters: better demand packages from your PI lawyer = larger settlements for you. The empowerment is mediated through the firm, not direct.

## Net for the wiki page
- The "Big Legal AI" names of 2024-2026 (Harvey, CoCounsel, Spellbook, Eve, EvenUp) are all enterprise. None of them is what an individual uses to read their lease.
- The actual consumer legal AI is ChatGPT / Claude / Gemini — same general models as everywhere else, no specialized legal product gating, no UPL settled but no UPL-as-practiced enforcement against you-the-consumer.
- DoNotPay was the one consumer-positioned legal vendor and it was disciplined by the FTC in Feb 2025.
