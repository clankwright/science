# Mental health wearables and longitudinal stress coaching

## What it is

Wearables and devices specifically targeting autonomic-nervous-system regulation (HRV, vagal tone) and emotional state, paired with conversational AI. Distinct from therapy chatbots (Wysa, Woebot) because the input is biometric, not self-report.

## Specific unlocks

Apollo Neuro:
- Wearable that delivers patterned haptic vibrations claimed to shift autonomic state via touch receptors. Seven modes (sleep, calm, focus, etc.). Integrates with Oura Ring and Apple Health.
- 2025-2026: SmartVibes AI subscription that personalizes vibration patterns based on Oura/Apple-Health-pulled HRV and reported state.
- Hardware ~$349, AI membership recurring.

Pulsetto:
- Vagus-nerve neck-stim device, $149-269 hardware. Pitched as more direct intervention than Apollo's haptic touch model.
- App pairs sessions with self-rated stress and (optionally) wearable HRV trends to suggest session frequency.

Sensate, Moonbird (handheld breathwork pacers), and similar devices: same template. The AI layer is mostly schedule + nudge + correlate-with-HRV.

Conversational stress coaches:
- Welltory, Stress & HRV trackers, third-party Apple Health readers that produce daily "your stress was X today, here's why" narratives drawing on HRV, sleep, weather, calendar density.
- Bevel and other aggregators (see aggregators page) include stress as one signal.

Specific newly-realistic patterns:
- HRV-driven personal stress journal: the wearable detects elevated stress, the LLM asks "what's happening right now?" and over weeks builds a personalized stressor map (specific people, meeting types, foods).
- Pre/post intervention loops for breathwork, cold exposure, supplements, on actual measured HRV deltas not subjective feeling.
- Cycle-aware mental health framing (Oura's women's-health LLM extends here).

## Pre-AI baseline

A $200 therapy session per week and a paper journal. HRV biofeedback was a niche $1,000+ device with no software around it. Apollo, Pulsetto, and the AI overlays make this a $350-500 one-time + subscription tier instead.

## Cost / access

Apollo Neuro $349 + SmartVibes AI subscription. Pulsetto $149-269. Most stress chatbots/aggregators $5-15/mo.

## Maturity

Where it works: as a second-opinion/log on top of HRV from a wearable you already wear. The data is real, the conversation makes it usable.

Where it's questionable: clinical evidence for haptic touch therapy (Apollo) and vagus-nerve stim (Pulsetto consumer-grade) on stress/anxiety/depression is thin. Some small studies, no large RCTs. The mechanism-of-action stories are speculative beyond well-known autonomic effects of slow breathing.

Where the AI layer mostly is theater: telling you "your HRV dropped, try a breathing exercise" is something a $0 watch app can do. The longitudinal-personal-pattern surfacing is the real differentiator, not the in-session coach.

Overlap with therapy bots: Wysa, Limbic, Woebot, Replika - covered in the edu-therapy-email category. The wearable-driven pattern is specifically about biometric input shaping the conversation, vs pure-text therapy chat.
