# Function Health and the blood-panel-as-coachable-corpus pattern

## What it is

Function Health, Superpower, InsideTracker, Marek Health, Vitals Vault, Elo - a 2023-2026 wave of subscription blood-panel services that test 100+ biomarkers per draw and lay an AI synthesis layer on top of the results. The category-defining move is treating the blood panel not as a one-time PDF but as a longitudinal corpus you can chat with, with optimal (longevity-literature) ranges instead of conventional disease-flag ranges.

## Specific unlocks

- Have a conversation with your blood panel: "Why did my ApoB rise from January to October?" answered with reference to your dietary log, weight trend, training load, and prior values.
- Optimal-range framing: instead of "your ferritin is in the normal range (30-400)" you get "your ferritin at 38 is in the optimal range (50-150 per longevity literature) and is trending down 3 panels in a row, here's what often correlates."
- Repeat panels every 6 months at a price point that previously bought you one annual draw at most. Year-over-year deltas were the missing data layer.
- Protocol suggestions in plain language: "given your hs-CRP and homocysteine pattern, common interventions to discuss with your physician are X, Y, Z." Not prescriptions, but a starting point that previously required a $500 functional-medicine visit.
- Cross-source: Superpower and Bevel-style aggregators take blood values and merge with wearable HRV, sleep, weight, and CGM trends.

## Pre-AI baseline

The 2010s baseline: an annual physical that tested ~20 markers, a PDF emailed by a nurse, results explained in a 7-minute appointment, no follow-up. Or a $500-2,000 functional medicine appointment with a 100-marker panel and a printed protocol you read once. The longitudinal coaching layer didn't exist outside concierge-medicine ($5-25k/yr).

## Cost / access

- Function Health: $365/yr (2026 pricing) for one Annual (100+ tests) + one Mid-Year (60+ tests) panel, HSA/FSA eligible. Co-founded by Dr. Mark Hyman.
- Superpower: $199/yr, 100+ biomarkers, AI explainer + concierge doc access.
- InsideTracker: per-test pricing, focuses on athletic optimization, has had ML for years, added LLM layer 2024.
- Marek Health: telehealth-first, focused on hormone optimization and men's health, more clinician-driven than AI-driven.
- Elo: subscription with bundled supplements (the conflict-of-interest variant).
- Quest Diagnostics partnership for actual blood draws is the standard pattern.

## Maturity

Where it works: making a 100-marker panel actually readable. The "optimal range" framing is genuinely useful even if some longevity-literature thresholds are debated. Two panels per year at $365 is a price step-change.

Where it's marketing: anything called "biological age" from biomarkers is methodologically shaky - methylation-based clocks are better, and the panel-derived versions are correlative at best. AI "protocols" that suggest specific supplements in specific doses are operating at the edge of FDA dietary-supplement claim regulations. Clinician review depth varies wildly across services.

Where to be careful: AI hallucination on medical queries runs 15.6% in current benchmarks. Function and Superpower both gate clinical guidance through human reviewers, but the chat interface can still over-confidently narrate causality from correlations. Most services explicitly say "discuss with your doctor."

The category is real because the price/access change is real. Whether the AI layer adds clinical value beyond "makes the panel actually get read" is an open question with no RCT.
