# Sleep coaching: RISE, Eight Sleep, and the closed-loop bed

## What it is

Three distinct things in one category:
1. RISE Sleep: phone-based sleep-debt and circadian app, AI nudges and an "AI Expert" upsell.
2. Eight Sleep Pod 4 + Autopilot 4.0: an actual mattress cover with thermal control that closes a sense-decide-act loop overnight.
3. Wearable-driven sleep coaches (Whoop, Oura, Sleep As Android, Athlytic) that narrate but don't actuate.

## Specific unlocks

RISE Sleep:
- Sleep-debt-and-circadian framing (vs sleep-stage scoreboarding) is the underlying value proposition. AI nudges 16 science-based habits at the right time of your personal energy curve.
- "AI Expert Access" $29.99 add-on for Q&A.
- Reported 80% of users feel more energy within 5 days, 83% within a week (self-report, vendor data).
- Apple Best Apps of 2026.

Eight Sleep Pod 4 + Autopilot 4.0 (the most interesting unlock in this category):
- Predicts how you'll likely sleep based on day's data, then adjusts thermal environment in real-time, then explains what happened in plain language each morning.
- Pulls Apple Health / Health Connect data automatically (workouts, activity, manual tags like "alcohol," "late meal," "stress").
- 99% accuracy heart rate, 95% HRV, 98% breathing rate via clinical-grade sensor strip in the bed - no wearable required.
- This is the only consumer product that actually closes the loop. Whoop tells you you slept badly; Pod 4 tries to make you sleep better, observes whether it worked, updates next-night strategy.

Athlytic, Sleep As Android, Welltory: third-party HRV/sleep coaches that read HealthKit and add narration. Useful as second opinions on Whoop/Oura data.

## Pre-AI baseline

Sleep medicine appointments (months of waiting for non-CPAP issues), a $40 sleep-hygiene book, or a CBT-I therapist ($150-250/session). Thermal sleep-environment optimization didn't have a consumer product before Eight Sleep at all.

## Cost / access

RISE: $69.99/yr ($5.83/mo).
Eight Sleep Pod 4: $2,649 hardware + $17/mo Autopilot subscription.
Whoop, Oura: covered in their own pages.

## Maturity

Where it works: Eight Sleep Autopilot 4.0 is the standout because it actuates, not just narrates. RISE has unusually high reported satisfaction for a sleep app and the sleep-debt model is more grounded than stage-scoring.

Where it's questionable: stage-classification accuracy (covered in Whoop/Oura pages) means narration of "you got too little REM" is narrating a noisy estimate. Wearable sleep-stage Cohen's kappa vs PSG is fair-at-best across the category.

Where the loop matters: closing the loop (sense → adjust → re-sense) is what differentiates Pod 4 from every other sleep product. The thermal intervention is real (well-documented effect of cooler sleeping temp on slow-wave sleep) and the AI is mainly there to personalize set points and surface anomalies in plain English.

The price is the friction. $2,649 + $204/yr is concierge-medicine money, not consumer money.
