# CGM and metabolic AI coaching

## What it is

In 2024 the FDA cleared two over-the-counter CGMs for non-diabetic adults: Dexcom Stelo (March 2024) and Abbott Lingo (September 2024). For the first time you could buy a CGM at Amazon without a prescription, with companion apps that include AI-driven glucose-pattern coaching. This collapses what was previously a Levels Health / Nutrisense subscription middleman ($199-400/mo) into an $84-89/mo direct-to-consumer hardware product.

## Specific unlocks

Stelo (Dexcom):
- 15-day wear (longest of any Dexcom product), GenAI insights launched Dec 2024, AI photo meal logging July 2025, Clarity Clinic data sharing with a clinician.
- "Ask your glucose" pattern: snap a meal, see the resulting curve, then chat about why this carbs-then-walk combination produced a 30 mg/dL spike instead of 60.

Lingo (Abbott):
- 14-day wear, in-app coaching, "Lingo Count" gamified glucose-stability score for behavior change.
- Cheaper ($84/mo).

Levels Health (subscription overlay over Stelo/Libre):
- $24/mo or $288/yr base plan; $199/mo Complete plan with unlimited dietitian coaching via Season Health partnership (June 2025), comprehensive blood testing, AI-powered insights.
- Repositioned in 2025 as a full metabolic platform, not just CGM scoring, since CGMs are now commoditized.

Nutrisense: similar dietitian + CGM model, longer-running.

Specific newly-realistic moves:
- Personal n=1 metabolic experiments without a prescription. "Does adding 10 g protein before this meal flatten my response?" Test it across 14 days with a $89 sensor and an LLM looking at the curves.
- Pre/post intervention comparison for time-restricted eating, walking after meals, sleep-debt vs glucose response - all without needing a research lab.
- Continuous loop: meal photo → predicted curve from prior personal data → actual curve → AI explainer.

## Pre-AI baseline

Pre-2024: prescription-only CGM (gatekept by physician), or expensive subscription overlay ($199-400/mo for Levels/Nutrisense), or no continuous data at all. Dietitian-mediated metabolic interpretation cost $150-300/session.

## Cost / access

Stelo $89/mo or 2-pack $99 from Amazon. Lingo $84/mo from Amazon, pharmacies, direct. Levels $24-199/mo on top of CGM cost. Nutrisense $225-400/mo.

## Maturity

Where it works: the hardware is mature (Dexcom/Abbott have been in T1D/T2D for a decade, accuracy is medical-grade), the AI overlay on top is real and conversational. For diet experimentation, the unlock is genuine.

Where it's marketing: clinical evidence that non-diabetic CGM use changes long-term outcomes is thin. Glucose variability in healthy adults is mostly normal physiology, and chasing flat curves can drive disordered-eating patterns. The supposed "metabolic age" or "metabolic score" outputs are not validated against hard endpoints.

Where it's risky: AI overinterpretation of normal variability ("your spike to 145 means insulin resistance is starting") is the failure mode. Levels in particular has been criticized for blurring this line in marketing.
