# Image and Face Search: PimEyes, FaceCheck.ID, Yandex

## What it is

Mass-market reverse face-search engines that index hundreds of millions to billions of public web images. A user uploads a single photo of a face and the service returns visually similar faces and the URLs where they appear. Distinct from Clearview AI, which sells exclusively to law enforcement and was the subject of a $51.75M class settlement in March 2025. PimEyes and FaceCheck.ID sell directly to consumers.

## Specific unlocks

- Verify a Tinder match's identity by uploading their profile photo to FaceCheck.ID; if the same face appears on three other dating profiles under different names plus a romance-scam-warning forum, the date is off.
- Find an estranged sibling's current Instagram from a 1990s school photo. PimEyes works on aged faces tolerably well.
- Identify a scammer who posed as a Western soldier in a romance-fraud message: paste the photo into FaceCheck.ID, get back the original Russian-language fitness influencer's account whose photos were stolen.
- Adoptee searches: cross-reference a presumed birth-parent photo from an obituary against the open web for current photos.
- Cold-case help: the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children and various civilian sleuths use combinations of PimEyes, Yandex, and Google Lens to identify Jane Doe remains and missing-persons cases.
- Confirm that an academic source ("Dr. Sarah Bennett, MIT") whose paper looks suspicious is actually a stock-photo face on a synthetic researcher profile generated by a paper mill.

## Tools that matter

- **PimEyes.** Subscription model. Free tier shows blurred thumbnails; Open Plus at $15.99/mo for 25 unblurred searches/day; PROtect at $29.99/mo adds opt-out monitoring; Advanced tier $89.99/mo. Indexes the open web; declines to index social-media platforms.
- **FaceCheck.ID.** Pay-per-search credits rather than subscription. Indexes scam and dating-fraud datasets specifically; better than PimEyes for fraud-detection use cases.
- **Yandex Images.** Free. The default OSINT first-stop for Eastern European and Russian-domain faces. FindClone (formerly SearchFace) algorithm.
- **Google Lens.** Free. Best for object and landmark recognition; weak on face matching specifically because Google deliberately throttles face search.
- **TinEye.** Free. Reverse image search by exact-or-near-duplicate; not a face engine but useful for confirming an image's earliest publication date.
- **FaceOnLive.** On-premises biometric vendor; for use cases where uploading the target face to a third party is unacceptable.

## Pre-AI baseline

Mass face search across the open web simply did not exist for civilians. Clearview AI built the capability for law enforcement starting around 2017. Yandex was the only open-web option and worked best on faces likely to appear in Russian-language sources. Identifying a face required either someone in your social network recognizing it, or paying a private investigator $200-500 for a few hours of work.

## Cost / access

- PimEyes: $15.99-89.99/month, no per-search option.
- FaceCheck.ID: per-search credits, ~$2-6 per useful search.
- Yandex / TinEye / Google Lens: free.
- FaceOnLive (on-prem): enterprise pricing.

## Maturity

Production. PimEyes and FaceCheck.ID are reliable consumer products with clear failure modes (low-quality input photos, partial faces, drastic age gaps). Yandex is production but quirky. None of them indexes major social media platforms (Facebook, Instagram, TikTok), which the platforms themselves block via legal and technical means; results come from blogs, news sites, dating profiles, scam forums, fitness sites, adult content, and personal portfolios.

Where it breaks: deepfake and AI-generated face contamination in indexes is rising; misidentifications are confident-looking and can ruin lives if acted on without confirmation.

## Risk shape

Every legitimate use case has a stalker mirror. PimEyes faced criminal complaints in Germany and the EU in 2022-2024. FaceCheck.ID has been used to dox sex workers, identify protestors, and harass internet users. Tinder rolled out mandatory facial liveness verification in California (2025) and US-wide (Oct 2025), partly in response to fraud surge documented by Barclays (romance scams up 20% YoY in Q1 2025); the same biometric infrastructure becomes the surveillance scaffolding it was meant to defeat.
