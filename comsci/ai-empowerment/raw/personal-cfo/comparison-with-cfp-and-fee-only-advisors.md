# Comparison with human CFP and fee-only advisors

## What is actually being compared

The baseline against which AI personal-CFO tools are pitched: a fee-only Certified Financial Planner relationship. Two structural variants matter:

- **Retainer model**: $2,000-$5,000/yr ongoing, holistic relationship. Common in XY Planning Network (XYPN), Garrett Planning Network, NAPFA fee-only firms.
- **Hourly / project model**: $150-$300/hr (median), 3-hour minimum typical. Used for one-shot plans or limited-scope engagements.

Neither model includes investment management; that's separate (often AUM-based 0.5-1%, sometimes flat-fee).

## What a $2-5k CFP actually does

The work that justifies the price tag:

1. **Comprehensive plan**: 50-100 page document covering cash flow, tax, retirement, education, insurance, estate, equity comp.
2. **Ongoing tactical advice**: between-meeting calls/emails for life events.
3. **Coordination with other professionals**: CPA, attorney, insurance broker.
4. **Behavior coaching**: the soft-skill work that keeps clients invested in March drawdowns.
5. **Implementation support**: actually opening accounts, executing trades, filing paperwork.
6. **Fiduciary accountability**: documented responsibility for advice quality.
7. **Edge-case judgment**: knowing when something is unusual enough to warrant deeper investigation.

## What AI does and doesn't replace

| Function | AI handles | Human CFP still needed |
|---|---|---|
| Comprehensive plan document | Yes (Origin, Boldin, Range AI) | Edge cases over $1-2M net worth |
| Standard retirement projection | Yes (Boldin, ProjectionLab) | No |
| Roth conversion modeling | Yes (Boldin Roth Explorer, Range) | Complex multi-account/state cases |
| Tax-strategy basics | Yes (Origin, TaxGPT, Keeper) | K-1, multi-state, international |
| Estate documents (simple) | Yes (FreeWill, Trust & Will) | Taxable estate, blended family, business |
| Cash-flow analysis | Yes (Monarch, Copilot, Origin) | No |
| Insurance analysis | Partial (DIY w/ LLM) | Disability, complex life, business |
| Behavior coaching | No | Yes |
| Implementation execution | Partial (account opening flows) | Coordinated multi-step execution |
| Coordination across CPA/attorney | No | Yes |
| Fiduciary accountability | Origin and Range only | Yes for everyone else |
| Edge-case detection | Weak | Strong |

## The XY Planning Network angle

XYPN is the dominant network for fee-only CFPs serving Gen X / Gen Y / millennials at flat-fee or monthly-subscription pricing rather than AUM-based. The pitch was always "comprehensive financial planning at a transparent monthly fee." Typical XYPN advisor: $200-$400/month subscription, no AUM minimum, full holistic planning.

Where this leaves XYPN advisors as of 2026:

- Their differentiation vs AI is shrinking on the modeling/calculation side. Boldin AI + Origin replicates a lot of standard XYPN deliverables.
- Their differentiation is growing on the soft-skill, coordination, behavior, and edge-case sides.
- The XYPN response is "advice-only firms" that explicitly skip implementation and focus on advice + accountability + behavior. This is the part AI can't do.

Garrett Planning Network (hourly fee-only) is in a similar position: hourly advice on specific questions remains valuable for edge cases AI gets wrong, and for users who want a human to be accountable for the recommendation.

## The decision framework

Roughly:

- Net worth < $250k, simple W-2 income: Origin ($99/yr) covers most needs. ChatGPT/Claude DIY for edge questions.
- Net worth $250k-$1M, moderate complexity: Origin + Boldin ($220/yr combined) or one XYPN advisor ($2,400-$3,600/yr) for behavior + accountability.
- Net worth $1M-$5M, equity comp, multi-state: Range Premium ($2,950) is the natural fit, or a XYPN advisor + Boldin + ChatGPT for cross-checking.
- Net worth $5M+, business interests, K-1s, trusts: human CFP + CPA + estate attorney team. AI as augmentation, not replacement.

## What XYPN/Garrett advisors are doing in response

Anecdotally as of 2026:

- Adopting AI tools internally (eMoney, MoneyGuidePro, Wealth.com Ester) to be more efficient.
- Repositioning around accountability, behavior, and judgment.
- Moving toward higher-touch, smaller-client practices.
- Some advisors are using ChatGPT/Claude to draft client deliverables faster.
- A few are moving toward "advice-only" (no implementation, no AUM) to focus on the human-judgment differentiator.

## Honest take

The category structure is settling into:

- **DIY tier** ($0-$250/yr): aggregator + LLM + retirement modeler.
- **Subscription AI advisor tier** ($99-$200/yr): Origin, Boldin combos.
- **Hybrid AI + human tier** ($2,950-$9,950/yr): Range, Facet.
- **Pure human CFP tier** ($2,400-$5,000/yr ongoing or hourly): XYPN, Garrett, NAPFA.
- **Wirehouse / private banker** (1%+ AUM, $250k+ minimum): Merrill, Morgan Stanley, etc.

The pure-AI tier and the hybrid tier are both growing fast and taking share from the wirehouse / 1% AUM tier (which was always overpriced for what it delivered to the mass-affluent customer). The XYPN/Garrett tier is repositioning around the parts AI can't do; how successfully is the open question for the next 2-3 years.
