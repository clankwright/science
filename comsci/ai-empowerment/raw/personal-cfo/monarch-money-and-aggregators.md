# Monarch Money, Copilot Money, Empower: AI overlays on net-worth aggregators

## What they are

Three apps that started as Mint replacements (after Intuit killed Mint in 2024) and bolted on AI assistants on top of the underlying aggregator + budgeting + net-worth tracking core. None of them is a CFP replacement; all of them now present themselves as one.

## Specific unlocks

- "Ask Monarch's chatbot 'why did my net worth drop $8k last month' and get a transaction-level explanation, not a generic 'market volatility' answer."
- "Auto-categorize 18 months of imported transactions in 30 seconds instead of an evening of Excel."
- "Click a sparkle icon on any chart in Monarch and have the AI summarize what changed, why, and what factors mattered, with the date range/filters/accounts auto-passed as context."
- "Get a weekly text-message financial recap (Monarch) instead of opening the app."

## Monarch Money

- Pricing: ~$15/mo or $99/yr.
- AI features (2026 Winter Release): AI Assistant (natural-language queries against your actual data), AI Insights (sparkle-icon explainers on dashboards), Weekly Recap (auto-summary).
- Built with input from in-house CFPs to constrain answers to "real-world financial best practices."
- Other AI: auto-categorization, "Explain this transaction" on transaction details, receipt scanning (added 2026), equity tracking.
- Best for: couples and households who want shared visibility (replaced Mint's role).

## Copilot Money

- Pricing: $13/mo or ~$95/yr.
- AI Assistant: in public beta as of April 2026.
- Strengths: design, AI auto-categorization (learns your tagging over time), Apple-native (iOS, iPadOS, macOS).
- Weaknesses: iOS-only (limited web), premium price for power users who want more depth.
- The aggregator + AI categorization is the strong play; the conversational layer is still beta.

## Empower Personal Dashboard (formerly Personal Capital)

- Pricing: free dashboard. Investment advisory: 0.89% AUM (first $1M), down to 0.49% over $10M.
- Retirement Planner: Monte Carlo with 5,000 scenarios, free.
- AI: less aggressive than Monarch/Copilot. Their pitch is the aggregation + free Monte Carlo + advisor upsell.
- Distinguishing feature: Empower has been doing free net-worth aggregation since 2009 (Personal Capital era); has the largest install base and the best account-linking coverage.

## Pre-AI baseline

- Mint (free, ad-supported, killed 2024).
- Personal Capital free dashboard.
- YNAB ($109/yr) for envelope budgeting, no aggregation.
- Quicken ($60-$120/yr, desktop heritage).
- Excel + Tiller ($79/yr) for the spreadsheet-native crowd.

The pre-AI version of all three new tools was: download CSVs, classify in Excel, build pivot tables, ask your own questions. The AI overlay collapses the "ask my own questions" step into chat.

## Cost / access

- Monarch: $99/yr.
- Copilot: $95-$156/yr depending on monthly vs annual.
- Empower: free dashboard, AUM-priced advice if you want it.

All three have free trials, no signup gates beyond that.

## Maturity

- Auto-categorization and dashboard insights: production. Reliable.
- Conversational AI: production at Monarch, beta at Copilot. Both are still mostly chatbot-on-aggregator. They will tell you what your data shows; they won't tell you what to do about it the way Origin or Boldin will.

## Honest take

These are dashboard-with-a-chatbot products. The AI does narrow things well: explain a category change, summarize a week, categorize a transaction. It does not do scenario modeling (use Boldin/ProjectionLab) or hold a fiduciary planning conversation (use Origin/Range). Their value is real but bounded: they replace the manual "look at numbers, ask question, build pivot, get answer" loop.
