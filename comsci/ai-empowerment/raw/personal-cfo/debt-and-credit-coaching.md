# Debt, credit coaching, and credit card optimization

## What it is

The bottom of the personal-finance funnel: debt payoff prioritization, credit score management, credit card rewards optimization, sign-up bonus chasing. AI shows up in three forms: chatbots that nag/encourage (Cleo, Charlie), conversational debt-payoff planners, and LLMs as a research engine for the manual-but-lucrative game of credit card optimization (Doctor of Credit + ChatGPT pattern).

## Specific unlocks

- "Ask Cleo to roast your spending and get a debt-prioritization plan with the avalanche/snowball trade-off explained against your actual balances and APRs."
- "Have Claude design your next 12 months of credit card sign-ups against your travel goals, including: where you are on Chase 5/24, AmEx pop-up risk, the minimum spend you can hit organically, and the optimal opening sequence to stack bonuses."
- "Paste your credit reports from all three bureaus into Claude and have it identify dispute targets, optimal credit utilization moves, and which closed accounts to leave alone."
- "Get an AI to maintain a churning calendar that respects each issuer's family-rules (Chase 48-month, AmEx once-per-lifetime, Citi 24-month, Cap One 6-month) without manually tracking it."

## Cleo

- AI financial assistant launched 2018, leaned hard into snarky/sass voice.
- Free + paid tiers (Cleo Plus ~$6/mo, Cleo Builder ~$14.99/mo for credit-building).
- Capabilities: chat-driven budgeting, debt prioritization (consolidates balances and recommends payment order), small cash advances, credit-building secured account.
- AI value: engagement layer rather than analytical depth. Best for users who don't engage with neutral spreadsheet tools.

## Charlie (the original Catch-app version, mostly defunct as of 2024)

- Penguin chatbot that learned spending habits and suggested savings.
- Operated via SMS + web.
- Largely subsumed by Cleo and the broader chatbot personal-finance category.
- A separate "Charlie" launched April 2026 from InvestSuite, but that's an investing chatbot, not the original Charlie.

## The credit card optimization pattern (Doctor of Credit + LLM)

The credit card rewards / churning game produces real value: a disciplined household can extract $3k-$10k/yr in cashback + travel rewards through systematic sign-up bonus chasing, category bonus optimization, and merchant-coded spend routing. Pre-LLM, this required:

- Reading Doctor of Credit, Reddit r/churning, The Points Guy daily.
- Maintaining a spreadsheet of card opening dates, family rules, minimum spend windows.
- Tracking shopping portals, transfer partner sweet spots, devaluation news.

Post-LLM, the workflow compresses:

- **Strategy planning.** "Given my spend profile (groceries $1,200/mo, dining $400/mo, travel $5k/yr), my current cards (Chase Sapphire Preferred, AmEx Gold), and my goal (premium-cabin Asia trip in 18 months), recommend the next 3 cards to open in optimal sequence with rationale." LLMs handle this well.
- **Rule lookups.** "Am I eligible for the Chase Ink Business Cash if I have 4 personal Chase cards opened in the last 24 months?" LLMs can answer; verify against current Chase 5/24 status.
- **Award chart navigation.** "What's the cheapest way to fly LAX-NRT in business using my 200k Amex MR points?" LLMs do okay at this; sometimes wrong on current award chart updates.

### Where it breaks

- Rules change. Issuer pop-up rules, application family rules, minimum spend windows shift monthly. LLM training data is months stale; Doctor of Credit is updated daily.
- LLMs sometimes hallucinate award availability or transfer ratios. Always verify against the actual issuer/airline before transferring points.
- Best practice: use the LLM for strategy and structure, verify specific facts (current bonuses, rules, award charts) against primary sources.

## AmEx ChatGPT credit (a tell about market direction)

In 2026, AmEx Business Platinum and Business Gold added a $300/yr ChatGPT statement credit. That a major credit card issuer is co-marketing with OpenAI signals that the AI-as-financial-tool pattern is mainstream enough to drive card spend.

## Pre-AI baseline

- Cleo (pre-AI): pre-canned budgeting prompts.
- Doctor of Credit + manual spreadsheet for churning.
- Credit Karma free dashboard (since 2007).
- Free credit reports via annualcreditreport.com.

## Cost / access

- Cleo Plus: ~$72/yr. Cleo Builder: ~$180/yr.
- LLM subscription: $20/mo for Claude Pro or ChatGPT Plus.
- Doctor of Credit, r/churning: free.

## Maturity

- Cleo: production. Engagement is real; analytical depth is limited.
- LLM-driven credit card strategy: production for the disciplined user. The LLM is a competent research analyst; the user has to verify and execute.
- Automated churning calendar tools: still mostly DIY (Sheets + manual tracking). No dominant SaaS for this; hand-rolled scripts are common in the r/churning community.

## Specific weird unlock

The "credit card portfolio optimizer" prompt is one of the highest dollar-per-prompt LLM use cases in personal finance. A 2-hour conversation with Claude that designs an 18-month cards-and-bonuses calendar can produce $4k-$8k of redemption value. The labor-rate equivalent is genuinely high. This used to require either being deeply embedded in the churning community or paying a "miles strategist" $200-$500 for a custom plan; it's now a $20/mo Claude subscription.
