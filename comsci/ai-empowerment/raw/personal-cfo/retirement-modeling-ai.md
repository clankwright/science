# Retirement modeling AI: Boldin, ProjectionLab, Empower

## What it is

The retirement-projection space had three pre-LLM stalwarts: Empower's free Monte Carlo (ex-Personal Capital), NewRetirement (now Boldin) for serious DIY-ers, and ProjectionLab for the FIRE community. The AI overlay arrived in early 2026: conversational scenario modeling sitting on top of the established calculator engines.

## Specific unlocks

- "Run a 30-year Monte Carlo with your actual portfolio, custom withdrawal rates, custom Social Security claiming age, and a side-bet on whether your spouse's pension survives, in a 5-minute conversation instead of 2 hours of toggle-clicking."
- "Ask Boldin AI 'should I do Roth conversions for the next 5 years' and have it run the Roth Conversion Explorer against your actual scenario, return a recommended conversion ladder, and apply it to your plan with one click."
- "Convert from a manual ProjectionLab scenario builder to a conversational interface that asks the right follow-up questions: 'You said retire at 55, but your spouse is 8 years younger; should we model her retiring with you, separately, or in a coordinated path?'"
- "Generate Sankey cash-flow diagrams from your retirement plan automatically, instead of building them in Sankeymatic by hand."

## Boldin (formerly NewRetirement)

- Rebranded from NewRetirement to Boldin in 2024 to reflect non-retiree audience.
- Pricing: free Basic tier; PlannerPlus $120/yr (after 14-day trial).
- The Boldin AI Planner Assistant launched January 2026.
- All users get access; PlannerPlus gets unlimited conversations + Roth conversion modeling + Monte Carlo.
- Roth Conversion Explorer (added/expanded January 2026): runs an algorithm to recommend optimal Roth conversion strategies based on goals, tax situation, income assumptions. New strategy added Jan 29, 2026: limit conversions to "deductions only — 0% federal income tax."
- Strength: most rigorous tax modeling in the consumer space (state taxes, IRMAA, Social Security taxation, ACA subsidies, Medicare premiums).
- Weakness: UI feels dated; learning curve.

## ProjectionLab

- Pricing: free (no save), $129/yr premium, $1,199 lifetime.
- Built for FIRE community by Kyle Nolan.
- Strengths: most flexible scenario engine, beautiful UI, Sankey cash-flow diagrams, Monte Carlo with customizable distributions, historical backtesting, multi-country tax estimation.
- AI integration is lighter than Boldin's; ProjectionLab leans on its visual scenario-building UI more than chat.
- Best for: under-50 wealth-builders, FIRE-targeting households, scenario tinkerers.
- Recent reviews: some Boldin users dropping Boldin for ProjectionLab; community sentiment mixed on which is better.

## Empower Retirement Planner

- Free, no signup beyond linking accounts.
- 5,000-scenario Monte Carlo.
- Add Social Security estimates for both spouses.
- AI features: minimal vs. Boldin/ProjectionLab. Empower's pitch is the free aggregation + Monte Carlo + AUM advisory upsell, not a chat-driven planner.
- Best for: anyone who wants a free baseline projection without committing to a paid tool.

## Pre-AI baseline

- CFP retirement plan: $1,500-$5,000 one-shot.
- DIY Excel + Bogleheads: free, hours of work, error-prone.
- Quicken Lifetime Planner: $80/yr, deterministic (no Monte Carlo).
- Fidelity Retirement Planner: free for clients, decent but generic.

## Cost / access

- Boldin: $120/yr PlannerPlus (or free Basic).
- ProjectionLab: $129/yr premium or $1,199 lifetime.
- Empower: free.
- All three are sub-$150/yr for full features. The category is post-CFP-disruption already, even before AI; AI is an interface improvement.

## Maturity

- Calculation engines: production, mature, used by tens of thousands of households.
- AI conversational layer at Boldin: production as of Jan 2026, but new. Quality depends on how well the chat layer translates user questions into the underlying engine's parameters.
- ProjectionLab AI features: less developed; ProjectionLab leans on the UI.
- The category is one of the cleanest "AI improves an existing strong tool" stories in personal finance: the modeling engines were already good; chat is just a better interface.

## Specific weird unlocks

1. **Roth Conversion Explorer with deduction-floor strategy.** Boldin added a strategy that says "only convert what stays in the standard deduction window," meaning $0 federal income tax on the conversion. This is a strategy that sophisticated CPAs run by hand; Boldin productized it.
2. **Multi-country tax estimation** (ProjectionLab). For digital nomads / dual-residents / expats considering retiring abroad, modeling US + foreign tax treatment side-by-side used to be a $300/hr international tax accountant problem.
3. **Sankey cash-flow diagrams as plan output** (ProjectionLab). The "where does my money come from and go to over 40 years" visualization used to require building it in a separate diagramming tool.
4. **5,000-scenario Monte Carlo for free** (Empower). What used to require institutional software (eMoney, MoneyGuidePro, both $4k+/yr advisor licenses) is now free at consumer-tier UX.
