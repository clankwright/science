# Estate planning AI: Trust & Will, Wealth.com, FreeWill

## What it is

The estate-document space (will, revocable trust, healthcare directive, power of attorney) has been online and DIY-friendly since LegalZoom in the 2000s. The AI overlay is recent: AI walks you through interview-style document creation, summarizes existing documents, visualizes wealth transfer, and (in Wealth.com's case) acts as an "estate intelligence" layer for advisors.

## Specific unlocks

- "Get a fully drafted will + healthcare directive + power of attorney in 20 minutes for $0 (FreeWill) instead of $400-$1,500 with a local attorney."
- "Upload your existing 1995 will to Wealth.com's Ester AI, ask it 'what changes if my daughter dies before me,' and get a scenario walked through with the actual document text."
- "Visualize multi-generation wealth transfer flows as Sankey diagrams from your actual estate documents, instead of squinting at a flowchart drawn in a lawyer's office."
- "30% of Americans now trust AI advice over a human attorney for estate planning (Trust & Will 2026 Estate Planning Report), up from 20% in 2025; among Gen Z that figure is 46%."

## The 2026 estate-AI landscape

### Trust & Will
- Consumer-facing online estate platform.
- Wills from $199, trusts from $599 (one-time fees), or membership tiers with attorney support.
- Series C funding announced in 2025 specifically to fund AI advancement.
- AI features: document review, scenario explanation, conversational interview.
- 2026 Estate Planning Report (their own data): 56% of Americans still have no estate plan.

### Wealth.com (Ester AI)
- Advisor-facing, B2B (advisors use it with their clients).
- Series B of $65M in April 2026.
- 664% YoY growth in 2025.
- Ester is the AI layer: extracts and summarizes documents, runs deterministic calculations (1,000+ per estate distribution per their disclosure), maps roles and relationships, visualizes wealth transfer.
- Processed 100k+ estate documents in 2025.
- Used by 1,000+ advisory firms; T3/Inside Information's 2026 Software Report says Wealth.com leads market share among purpose-built estate platforms.
- Consumers don't sign up directly; access through their advisor.

### FreeWill
- Truly free. Funded by 2,300+ nonprofit partners that benefit from charitable bequests.
- Wills, trusts, advance directives, all free, unlimited revisions.
- Q&A interview format (less AI-conversational, more wizard).
- ~20 minutes typical to complete a will.
- Best-positioned for the simple-estate case (no business interests, single jurisdiction, no blended family complications).

## Pre-AI baseline

- Estate attorney: $400-$1,500 simple will, $2k-$5k revocable trust package.
- LegalZoom / Rocket Lawyer: $89-$179 for a will, attorney chat upsell.
- Online templates (Nolo): $40-$60.
- Doing nothing: ~56% of Americans (Trust & Will 2026).

## Cost / access

- FreeWill: $0.
- Trust & Will: $199 (will) to $599+ (trust); $39/yr maintenance.
- Wealth.com: not direct-to-consumer; routed through your advisor.

## Maturity

- Document generation: production. Online wills have been valid (when properly executed) for 15+ years.
- AI document review and explanation: production at Wealth.com (advisor-mediated), beta at consumer products.
- AI scenario modeling on wealth transfer: production at Wealth.com.

## Honest limits

- Online wills are valid in most states for simple cases. They are not valid for: complex family structures, taxable estates over the federal exemption ($13.99M single / $27.98M couple in 2025, slated to drop in 2026 absent extension), blended families with stepchildren conflicts, business interests with succession requirements, special-needs beneficiaries.
- An attorney is still load-bearing for: anything taxable estate, anything where state-specific quirks matter (e.g., community property states), anything with potential will contests.
- Per Trust & Will's 2026 report, only 5% of Americans are willing to use AI for estate documents *without* attorney review. The trust is in AI-assisted, not AI-replaced.
- Notarization, witness signing, and proper execution still must happen physically (with state exceptions for online notarization).

## What's actually weird here

Wealth.com's Ester reads existing estate documents and explains them. The legal-document interpretation problem (parsing "per stirpes," contingent beneficiaries, exoneration clauses, no-contest provisions) used to require an attorney to read your existing will and tell you what it would do in scenario X. AI now does this in seconds, with the document text right there for verification. This is one of the highest-leverage estate-AI unlocks because it works in the case where you already have a will but don't really understand what it says.
