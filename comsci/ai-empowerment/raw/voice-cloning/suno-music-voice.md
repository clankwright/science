# Suno (music with voice cloning overlap)

Primarily a music-generation product, but as of v5.5 (March 2026) Suno includes personal voice cloning that overlaps with the voice-synthesis category. Mentioned here for completeness; deeper coverage in a separate music-generation capability page.

## Vendor / access
- Suno Inc. (US, Cambridge MA, founded 2022). https://suno.com
- Web app + mobile.

## Voice features (v5.5, March 2026)
- **Voices.** Record or upload audio of yourself singing; Suno extracts a vocal identity and uses it in generated tracks.
- Pro/Premier subscribers only ($10+/mo).
- Limited to 3 custom-model fine-tune slots per user.
- Verification layer: Suno requires the user prove they are the voice owner.
- Voices private by default — only the account holder can use a captured voice.

## Other versions
- v4 (mid-2024): 4-minute songs.
- v4.5 (May 2025): 8-minute songs, 1,200+ genre tags.
- v5: 44.1 kHz audio, richer instrumentation.
- v5.5 (March 2026): voice cloning, custom models, personalization.

## Pricing
- Free: 50 daily credits (~10 songs), v4.5-all access, no commercial rights.
- Pro: $8/mo annual or $10/mo monthly. 2,500 credits/mo (~500 songs), commercial rights, voice cloning, stem editing, custom model fine-tuning.
- Premier: higher tier with more credits and slots.

## Company state (Feb 2026)
- 2 million paid subscribers.
- $300M ARR.
- $250M Series C closed Nov 2025 at $2.45B valuation.

## Distinctive trait (vs the rest of this list)
Music + vocal identity together. Other voice products do speaking voice; Suno does *singing* voice. The verification + private-by-default model is a notable safety design choice — explicitly different from ElevenLabs' more permissive approach.

## What an individual unlocks
- Generate songs in your own singing voice without learning to sing well
- Custom vocal style for game soundtracks / personal projects
- Demo songs in your voice to send to producers / collaborators

## Limits
- Singing voice cloning much harder than speech; quality varies wildly.
- Lyric / melody coherence still imperfect on 5+ minute tracks.
- Ongoing legal disputes with major labels (RIAA filed suit June 2024); some training data provenance contested.
- Commercial rights only on paid tiers — free tier output cannot be monetized.
