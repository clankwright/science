# Video Dubbing: HeyGen, Rask, ElevenLabs Dubbing Studio

The killer app for solo creators to globalize content. Take a video in English, get back the same video — same speaker's voice, lip-synced — in 30+ languages.

## HeyGen

- Vendor: HeyGen (US/SG). https://www.heygen.com
- **AI Video Translator** product — translate + voice-clone + lip-sync video into 175+ languages.
- Originally an AI avatar / talking-head company; pivoted to lead the dubbing space in 2024.
- Pricing: Free (3 videos/mo, 3 min max), Creator $24/mo, Pro $99/mo, Enterprise custom.
- Maturity: production. Most-used dubbing tool by YouTubers in 2024-2025 (notable: MrBeast launched HeyGen-dubbed channels in Spanish, Portuguese, French, etc., reportedly partnering directly with HeyGen).
- Distinctive trait: lip-sync quality is the best in market; voice cloning preserves speaker identity reasonably well across languages.

## Rask AI

- Vendor: Rask. https://www.rask.ai
- 130+ languages with voice cloning preserving emotional authenticity.
- Positioned for short-form / social-media creators.
- Pricing: per-minute model with team workspace; mid-range cost.
- Maturity: production. Good for batch processing many short videos.
- Distinctive trait: workflow features for marketing teams processing dozens of clips/week. Snappier UI than HeyGen.

## ElevenLabs Dubbing Studio

- Bundled into ElevenLabs platform (covered in elevenlabs.md).
- 30+ languages, voice cloning included.
- Best when you're already in the ElevenLabs ecosystem; less polished video pipeline than HeyGen.

## Pinch (newer entrant)

- API-first dubbing service. Competes on developer ergonomics.
- Per-minute pricing.

## What an individual unlocks

- A solo YouTuber going from 1 language to 30 in an afternoon
- A course creator selling localized versions of the same video without re-recording
- A news / podcast organization republishing content for global distribution
- A small business doing localized video ads in markets they'd never have entered

## Pre-AI baseline

- Hire dubbing studio: $50-200 per finished minute, weeks of turnaround.
- Subtitles only (cheap but loses retention; data shows dubbed video keeps 2-3x watch time vs subtitled).
- Re-record yourself in target language: requires fluency.

## Limits

- Lip-sync is uncanny-valley on extreme camera angles or fast cuts.
- Cultural translation is literal — jokes / idioms / cultural references often don't carry.
- Cloned voice in target language can sound like the speaker's English with a foreign accent rather than a native speaker.
- Long-tail languages (Vietnamese, Tamil, Swahili) noticeably weaker than top 10 European/Asian languages.
- Consent: you can dub *anyone's* video unless platforms enforce. Watermarking weak.
