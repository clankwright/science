# AI Dungeon, Friends & Fables, and the LLM-driven solo TTRPG

## What it is

The category of "type a few sentences, get an open-ended interactive story / RPG run by an LLM." Distinct from a pure chat — these platforms have persistence, character sheets, dice rolls, and (in some cases) maps and combat.

The 2026 lineup:

- **AI Dungeon (Latitude)** — the OG (2019). Latest models (April 2026): Atlas (DeepSeek 3.2 base, cache-efficient, with a summary system for long-context memory), Raven (GLM 4.6 base), Wayfarer Small 2 (in-house finetune for combat, harsh worlds). Mythic tier subscription unlocks 32K+ context. Parent company Latitude launched **Voyage** in April 2026: full worlds with deterministic systems, persistence, and challenges (open beta later 2026).
- **Friends & Fables** (fables.gg) — markets itself as "the world's first generative TTRPG." Built on D&D 5e SRD. AI GM "Franz" narrates, adjudicates rules, runs combat with interactive battle maps and minis. World-builder generators for NPCs, monsters, items, spells, classes, races, maps. Solo or shared sessions.
- **MacerAI** — D&D 5e core, expanded into sci-fi and horror "Featured Adventures" with AI-generated full voice narration, AI illustrations, ambient music, tactical battle maps, auto-journaling.
- **RoleForge** — newer (free alpha as of 2026). Differentiator: deterministic rules engine + persistent NPC memory, hand-drawn maps with fog of war. Supports D&D 5e and Basic Fantasy.
- **NovelAI** — narrative-first, less rules-bound, strong for collaborative fiction.

## Specific unlocks
- "Run a 30-hour solo D&D campaign across a weekend, with a GM that remembers your NPC betrayed you in session 2."
- "No scheduling four adults; play TTRPG when you have the energy at 11pm on a Tuesday."
- "Convert your homebrew setting (custom races, spells, monsters) into a playable campaign in an evening, no DM."
- "Voice-narrated, illustrated, scored adventure on your phone during a flight."
- "Try out a class concept in an actual playable session before committing to a real-table campaign."

## Where the wheels still come off

Long-context coherence is the recurring failure mode. Atlas's "summary system" is the 2026 attempt to fix it — keep a rolling LLM-maintained summary so a 200K-token campaign can still remember what happened in chapter 1. It works partially. Combat balancing is variable. Plot agency: AI GMs tend to follow your suggestions too eagerly, so player-driven challenge requires self-discipline. Rules adjudication is approximate even on D&D 5e (the most-trained ruleset).

## Pre-AI baseline

Solo TTRPG meant a stack of solo-play oracle books (Mythic GM Emulator, Ironsworn) and lots of journaling. Group play meant scheduling 4-6 adults — the dominant reason campaigns die.

## Cost / access

AI Dungeon: free tier, paid tiers $10-30/mo, Mythic tier higher. Friends & Fables: free tier, paid tiers ~$10-25/mo. MacerAI: subscription model. RoleForge: free alpha. NovelAI: $10-25/mo.

## Maturity

Production for solo and casual co-op. Not a replacement for a good human GM but a real category for people who can't get a table together.
