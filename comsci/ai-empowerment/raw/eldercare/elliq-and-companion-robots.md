# ElliQ and companion robots

## What it is

ElliQ is a tabletop social robot from Intuition Robotics (Israeli, founded 2016, launched US consumer 2022). Looks like a small lamp with an articulated head and a separate touchscreen. Speaks unprompted ("good morning, want to do a stretch?"), holds short conversations, plays music, leads cognitive games, suggests calls to family, schedules medication reminders, reads news, and runs proactively even when the user hasn't initiated.

Two design choices distinguish it from "Alexa for old people":

1. **Proactive engagement**: it talks first, every day, multiple times. Does not wait for a wake word. Speech recognition tuned for older voices and slower cadence.
2. **Not a person**: it deliberately presents as a robot, not a humanoid character. Reduces the deception/manipulation concern (somewhat).

## Specific unlocks

- New York State Office for the Aging deployed ElliQ to ~900+ seniors. Reported 95% reduction in self-reported loneliness for users engaging 30+ days, 94% reporting improved overall wellbeing, average ~60 user-initiated interactions/day. Free to recipients (state-funded). This is the largest population-scale rollout of any companion AI to date.
- Catch a depression spiral early: ElliQ logs interaction patterns. A sudden drop in voluntary engagement gets flagged to a human care manager. Same for medication-reminder no-acks.
- Florida and New Jersey followed NY with state-funded distribution programs in 2024-2025.
- Get an 84-year-old who refused to use a smartphone or computer to do guided breathing, take a walk, video-call a grandchild, all initiated by the device, with no app to learn.
- Reduce caregiver phone burden: an adult child who used to call mom 2x/day "to keep her connected" can let the device handle the social-contact baseline and use calls for substantive things.

## Pre-AI baseline

Required: paid adult day-care or senior center attendance ($75-$150/day, not always available, transportation problem), a paid companion ($25-$35/hr in-home), a family member visiting daily, or — the actual default for solo-aging seniors — nothing, and severe loneliness. Loneliness in older adults is associated with all-cause mortality at effect sizes comparable to smoking 15 cigarettes/day (Holt-Lunstad meta-analyses).

## Cost / access

ElliQ retail: $599 device + $59.99/month subscription. State-distributed in NY/FL/NJ for free to qualifying seniors. Some Medicare Advantage plans piloting reimbursement in 2025-2026. AARP AgeTech Collaborative member.

## Maturity

Production. The NY rollout is the strongest population-level evidence for any AI eldercare intervention. Academic publication (PMC, 2024) "ElliQ, an AI-Driven Social Robot to Alleviate Loneliness: Progress and Lessons Learned" lays out the evidence base honestly: not RCT, observational, self-reported, but at scale and consistent.

Caveats:
- "Loneliness reduction" is self-report on UCLA Loneliness Scale; selection bias likely (people who used the device 30+ days are people who liked it).
- Long-term retention numbers less publicized.
- The deception question: even with explicit "I am a robot" framing, mild-to-moderate cognitive impairment users may form attachments that complicate eventual device removal or vendor failure.

Other companion robots largely failed to achieve this scale: Pepper (SoftBank) was a demo product with no real elder traction; Paro (the seal) is real and still used in dementia care, but is a tactile soothing toy, not a conversational AI; Mabu (Catalia Health) pivoted away from elder care.
