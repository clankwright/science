# Vision impairment overlap with eldercare

## What it is

Age-related macular degeneration, diabetic retinopathy, glaucoma, and cataracts together leave roughly 1 in 4 adults over 75 with significant vision loss. The AI vision-assistance category was built for the blind community but its biggest growth user base is now older adults losing vision who would never identify as "blind."

Players:

- **Be My Eyes**: free app. Originally a volunteer marketplace (sighted volunteers describe a video feed in real time). Since GPT-4V integration as "Be My AI" (2023, expanded 2024-2026): an LLM does the description instantly, no human required. Now multilingual, available 24/7.
- **Microsoft Seeing AI**: free iOS/Android. Reads text, describes scenes, recognizes friends' faces (user-trained), identifies currency, reads barcodes.
- **OrCam MyEye**: $4,500 wearable that clips to glasses, runs entirely offline, reads text, recognizes pre-trained faces and announces them, identifies barcoded products, identifies currency. Notable update: OrCam announced in 2024-2025 it is winding down the visually-impaired reader product, laying off staff. Existing devices still supported but the future is uncertain.
- **Envision Glasses** (on Google Glass Enterprise hardware): $3,500. Similar feature set, hands-free.
- **Vision Buddy**: head-mounted display + camera, magnification + voice, lower price.
- **Apple iPhone Magnifier app + VoiceOver Image Descriptions**: free, built-in. Increasingly capable in iOS 18+.
- **Aira**: paid live-agent service ($30-200/month), human professional describes via smart-glass camera.

## Specific unlocks

- An 82-year-old with macular degeneration takes a photo of her medication bottle and the LLM reads the label aloud — including the small-print "do not take with grapefruit" warning that even her caregiver sometimes misses. Pre-AI required either a paid sighted assistant or a magnifier she could no longer focus.
- Be My AI describes a paper letter from the IRS in plain language, including which paragraph is the actual deadline. Vital for solo agers handling their own paperwork.
- OrCam recognizes her granddaughter walking up at the grocery store and whispers "Sarah, ten feet ahead" so she can wave first instead of looking past her.
- iPhone Magnifier "people detection" + "door detection" tells a vision-impaired elder when a person is approaching them in a hallway and how far away the next door is. Unblocks navigation in unfamiliar buildings (clinics, airports).
- Currency identification: an elder pays the right amount at the cab without trusting the driver to take the right bill.
- LLM-described family photo: "this looks like a photo of you and three other people at a beach, you appear to be wearing a red dress, the youngest person looks about 8 years old." Meaningful for someone losing the ability to enjoy the photo album.

## Pre-AI baseline

Required: a sighted family member or aide present, a magnifying glass that no longer worked once central vision was gone, large-print everything (often unavailable for personal mail), a CCTV-style desktop magnifier ($2-3k that didn't help with mobile tasks), or audiobooks/screen readers requiring substantial setup.

## Cost / access

- Be My Eyes / Be My AI: free.
- Seeing AI: free.
- iPhone built-in: free with iPhone (~$429+ for iPhone SE).
- OrCam MyEye: $4,500 one-time, no subscription. May be HSA/FSA eligible. Some state vocational rehab programs cover.
- Envision Glasses: $3,500.
- Aira: $30-200/month.

The free tier (Be My AI + Seeing AI on a phone the elder may already own) is the biggest unlock. The premium hardware tier is shrinking as smartphone AI catches up.

## Maturity

Production. Be My AI and Seeing AI are mature, work well, are widely deployed. The 2026 trend: standalone hardware (OrCam-style) is losing to smartphone AI. The cross-cutting story matters because the vision-loss elder is exactly the user who *cannot* read a smartphone screen — voice-first AI assistance is the only interface that works.

Worth noting: the LLM-vision pipeline is so much cheaper than dedicated hardware that price-equity is dramatically better than 5 years ago. A free Be My AI session does what a $4,500 OrCam did in 2018.
