## Trade Ideas (Holly)

The longest-running AI signal product in retail (since 2003; AI rebrand "Holly" ~2019). Pricing: $108/month (Standard), $228/month (Premium).

### What Holly does

Overnight, runs 70+ algorithmic strategies against the prior day's tape. Each morning, surfaces 3–5 "trade ideas" with entry, stop, target. User executes manually or auto-routes to a connected broker (Interactive Brokers, E*TRADE).

### Holly's actual track record

Trade Ideas publishes Holly's daily picks and aggregate annual return. Reviewers note (a) the published returns are unaudited and use specific position-sizing assumptions, (b) the variance is high (drawdowns of 20%+ in bad years), (c) the picks are time-decay sensitive (entry within minutes of open or the edge erodes).

For a day-trader with the discipline to execute exactly what Holly says, exactly when, the product can plausibly be profitable. For the median subscriber who picks-and-chooses ("I'll skip that one, looks risky") the return is closer to "expensive entertainment."

## Tickeron

Pattern-recognition focused. 34 advertised "AI stock trading systems" plus "AI Robots" that scan thousands of charts per minute for chart patterns (head-and-shoulders, breakouts, etc.). Subscriptions $30–$300/month tiered by feature.

Hedge-fund-style "AI model portfolios" with audited annualized returns (Tickeron's own claim). Several Tickeron robots are advertised as having beaten the S&P 500 in calendar 2024 by significant margins; details on backtesting methodology and out-of-sample period are mixed.

### Reception

Among 2026 retail trading reviews, Tickeron rates well for chart-pattern alerting (the AI is reasonably good at finding the patterns) and middling for actionable trading edge (pattern recognition does not equal predictive return).

## Kavout (Kai Score)

Quantitative platform with a proprietary "Kai Score" derived from a neural net over fundamentals, price/volume, sentiment, and alternative data (the "satellite imagery of retail parking lots" claim is real but a tiny part of the model). Aimed at more sophisticated users than Trade Ideas.

Pricing tiers $20–$200+/month. Less mass-market than Trade Ideas or Tickeron; more positioned as a quant data product than a "trading bot."

### Where Kavout is honest

The Kai Score is a ranking, not a guarantee. Kavout's marketing is comparatively restrained (less "beat the market while you sleep" rhetoric, more "factor in additional signal"). Better suited as one input to an investment process, not the entire process.

## Why these are mostly not what they look like

- **All three publish their winning strategies; none publish aggregate user P&L.** Survivorship bias is structural to the category.
- **Subscription costs erode realistic edge.** $108–$300/month plus broker fees plus slippage means a $50K trading account needs to earn 3–10% above the index just to net out. That's a high bar for any retail strategy.
- **Backtest periods.** Many of the published "beats S&P by X%" claims are in narrow windows (2023 bull rally; 2024 mega-cap concentration). Bull-market backtests are ubiquitous in this category.
- **Day-trading the signals incurs short-term cap-gains tax** (top-marginal 37% federal in US plus state). The same return in a buy-and-hold ETF is taxed at long-term rates (15–20%). Tax-equivalent return must clear a much higher hurdle.

## What the LLM era added

Almost nothing in this subcategory. Trade Ideas, Tickeron, Kavout are predominantly classical ML / pattern recognition products that predate the LLM boom. The "AI" in their marketing is mostly the same models they had in 2022. New 2025–2026 features in each product:

- Trade Ideas: chat interface to ask Holly "why this pick?"
- Tickeron: natural-language strategy description.
- Kavout: AI summary of the factors driving a Kai Score.

These are interpretability features, not new sources of alpha. The core signal generation has not been transformed by LLMs.

## How this category fits the wiki thesis

This is the clearest example in the trading section of "feels-empowering, isn't." The retail user can, for $200/month, access tools that look like a Bloomberg terminal lite. The actual financial outcome is not improved over an index fund for the typical user. The only durable use is for already-skilled active traders for whom these are one input of many; in which case the user is also the kind of person who could justify the $200/month from their other edges.

For the unhobbling thesis: there is no individual capability newly unlocked here. A 2018 Trade Ideas subscriber and a 2026 Trade Ideas subscriber have substantially the same product.

## How the marketing rotates

The persistent feature of this subcategory is the marketing churn. Each year, one or two AI-trading-bot products are heavily promoted on YouTube, Twitter (X), and finance-podcast sponsorship as "the AI that beats the market." The track record so far:

- 2019–2021: Trade Ideas Holly. Real product, durable; marketing has settled to honest promotion.
- 2021–2022: Tickeron AI Robots. Surge of Reddit interest during the meme-stock era.
- 2022–2023: Multiple crypto AI bots (Cryptohopper, 3Commas, Pionex). Bull-market backtests sold heavily; many users lost in the 2022 crash.
- 2023–2024: ChatGPT-based stock pickers (PortfolioPilot, Magnifi). Conversational UX as the differentiator.
- 2024–2025: Kavout, Danelfin, several quantum-flavored entrants.
- 2025–2026: Composer's Trade With AI, eToro Agent Portfolios, "vibe trading" framing.

Each cycle, the surviving products from the prior cycle get less marketing oxygen but remain in the market. The pattern suggests the category is real (there is durable demand for AI-augmented trading tools) but the alpha they deliver is not durable — each cycle's winners have to find a fresh angle to market because the prior angle stopped beating the index in some recent year.

## Honest product use

For an individual who already trades actively and has ruled out indexing as their primary strategy, the screener / signal products do offer genuine value as one input:

- **Daily watchlist generation**: Trade Ideas Holly's morning picks, Tickeron's overnight pattern alerts, are useful starting points for further research. They save the time of running 70 screens manually.
- **Idea triage**: Kavout's Kai Score on a specific name is a useful sanity check ("is this a high-quality company by the numbers?") even if the user doesn't blindly follow the score.
- **Education**: paying $108/month for Trade Ideas for 3 months is a way to learn what professional-grade scanning looks like.

The mistake to avoid: treating the AI's output as a complete trading system rather than as one signal among many. The marketing of this category encourages exactly that mistake; the honest use case requires the user to do the integration work the marketing implies the AI has already done.
