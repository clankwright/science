## What it is

Voice-first AI math tutor for kids 5-11 covering K-5 curriculum. The pedagogical bet: teach via concrete manipulatives (number lines, blocks, word problems) rather than symbol manipulation. The AI talks; the kid talks back. No text-only mode for younger users.

$119/year. iPad-primary; some web access.

## Maturity

Production since 2023, refreshed through 2024-2025. ~9K App Store reviews, 4.7 average. Synthesis pivoted: the original Synthesis School (live cohort game-based learning) is now de-emphasized in favor of the AI tutor product.

## Capability story

Pre-AI: a 7-year-old struggling with subtraction needed either a parent willing to teach with manipulatives (most are not trained), Kumon at $150-200/mo, or a tutor.

Synthesis Tutor delivers voice-mode Socratic instruction with the same-problem-multiple-representations approach that good elementary teachers use. Voice is load-bearing: kids who cannot yet read fluently can still learn math from it. Whisper-class STT plus TTS makes this work where text-only would not.

## Limits

- Trustpilot reviews flag billing/cancellation friction.
- Curriculum depth: above-average kids reportedly clear available content in weeks, not the full year promised.
- Methodology: AI-only, no human in the loop. Critics argue good for practice, not first-time instruction.
- Requires parent in the room; no parent dashboard rich enough to leave a 6-year-old alone with it for an hour.

## Individual-empowerment angle

A parent who is not themselves comfortable teaching math gets a competent pedagogical partner for ~$10/mo. The voice modality is the unhobbling for pre-literate or dyslexic kids. Closes the most-stark elementary-math gap (manipulative-based instruction) for households without a teacher-parent.
