# Evidence base for AI tutoring (2024-2025 meta-analyses)

**Captured:** 2026-05-03
**Sources:**
- https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/jcal.70117?af=R (Ma et al. 2025, generative AI on learning outcomes)
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12078640/ (systematic review of AI ITS in K-12)
- https://www.nature.com/articles/s41539-025-00320-7 (npj Science of Learning, 2025)
- https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/01443410.2025.2553639 (AI vs human feedback meta-analysis)
- https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2025.1691414/full (blended learning)
- https://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/EJ1465704.pdf (ERIC meta-analysis)

## Headline numbers

- Ma et al. (2025) meta-analysis: overall effect of generative AI on learning achievement is medium positive, g~0.50 in blended-learning settings.
- Some cited individual studies report effect sizes as high as 0.8, but heterogeneity is large and publication bias likely.
- AI feedback vs. human feedback (41 studies, n=4813): no statistically significant difference in learning performance. AI is at least as good as human-generated written feedback for the studied tasks.
- K-12 ITS systematic review (28 studies, npj Science of Learning 2025): effects are positive but smaller when compared against non-AI ITS, suggesting much of the gain is from the tutoring structure, not the AI specifically.

## The novelty-decay finding

Critical caveat: in long-term interventions (1 semester to 1 year), the AI effect size shrinks to negligible (g=0.08 in one analysis). Plausible mechanisms:
- Novelty effect: students engage hard for the first weeks, then disengage.
- Ceiling: once foundational gaps are closed, marginal AI tutoring helps less.
- Substitution: students start using the AI to short-circuit hard work rather than to learn.

This matches the qualitative reports on Khanmigo and Synthesis Tutor: kids initially love the AI, then return to baseline engagement.

## What the evidence does NOT show

- No RCT yet of frontier-model (GPT-4o, Claude 3.5+) Socratic tutoring at scale over a full school year.
- Most studies are short (4-12 weeks), small (n<200), and use within-system metrics (course completion, problem accuracy) rather than transfer measures (standardized tests, retention 6 months later).
- Effect of voice-mode tutoring (Synthesis, Duolingo Video Call) is essentially unstudied.

## Implication for the wiki

AI tutoring is real and beats no tutoring. It is roughly equivalent to good non-AI ITS. It is not yet shown to match a skilled human 1:1 tutor (the Bloom 2-sigma target). The honest framing: AI tutoring is a 0.5 SD improvement over self-study, available to anyone with $4-20/mo, where 1:1 human tutoring would be 1.5-2 SD at $40-100/hr.
