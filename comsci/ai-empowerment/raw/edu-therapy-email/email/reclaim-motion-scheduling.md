## Reclaim AI

Layer over Google Calendar (and Outlook, since Aug 2025). Auto-schedules:
- Tasks (priority-aware insertion into free slots).
- Focus time (auto-defended deep-work blocks).
- Habits (recurring routines that flex around meetings).
- Smart meetings (best-time-for-attendees scheduling).
- Buffer time (auto-inserted breaks between meetings).
- No-meeting days.
- 1:1s that auto-find the next mutually free slot.

Recent 2025 features: Outlook support (Aug), Focus Time intelligence (May), custom-branded scheduling links (Aug).

Free tier exists; paid plans $8-18/mo. Acquired by Dropbox in early 2025; product roadmap continued independently.

## Motion

More aggressive than Reclaim: a project-management-plus-calendar product. AI auto-schedules tasks based on deadlines, priorities, and dependencies. Re-shuffles your calendar daily as new tasks land or meetings shift.

2025 additions: "AI Employees." Alfred (Executive Assistant: meeting briefings in 16 seconds), Suki (Marketing Associate: drafts blog posts and social content). These straddle the personal-productivity / autonomous-agent boundary.

$19-34/mo. Outlook supported as of 2025.

## Capability story

Pre-AI: a person with an EA had calendar protection, focus-time scheduling, and proactive rebooking when conflicts arose. Below the EA tier, you tetris your own calendar manually and lose deep work to meeting creep.

Reclaim/Motion deliver continuous re-optimization: every time you add a task or get a meeting invite, the calendar reflows. The unhobbling is constraint-solver-driven scheduling on top of existing calendar APIs, presented in a way non-technical users can configure (rules: "Tuesdays after 3pm are no-meetings").

## Limits

- Both products struggle when calendars span teams that do not share availability data.
- Motion's "AI Employees" branding overstates current capability; reviewers find Alfred meeting briefings useful but Suki's content output mediocre.
- Re-shuffle behavior can be jarring: a meeting you mentally locked into a 10am slot may move to 2pm overnight.
- Reclaim's free tier is generous; Motion's price is steep for individual users without project-management need.

## Combined with email AI

The natural pairing: Superhuman or Shortwave drafts the meeting-confirmation email; Reclaim/Motion picks the slot; Calendar fires the invite. End-to-end, this collapses the "schedule a meeting" workflow from 5-10 emails to one user instruction.

## Individual-empowerment angle

A solo professional gets EA-grade scheduling for $10-30/mo. The savings compound when multiple parties use scheduling-link tools (no email back-and-forth). Most empowering for: founders, freelancers, doctors, lawyers, anyone whose calendar is contested by external parties.
