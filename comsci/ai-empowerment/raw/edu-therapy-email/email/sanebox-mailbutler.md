## SaneBox

Pre-LLM (founded 2010) email triage service that bolted ML classification onto your existing email (Gmail, Outlook, iCloud, IMAP). Sorts inbound mail into folders: SaneLater (low-priority), SaneNews (newsletters), SaneBlackHole (auto-mute senders), with daily digest.

Distinguishing trait: works with any email client; you never change your software. Pricing ~$7-36/mo by tier.

In the LLM era SaneBox added smart-summary and AI-reply features but remains primarily a classifier-plus-folder system. Marketing claim: 3-4 hours/week saved.

Reasonable choice for users who want triage without switching email clients.

## Mailbutler

Browser/desktop extension overlay for Apple Mail, Gmail, Outlook. Adds tracking, scheduling, follow-up reminders, templates, snippets, task creation. AI features include "Smart Compose" and "Smart Respond" using a connected LLM.

Less aggressive on auto-triage than SaneBox; more focused on per-message productivity tooling. Often paired with SaneBox by users who want both layers.

## Where they sit in the category

These are the lightweight middle ground: more than just Gmail's built-in Smart Compose, less than a full client switch to Superhuman or Shortwave. For users who are not ready to abandon Apple Mail or Outlook, they are the practical entry point.

## Limits

- SaneBox classification errors are notable in the first weeks; requires training feedback.
- Mailbutler's AI features are acceptable but not class-leading; Superhuman and Shortwave have stronger draft generation.
- Both are subscription-on-top-of-email-already; total monthly cost adds up.

## Individual-empowerment angle

A user with 2K+ unread emails and no time to migrate clients can install SaneBox in 10 minutes and have a triaged inbox by the next morning. The lowest-friction path to email-AI value, even if not the highest-ceiling option.
