Superhuman started as a power-user email client (keyboard-shortcut-driven, $30/mo, Gmail/Outlook). The 2024-2025 AI features:

- **Auto Drafts**: writes follow-up replies in your voice without prompting; appears in the compose window ready to edit.
- **Auto Labels**: classifies inbound mail (response needed, waiting on, meeting, marketing, cold pitch).
- **AI Summarize, AI Reply, Instant Reply**: triggered manually for any thread.
- **CRM integrations**: HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive (sales-team play).

July 2025 acquired by Grammarly for ~$825M. October 29, 2025 Grammarly's parent company rebranded itself to "Superhuman." The Superhuman Suite now bundles email, Coda docs, Grammarly writing, and a "Superhuman Go" agentic assistant for $33-40/mo.

Founded by ex-Google Inbox engineers (Andrew Lee). Gmail/Workspace only. Spiritual successor to Inbox by Gmail. Faster shipping cadence than Superhuman; 96 features in 2024.

Distinguishing AI features:
- **Ghostwriter**: drafts replies in your style trained on your sent mail.
- **AI summaries**: per-thread, automatically.
- **Bundles**: groups related conversations for batch processing.
- **Splits**: divides inbox into multiple focused tabs.
- **Natural-language scheduling**: "schedule a 30-min meeting with Alex next Tuesday" creates the calendar event with Meet link.
- **Delivery scheduling**: receive newsletters only in the evening, etc.

Pricing tiered; AI features sit in mid and pro tiers. Less expensive than Superhuman.

## Capability story

Pre-AI baseline: an executive paid an executive assistant $50-80K/yr to triage email, draft replies in voice, and protect calendar. Below the EA tier, professionals just lived with inbox bankruptcy.

The 2024-2025 unhobbling: voice-trained drafts (RLHF on the user's own sent mail) plus same-tab agentic action (mark as done, schedule, archive) brings 30-60 minutes/day back. Reviewers consistently report 1-2 hours/week saved at the low end, several hours/week for heavy email users.

## Limits

- Voice mimicry is good but not perfect; replies still need human review for tone and accuracy.
- Auto-classification mistakes (flagging important mail as marketing) require user-trust-building and ongoing correction.
- Privacy: both products read all your mail with LLMs hosted on third-party infrastructure. Enterprise-skeptical organizations push back.
- Cost: Superhuman at $33-40/mo prices out casual users. Shortwave is more accessible.

## Individual-empowerment angle

A solo professional (consultant, founder, doctor in private practice) who would never hire an EA gets EA-grade triage and drafting for $20-40/mo. Reclaimed time goes into deep work or off-work life. The category is most useful for people in roles where email volume is the bottleneck.
