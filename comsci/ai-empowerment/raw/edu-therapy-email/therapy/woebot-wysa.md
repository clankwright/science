## What they are

Rule-based + ML CBT (cognitive behavioral therapy) chatbots. They walk users through structured exercises: thought records, cognitive restructuring, behavioral activation. NOT free-form LLMs; both Woebot and Wysa deliberately constrain dialog to scripted CBT modules with NLP for understanding user input.

- **Woebot Health** (US): founded 2017 by Stanford psychologist Alison Darcy. Pursued FDA Breakthrough Device designation for postpartum depression. As of 2024 the consumer app was discontinued; Woebot pivoted to B2B (health systems) and ended its standalone consumer offering. Important: this is a commercial setback even though the science was sound.
- **Wysa** (UK/India): broader functionality (sleep, anxiety, work stress, chronic pain). UK NHS-deployed at scale. Subscription consumer app plus enterprise.

## Evidence (this is the strongest in the category)

- Fitzpatrick et al. 2017 (foundational Woebot RCT, n=70 college students): Woebot group reduced PHQ-9 depression scores significantly vs. information-only control over 2 weeks (F=6.47; P=.01). Anxiety reduced in both groups.
- Wysa: a 2-week dose-response study found users engaging twice/week reported >5-point PHQ-9 reductions, clinically meaningful.
- 2024 systematic review: Woebot has the highest methodological rigor in the category (multiple RCTs). Wysa and Youper studies have larger samples but weaker controls.
- 2025 JMIR review concludes the chatbots "show notable potential" but evidence base remains thin compared to in-person CBT.

## Capability story

Pre-AI baseline: getting CBT in the US meant 8-20 weeks of $150-250/session therapy, or self-help books that most people abandon. UK NHS waitlists for talk therapy were 6-18 months in 2023.

Woebot/Wysa deliver a structured CBT-lite at $0-15/mo or via NHS for free. The "AI" component is modest; the unhobbling is mostly a usable mobile interface to evidence-based exercises that previously sat in workbooks.

## Limits

- These are NOT free-form therapy. They cannot handle suicidal ideation beyond redirecting to crisis lines.
- Effect sizes are smaller than in-person CBT.
- Engagement decay: most users churn within weeks.
- The Woebot consumer shutdown (2024) shows that even validated tools struggle commercially without insurance reimbursement.
- They are NOT covered by HIPAA the same way a human therapist's notes are; data practices vary.

## Individual-empowerment angle

A person without insurance, on an NHS waitlist, or in a country with effectively no mental-health workforce gets structured CBT delivered via phone. For mild-to-moderate depression and anxiety, evidence supports it as better than nothing and often comparable to bibliotherapy. This is the most-evidence-based therapy-AI category and the least hyped.
