# Transkribus

> **Summary:** Handwritten text recognition (HTR) platform with public models for major historical hands and the ability to train your own model on a 25-page tagged sample. The default tool for serious genealogy and amateur paleography work outside the FamilySearch corpus.

**Sources:** [[raw/investigation-osint-genealogy/transkribus-and-handwriting.md]]

## What it does

Upload scanned manuscript images, run a public model (English secretary hand, German kurrent, Polski Bizon for Polish, VILNISH for Lithuanian, L'Dor V'Dor for Yiddish/Hebrew), get text back. Can train custom models on a tagged 25-page sample for individual scribal hands.

## Access and cost

Free tier with monthly credits sufficient for hobby use. Paid tiers for higher throughput. Cooperative ownership model (READ-COOP SCE).

## Distinctive trait

Public model library covers many of the hands FamilySearch doesn't index. Training a custom model on an individual scribe is the killer feature: a researcher working through one priest's parish-register entries trains once, then transcribes the rest of the volume automatically.

## Limits

- 25-page training sample requires manual transcription of those pages first; not a free unlock.
- Quality varies dramatically by hand; some 18th-century court hands still require hand correction.
- eScriptorium (free, open-source) and Kraken (open) are alternatives with steeper setup but no per-page costs.
