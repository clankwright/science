# FamilySearch Full-Text Search

> **Summary:** Free full-text search over roughly 2 billion previously-unsearchable handwritten record images, launched March 2024. English, Spanish, and Portuguese live; Chinese, French, German, Dutch, and Italian announced for 2026. The single largest "stuff that exists but couldn't be found" unlock in genealogy ever.

**Sources:** [[raw/investigation-osint-genealogy/genealogy-foreign-archives.md]], [[raw/investigation-osint-genealogy/00-overview.md]]

## What it does

Indexes scanned handwritten record images using HTR. Records that previously required film-by-film hand-skimming (county probate, parish registers, immigrant arrival manifests, land deeds) are now searchable by name, place, or substring across the entire collection.

## Access and cost

Free with a FamilySearch account. No paid tier. Operated by the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints; account creation does not require LDS membership.

## Distinctive trait

Scale plus zero cost. Ancestry, MyHeritage, and FindMyPast charge $20-50/mo and have smaller indexed handwritten coverage. The Spanish and Portuguese coverage rank fourth and fifth by volume, making this the strongest tool for Latin American genealogy by a wide margin.

## Limits

- Pre-1700 paleographic hands (court hand, secretary hand) sometimes index poorly; OCR confidence varies.
- Index lag: newly digitized collections take months to enter the searchable corpus.
- Some collections gated by location (US-only access, in-LDS-FHC access) per record-holder agreements.
- 2026 language additions are announcements; verify availability before relying on them.
