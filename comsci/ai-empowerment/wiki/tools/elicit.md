# Elicit

> **Summary:** Research assistant grounded in 138M peer-reviewed papers with sentence-level citations from a real index (no fabricated DOIs). The credible option for evidence-grade literature reviews. Use whenever the answer should rest on papers, not blogs.

**Sources:** [[raw/autonomous-research/07-academic-tools.md]]

## What it does

Search 138M papers; ask questions; get answers grounded in real cited papers with sentence-level provenance. Extract data tables across multiple papers; build literature-review matrices.

## Access and cost

Free tier with limits. Plus $12/mo. Pro $49/mo. Higher tiers above.

## Distinctive trait

Citations come from a real curated index, not from open-web search; DOIs and authors are real, not hallucinated. This is the essential property for any literature-grounded decision (medical, scientific, technical).

## Limits

- Coverage is peer-reviewed papers; for current events, news, blogs, use [Perplexity](perplexity.md) instead.
- Free tier is limited; paid tiers are the realistic individual entry point.
- Less polished single-report output than OpenAI / Gemini / Perplexity Deep Research; more of a research-workflow tool than a one-shot report generator.
