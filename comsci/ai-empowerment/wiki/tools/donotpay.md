# DoNotPay

> **Summary:** Self-described "world's first robot lawyer." Listed as a cautionary tale in this wiki: FTC settled in February 2025 for $193k with restrictions on "AI lawyer" marketing, after promising legal capabilities the product did not deliver. The category-defining example of "AI for autonomous high-stakes legal action is not yet safe."

**Sources:** [[raw/personal-pro-services/legal/donotpay-and-consumer-legal.md]]

## What it does

Originally a parking-ticket dispute generator (which worked well). Expanded into broader consumer legal tasks (subscription cancellations, refund letters, demand letters) before being marketed as a general "AI lawyer" capable of replacing licensed counsel. The marketing claims, not the original tool, drew regulatory action.

## Why it is in this wiki

The DoNotPay FTC settlement (Feb 2025) sets the marketing-claims floor for the entire consumer-legal-AI category. Before this case, "AI lawyer" was acceptable marketing; after, it carries clear regulatory risk in the US.

## What to use instead for first-pass legal review

- ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini for first-pass contract reading and lease decoding.
- A human attorney for any irreversible legal action (signing, suing, settling).

For consumer-grade, narrowly-scoped tasks (parking disputes, subscription cancellation), the DoNotPay legacy product still has value; the broader "AI lawyer" framing does not.
