# Beyond Neural Activity Prediction: Probing Latent Representations in Mouse V1 Digital Twins

**Authors:** Adriano Lima, Yuchen Hou, Michael Beyeler, Marius Schneider
**Venue:** arXiv:2605.23122 (q-bio.NC)
**Date:** 2026-05-22 (v1); 2026-05-26 (v2)
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23122
**Access:** preprint

## Abstract (summary)

Digital twins of sensory cortex predict neural activity from naturalistic videos
in freely moving mice. Beyond held-out prediction accuracy, the authors probe
the internal latent representations of models that share identical training data
but differ in visual-encoder architecture. Representations are characterized at
three levels: linear decodability via visual probes (orientation, contrast,
motion); latent-unit tuning to canonical features; and population geometry of
hidden-layer activity. Key finding: better neural-response prediction correlates
with stronger probe accuracy, and highly predictive models display flatter
hidden-population eigenspectra (higher-dimensional representations) that match
mouse V1 signatures.

## Key results

- Three-level representational probing: linear decodability, unit-level tuning,
  population geometry.
- Models with near-identical prediction accuracy diverge sharply in internal
  representations.
- Stronger predictors show flatter eigenspectra (higher-dimensional coding)
  closer to biological V1.
- Trained on naturalistic videos from freely moving mice; complements
  MICrONS-based digital twins.

## Relevance

First critical evaluation showing cortical digital twins with equal accuracy can
encode very different representations. A representational-quality lens for the
connectome/foundation-model line.
